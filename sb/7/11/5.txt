====== SB 7.11.5 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-nārada uvāca
> natvā bhagavate 'jāya
> lokānāṁ dharma-setave
> vakṣye sanātanaṁ dharmaṁ
> nārāyaṇa-mukhāc chrutam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:n:nāradaḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---Śrī Nārada Muni said; [[synonyms:n:natvā]]---offering my obeisances; [[synonyms:b:bhagavate]]---unto the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:a:ajāya]]---ever existing, never born; [[synonyms:l:lokānām]]---throughout the entire universe; [[synonyms:d:dharma]]-[[synonyms:s:setave]]---who protects religious principles; [[synonyms:v:vakṣye]]---I shall explain; [[synonyms:s:sanātanam]]---eternal; [[synonyms:d:dharmam]]---occupational duty; [[synonyms:n:nārāyaṇa]]-[[synonyms:m:mukhāt]]---from the mouth of Nārāyaṇa; [[synonyms:ś:śrutam]]---which I have heard.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Nārada Muni said: After first offering my obeisances unto Lord Kṛṣṇa, the protector of the religious principles of all living entities, let me explain the principles of the eternal religious system, of which I have heard from the mouth of Nārāyaṇa.**

===== Purport =====

The word //aja// refers to Kṛṣṇa, who explains in //Bhagavad-gītā// [[books:bg:4:6|(Bg. 4.6)]], //ajo 'pi sann avyayātmā:// "I am ever existing, and thus I never take birth. There is no change in My existence."
