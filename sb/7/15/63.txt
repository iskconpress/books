====== SB 7.15.63 ======

===== Text =====

> kārya-kāraṇa-vastv-aikya-
> darśanaṁ paṭa-tantuvat
> avastutvād vikalpasya
> bhāvādvaitaṁ tad ucyate

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kārya]]---the result or effect; [[synonyms:k:kāraṇa]]---the cause; [[synonyms:v:vastu]]---substance; [[synonyms:a:aikya]]---oneness; [[synonyms:d:darśanam]]---observation; [[synonyms:p:paṭa]]---the cloth; [[synonyms:t:tantu]]---the thread; [[synonyms:v:vat]]---like; [[synonyms:a:avastutvāt]]---because of being ultimately unreality; [[synonyms:v:vikalpasya]]---of differentiation; [[synonyms:b:bhāva]]-[[synonyms:a:advaitam]]---the conception of oneness; [[synonyms:t:tat]] [[synonyms:u:ucyate]]---that is called.

===== Translation =====

**When one understands that result and cause are one and that duality is ultimately unreal, like the idea that the threads of a cloth are different from the cloth itself, one reaches the conception of oneness called bhāvādvaita.**
