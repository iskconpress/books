====== SB 7.10.21 ======

===== Text =====

> bhavanti puruṣā loke
> mad-bhaktās tvām anuvratāḥ
> bhavān me khalu bhaktānāṁ
> sarveṣāṁ pratirūpa-dhṛk

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:bhavanti]]---become; [[synonyms:p:puruṣāḥ]]---persons; [[synonyms:l:loke]]---in this world; [[synonyms:m:mat]]-[[synonyms:b:bhaktāḥ]]---My pure devotees; [[synonyms:t:tvām]]---you; [[synonyms:a:anuvratāḥ]]---following in your footsteps; [[synonyms:b:bhavān]]---you; [[synonyms:m:me]]---My; [[synonyms:k:khalu]]---indeed; [[synonyms:b:bhaktānām]]---of all devotees; [[synonyms:s:sarveṣām]]---in different mellows; [[synonyms:p:pratirūpa]]-[[synonyms:d:dhṛk]]---tangible example.

===== Translation =====

**Those who follow your example will naturally become My pure devotees. You are the best example of My devotee, and others should follow in your footsteps.**

===== Purport =====

In this connection, Śrīla Madhvācārya quotes a verse from the //Skanda Purāṇa://

> ṛte tu tāttvikān devān
> nāradādīṁs tathaiva ca
> prahrādād uttamaḥ ko nu
> viṣṇu-bhaktau jagat-traye

There are many, many devotees of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, and they have been enumerated in //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// [[books:sb:6:3:20-21|(6.3.20)]] as follows:

> svayambhūr nāradaḥ śambhuḥ
> kumāraḥ kapilo manuḥ
> prahlādo janako bhīṣmo
> balir vaiyāsakir vayam

Of the twelve authorized devotees---Lord Brahmā, Nārada, Lord Śiva, Kapila, Manu and so on---Prahlāda Mahārāja is understood to be the best example.
