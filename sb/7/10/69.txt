====== SB 7.10.69 ======

===== Text =====

> evaṁ dagdhvā puras tisro
> bhagavān pura-hā nṛpa
> brahmādibhiḥ stūyamānaḥ
> svaṁ dhāma pratyapadyata

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:evam]]---thus; [[synonyms:d:dagdhvā]]---burning to ashes; [[synonyms:p:puraḥ]] [[synonyms:t:tisraḥ]]---the three residences of the demons; [[synonyms:b:bhagavān]]---the supreme powerful; [[synonyms:p:pura]]-[[synonyms:h:hā]]---who annihilated the residences of the //asuras//; [[synonyms:n:nṛpa]]---O King Yudhiṣṭhira; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādibhiḥ]]---by Lord Brahmā and other demigods; [[synonyms:s:stūyamānaḥ]]---being worshiped; [[synonyms:s:svam]]---to his own; [[synonyms:d:dhāma]]---abode; [[synonyms:p:pratyapadyata]]---returned.

===== Translation =====

**O King Yudhiṣṭhira, thus Lord Śiva is known as Tripurāri, the annihilator of the three dwellings of the demons, because he burnt these dwellings to ashes. Being worshiped by the demigods, headed by Lord Brahmā, Lord Śiva returned to his own abode.**
