====== SB 7.10.26 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-brahmovāca
> deva-devākhilādhyakṣa
> bhūta-bhāvana pūrvaja
> diṣṭyā te nihataḥ pāpo
> loka-santāpano 'suraḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:b:brahmā]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---Lord Brahmā said; [[synonyms:d:deva]]-[[synonyms:d:deva]]---O my Lord, Lord of all the demigods; [[synonyms:a:akhila]]-[[synonyms:a:adhyakṣa]]---owner of the whole universe; [[synonyms:b:bhūta]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāvana]]---O cause of all living entities; [[synonyms:p:pūrva]]-[[synonyms:j:ja]]---O original Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:d:diṣṭyā]]---by Your example or because of our good fortune; [[synonyms:t:te]]---by You; [[synonyms:n:nihataḥ]]---killed; [[synonyms:p:pāpaḥ]]---most sinful; [[synonyms:l:loka]]-[[synonyms:s:santāpanaḥ]]---giving trouble to the entire universe; [[synonyms:a:asuraḥ]]---the demon Hiraṇyakaśipu.

===== Translation =====

**Lord Brahmā said: O Supreme Lord of all lords, proprietor of the entire universe, O benedictor of all living entities, O original person [Cc. Ādi-puruṣa], because of our good fortune You have now killed this sinful demon, who was giving trouble to the entire universe.**

===== Purport =====

The word //pūrvaja// is described in //Bhagavad-gītā// [[books:bg:10:8|(Bg. 10.8)]]: //ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate.// All the demigods, including Lord Brahmā, are manifested from the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Therefore the original person, the cause of all causes, is Govinda, the //ādi-puruṣam.//
