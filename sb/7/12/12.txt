====== SB 7.12.12 ======

===== Text =====

> añjanābhyañjanonmarda-
> stry-avalekhāmiṣaṁ madhu
> srag-gandha-lepālaṅkārāṁs
> tyajeyur ye bṛhad-vratāḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:añjana]]---ointment or powder for decorating the eyes; [[synonyms:a:abhyañjana]]---massaging the head; [[synonyms:u:unmarda]]---massaging the body; [[synonyms:s:strī]]-[[synonyms:a:avalekha]]---to glance over a woman or to paint a woman's picture; [[synonyms:ā:āmiṣam]]---meat-eating; [[synonyms:m:madhu]]---drinking liquor or honey; [[synonyms:s:srak]]---decorating the body with garlands of flowers; [[synonyms:g:gandha]]-[[synonyms:l:lepa]]---smearing the body with scented ointment; [[synonyms:a:alaṅkārān]]---decorating the body with ornaments; [[synonyms:t:tyajeyuḥ]]---must give up; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---those who; [[synonyms:b:bṛhat]]-[[synonyms:v:vratāḥ]]---have taken the vow of celibacy.

===== Translation =====

**Brahmacārīs or gṛhasthas who have taken the vow of celibacy as described above should not indulge in the following: applying powder or ointment to the eyes, massaging the head with oil, massaging the body with the hands, seeing a woman or painting a woman's picture, eating meat, drinking wine, decorating the body with flower garlands, smearing scented ointment on the body, or decorating the body with ornaments. These they should give up.**
