====== SB 1.4.15 ======

> स कदाचित्सरस्वत्या उपस्पृश्य जलं शुचिः ।
> विविक्त एक आसीन उदिते रविमण्डले ॥१५॥

===== Text =====

> sa kadācit sarasvatyā
> upaspṛśya jalaṁ śuciḥ
> vivikta eka āsīna
> udite ravi-maṇḍale

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---he; [[synonyms:k:kadācit]]---once; [[synonyms:s:sarasvatyāḥ]]---on the bank of the Sarasvatī; [[synonyms:u:upaspṛśya]]---after finishing morning ablutions; [[synonyms:j:jalam]]---water; [[synonyms:ś:śuciḥ]]---being purified; [[synonyms:v:vivikte]]---concentration; [[synonyms:e:ekaḥ]]---alone; [[synonyms:ā:āsīnaḥ]]---being thus seated; [[synonyms:u:udite]]---on the rise; [[synonyms:r:ravi]]-[[synonyms:m:maṇḍale]]---of the sun disc.

===== Translation =====

**Once upon a time he [Vyāsadeva], as the sun rose, took his morning ablution in the waters of the Sarasvatī and sat alone to concentrate.**

===== Purport =====

The River Sarasvatī is flowing in the Badarikāśrama area of the Himalayas. So the place indicated here is Śamyāprāsa in Badarikāśrama, where Śrī Vyāsadeva is residing.
