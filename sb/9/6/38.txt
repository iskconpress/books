====== SB 9.6.38 ======

===== Text =====

> śaśabindor duhitari
> bindumatyām adhān nṛpaḥ
> purukutsam ambarīṣaṁ
> mucukundaṁ ca yoginam
> teṣāṁ svasāraḥ pañcāśat
> saubhariṁ vavrire patim

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śaśabindoḥ]]---of a king known as Śaśabindu; [[synonyms:d:duhitari]]---unto the daughter; [[synonyms:b:bindumatyām]]---whose name was Bindumatī; [[synonyms:a:adhāt]]---begot; [[synonyms:n:nṛpaḥ]]---the King (Māndhātā); [[synonyms:p:purukutsam]]---Purukutsa; [[synonyms:a:ambarīṣam]]---Ambarīṣa; [[synonyms:m:mucukundam]]---Mucukunda; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:y:yoginam]]---a highly elevated mystic; [[synonyms:t:teṣām]]---of them; [[synonyms:s:svasāraḥ]]---the sisters; [[synonyms:p:pañcāśat]]---fifty; [[synonyms:s:saubharim]]---unto the great sage Saubhari; [[synonyms:v:vavrire]]---accepted; [[synonyms:p:patim]]---as husband.

===== Translation =====

**Māndhātā begot three sons in the womb of Bindumatī, the daughter of Śaśabindu. These sons were Purukutsa, Ambarīṣa, and Mucukunda, a great mystic yogī. These three brothers had fifty sisters, who all accepted the great sage Saubhari as their husband.**
