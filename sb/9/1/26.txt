====== SB 9.1.26 ======

===== Text =====

> tasmin praviṣṭa evāsau
> sudyumnaḥ para-vīra-hā
> apaśyat striyam ātmānam
> aśvaṁ ca vaḍavāṁ nṛpa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tasmin]]---in that forest; [[synonyms:p:praviṣṭaḥ]]---having entered; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---indeed; [[synonyms:a:asau]]---he; [[synonyms:s:sudyumnaḥ]]---Prince Sudyumna; [[synonyms:p:para]]-[[synonyms:v:vīra]]-[[synonyms:h:hā]]---who could very well subdue his enemies; [[synonyms:a:apaśyat]]---observed; [[synonyms:s:striyam]]---female; [[synonyms:ā:ātmānam]]---himself; [[synonyms:a:aśvam]] [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and his horse; [[synonyms:v:vaḍavām]]---a mare; [[synonyms:n:nṛpa]]---O King Parīkṣit.

===== Translation =====

**O King Parīkṣit, as soon as Sudyumna, who was expert in subduing enemies, entered the forest, he saw himself transformed into a female and his horse transformed into a mare.**
