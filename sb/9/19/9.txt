====== SB 9.19.9 ======

===== Text =====

> so 'pi cānugataḥ straiṇaḥ
> kṛpaṇas tāṁ prasāditum
> kurvann iḍaviḍā-kāraṁ
> nāśaknot pathi sandhitum

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---that he-goat; [[synonyms:a:api]]---also; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:a:anugataḥ]]---following the she-goat; [[synonyms:s:straiṇaḥ]]---hen pecked; [[synonyms:k:kṛpaṇaḥ]]---very poor; [[synonyms:t:tām]]---her; [[synonyms:p:prasāditum]]---to satisfy; [[synonyms:k:kurvan]]---making; [[synonyms:i:iḍaviḍā]]-[[synonyms:k:kāram]]---an utterance in the language of the goats; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:a:aśaknot]]---was able; [[synonyms:p:pathi]]---on the road; [[synonyms:s:sandhitum]]---to satisfy.

===== Translation =====

**Being very sorry, the he-goat, who was subservient to his wife, followed the she-goat on the road and tried his best to flatter her, but he could not pacify her.**
