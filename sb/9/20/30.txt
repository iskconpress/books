====== SB 9.20.30 ======

===== Text =====

> kirāta-hūṇān yavanān
> pauṇḍrān kaṅkān khaśāñ chakān
> abrahmaṇya-nṛpāṁś cāhan
> mlecchān dig-vijaye 'khilān

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kirāta]]---the black people called Kirātas (mostly the Africans); [[synonyms:h:hūṇān]]---the Huns, the tribes from the far north; [[synonyms:y:yavanān]]---the meat-eaters; [[synonyms:p:pauṇḍrān]]---the Pauṇḍras; [[synonyms:k:kaṅkān]]---the Kaṅkas; [[synonyms:k:khaśān]]---the Mongolians; [[synonyms:ś:śakān]]---the Śakas; [[synonyms:a:abrahmaṇya]]---against the brahminical culture; [[synonyms:n:nṛpān]]---kings; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:a:ahan]]---he killed; [[synonyms:m:mlecchān]]---such atheists, who had no respect for Vedic civilization; [[synonyms:d:dik]]-[[synonyms:v:vijaye]]---while conquering all directions; [[synonyms:a:akhilān]]---all of them.

===== Translation =====

**When Mahārāja Bharata was on tour, he defeated or killed all the Kirātas, Hūṇas, Yavanas, Pauṇḍras, Kaṅkas, Khaśas, Śakas and the kings who were opposed to the Vedic principles of brahminical culture.**
