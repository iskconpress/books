====== SB 9.4.52 ======

===== Text =====

> alabdha-nāthaḥ sa sadā kutaścit
> santrasta-citto 'raṇam eṣamāṇaḥ
> devaṁ viriñcaṁ samagād vidhātas
> trāhy ātma-yone 'jita-tejaso mām

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:alabdha]]-[[synonyms:n:nāthaḥ]]---without getting the shelter of a protector; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---Durvāsā Muni; [[synonyms:s:sadā]]---always; [[synonyms:k:kutaścit]]---somewhere; [[synonyms:s:santrasta]]-[[synonyms:c:cittaḥ]]---with a fearful heart; [[synonyms:a:araṇam]]---a person who can give shelter; [[synonyms:e:eṣamāṇaḥ]]---seeking; [[synonyms:d:devam]]---at last to the chief demigod; [[synonyms:v:viriñcam]]---Lord Brahmā; [[synonyms:s:samagāt]]---approached; [[synonyms:v:vidhātaḥ]]---O my lord; [[synonyms:t:trāhi]]---kindly protect; [[synonyms:ā:ātma]]-[[synonyms:y:yone]]---O Lord Brahmā; [[synonyms:a:ajita]]-[[synonyms:t:tejasaḥ]]---from the fire released by Ajita, the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:m:mām]]---unto me.

===== Translation =====

**With a fearful heart, Durvāsā Muni went here and there seeking shelter, but when he could find no shelter, he finally approached Lord Brahmā and said, "O my lord, O Lord Brahmā, kindly protect me from the blazing Sudarśana cakra sent by the Supreme Personality of Godhead."**
