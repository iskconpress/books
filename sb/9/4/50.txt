====== SB 9.4.50 ======

===== Text =====

> tam anvadhāvad bhagavad-rathāṅgaṁ
> dāvāgnir uddhūta-śikho yathāhim
> tathānuṣaktaṁ munir īkṣamāṇo
> guhāṁ vivikṣuḥ prasasāra meroḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tam]]---unto Durvāsā; [[synonyms:a:anvadhāvat]]---began to follow; [[synonyms:b:bhagavat]]-[[synonyms:r:ratha]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅgam]]---the disc appearing from the wheel of the Lord's chariot; [[synonyms:d:dāva]]-[[synonyms:a:agniḥ]]---like a forest fire; [[synonyms:u:uddhūta]]---blazing high; [[synonyms:ś:śikhaḥ]]---having flames; [[synonyms:y:yathā]] [[synonyms:a:ahim]]---as it follows a snake; [[synonyms:t:tathā]]---in the same way; [[synonyms:a:anuṣaktam]]---as if touching Durvāsā Muni's back; [[synonyms:m:muniḥ]]---the sage; [[synonyms:ī:īkṣamāṇaḥ]]---seeing like that; [[synonyms:g:guhām]]---a cave; [[synonyms:v:vivikṣuḥ]]---wanted to enter; [[synonyms:p:prasasāra]]---began to move quickly; [[synonyms:m:meroḥ]]---of Meru Mountain.

===== Translation =====

**As the blazing flames of a forest fire pursue a snake, the disc of the Supreme Personality of Godhead began following Durvāsā Muni. Durvāsā Muni saw that the disc was almost touching his back, and thus he ran very swiftly, desiring to enter a cave of Sumeru Mountain.**
