====== SB 9.14.30 ======

===== Text =====

> iti vāk-sāyakair biddhaḥ
> pratottrair iva kuñjaraḥ
> niśi nistriṁśam ādāya
> vivastro 'bhyadravad ruṣā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:v:vāk]]-[[synonyms:s:sāyakaiḥ]]---by the arrows of strong words; [[synonyms:b:biddhaḥ]]---being pierced; [[synonyms:p:pratottraiḥ]]---by the goads; [[synonyms:i:iva]]---like; [[synonyms:k:kuñjaraḥ]]---an elephant; [[synonyms:n:niśi]]---in the night; [[synonyms:n:nistriṁśam]]---a sword; [[synonyms:ā:ādāya]]---taking in hand; [[synonyms:v:vivastraḥ]]---naked; [[synonyms:a:abhyadravat]]---went out; [[synonyms:r:ruṣā]]---in anger.

===== Translation =====

**Purūravā, stricken by the sharp words of Urvaśī like an elephant struck by its driver's pointed rod, became very angry. Not even dressing himself properly, he took a sword in hand and went out naked into the night to follow the Gandharvas who had stolen the lambs.**
