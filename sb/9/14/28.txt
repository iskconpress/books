====== SB 9.14.28 ======

===== Text =====

> niśamyākranditaṁ devī
> putrayor nīyamānayoḥ
> hatāsmy ahaṁ kunāthena
> napuṁsā vīra-māninā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:niśamya]]---by hearing; [[synonyms:ā:ākranditam]]---crying (because of being stolen); [[synonyms:d:devī]]---Urvaśī; [[synonyms:p:putrayoḥ]]---of those two lambs, which she treated as sons; [[synonyms:n:nīyamānayoḥ]]---as they were being taken away; [[synonyms:h:hatā]]---killed; [[synonyms:a:asmi]]---am; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:k:ku]]-[[synonyms:n:nāthena]]---under the protection of a bad husband; [[synonyms:n:na]]-[[synonyms:p:puṁsā]]---by the eunuch; [[synonyms:v:vīra]]-[[synonyms:m:māninā]]---although considering himself a hero.

===== Translation =====

**Urvaśī treated the two lambs like her own sons. Therefore, when they were being taken by the Gandharvas and began crying, Urvaśī heard them and rebuked her husband. "Now I am being killed," she said, "under the protection of an unworthy husband, who is a coward and a eunuch although he thinks himself a great hero.**
