====== SB 9.14.4 ======

===== Text =====

> so 'yajad rājasūyena
> vijitya bhuvana-trayam
> patnīṁ bṛhaspater darpāt
> tārāṁ nāmāharad balāt

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---he, Soma; [[synonyms:a:ayajat]]---performed; [[synonyms:r:rājasūyena]]---the sacrifice known as Rājasūya; [[synonyms:v:vijitya]]---after conquering; [[synonyms:b:bhuvana]]-[[synonyms:t:trayam]]---the three worlds (Svarga, Martya and Pātāla); [[synonyms:p:patnīm]]---the wife; [[synonyms:b:bṛhaspateḥ]]---of Bṛhaspati, the spiritual master of the demigods; [[synonyms:d:darpāt]]---out of pride; [[synonyms:t:tārām]]---Tārā; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---by name; [[synonyms:a:aharat]]---took away; [[synonyms:b:balāt]]---by force.

===== Translation =====

**After conquering the three worlds [the upper, middle and lower planetary systems], Soma, the moon-god, performed a great sacrifice known as the Rājasūya-yajña. Because he was very much puffed up, he forcibly kidnapped Bṛhaspati's wife, whose name was Tārā.**
