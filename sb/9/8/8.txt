====== SB 9.8.8 ======

===== Text =====

> sumatyās tanayā dṛptāḥ
> pitur ādeśa-kāriṇaḥ
> hayam anveṣamāṇās te
> samantān nyakhanan mahīm

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sumatyāḥ]] [[synonyms:t:tanayāḥ]]---the sons born of Queen Sumati; [[synonyms:d:dṛptāḥ]]---very proud of their prowess and influence; [[synonyms:p:pituḥ]]---of their father (Mahārāja Sagara); [[synonyms:ā:ādeśa]]-[[synonyms:k:kāriṇaḥ]]---following the order; [[synonyms:h:hayam]]---the horse (stolen by Indra); [[synonyms:a:anveṣamāṇāḥ]]---while seeking; [[synonyms:t:te]]---all of them; [[synonyms:s:samantāt]]---everywhere; [[synonyms:n:nyakhanan]]---dug; [[synonyms:m:mahīm]]---the earth.

===== Translation =====

**[King Sagara had two wives, Sumati and Keśinī.] The sons of Sumati, who were very proud of their prowess and influence, following the order of their father, searched for the lost horse. While doing so, they dug into the earth very extensively.**
