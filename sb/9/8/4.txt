====== SB 9.8.4 ======

===== Text =====

> ājñāyāsyai sapatnībhir
> garo datto 'ndhasā saha
> saha tenaiva sañjātaḥ
> sagarākhyo mahā-yaśāḥ
> sagaraś cakravarty āsīt
> sāgaro yat-sutaiḥ kṛtaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:ājñāya]]---knowing (this); [[synonyms:a:asyai]]---unto that pregnant queen; [[synonyms:s:sapatnībhiḥ]]---by the co-wives of the wife of Bāhuka; [[synonyms:g:garaḥ]]---poison; [[synonyms:d:dattaḥ]]---was given; [[synonyms:a:andhasā]] [[synonyms:s:saha]]---with her food; [[synonyms:s:saha]] [[synonyms:t:tena]]---with that poison; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---also; [[synonyms:s:sañjātaḥ]]---was born; [[synonyms:s:sagara]]-[[synonyms:ā:ākhyaḥ]]---by the name Sagara; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:y:yaśāḥ]]---having a great reputation; [[synonyms:s:sagaraḥ]]---King Sagara; [[synonyms:c:cakravartī]]---the emperor; [[synonyms:ā:āsīt]]---became; [[synonyms:s:sāgaraḥ]]---the place known as Gaṅgāsāgara; [[synonyms:y:yat]]-[[synonyms:s:sutaiḥ]]---by the sons of whom; [[synonyms:k:kṛtaḥ]]---was excavated.

===== Translation =====

**Knowing that she was pregnant, the co-wives of the wife of Bāhuka conspired to give her poison with her food, but it did not act. Instead, the son was born along with the poison. Therefore he became famous as Sagara ["one who is born with poison"]. Sagara later became the emperor. The place known as Gaṅgāsāgara was excavated by his sons.**
