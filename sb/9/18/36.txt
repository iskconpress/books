====== SB 9.18.36 ======

===== Text =====

> śukras tam āha kupitaḥ
> strī-kāmānṛta-pūruṣa
> tvāṁ jarā viśatāṁ manda
> virūpa-karaṇī nṛṇām

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śukraḥ]]---Śukrācārya; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---unto him (King Yayāti); [[synonyms:ā:āha]]---said; [[synonyms:k:kupitaḥ]]---being very angry at him; [[synonyms:s:strī]]-[[synonyms:k:kāma]]---O you who have lusty desires for women; [[synonyms:a:anṛta]]-[[synonyms:p:pūruṣa]]---O untruthful person; [[synonyms:t:tvām]]---unto you; [[synonyms:j:jarā]]---old age, invalidity; [[synonyms:v:viśatām]]---may enter; [[synonyms:m:manda]]---you fool; [[synonyms:v:virūpa]]-[[synonyms:k:karaṇī]]---which disfigures; [[synonyms:n:nṛṇām]]---the bodies of human beings.

===== Translation =====

**Śukrācārya was extremely angry. "You untruthful fool, lusting after women! You have done a great wrong," he said. "I therefore curse you to be attacked and disfigured by old age and invalidity."**
