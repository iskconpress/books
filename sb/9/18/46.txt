====== SB 9.18.46 ======

===== Text =====

> sapta-dvīpa-patiḥ saṁyak
> pitṛvat pālayan prajāḥ
> yathopajoṣaṁ viṣayāñ
> jujuṣe 'vyāhatendriyaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sapta]]-[[synonyms:d:dvīpa]]-[[synonyms:p:patiḥ]]---the master of the entire world, consisting of seven islands; [[synonyms:s:saṁyak]]---completely; [[synonyms:p:pitṛ]]-[[synonyms:v:vat]]---exactly like a father; [[synonyms:p:pālayan]]---ruling; [[synonyms:p:prajāḥ]]---the subjects; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]-[[synonyms:u:upajoṣam]]---as much as he wanted; [[synonyms:v:viṣayān]]---material happiness; [[synonyms:j:jujuṣe]]---enjoyed; [[synonyms:a:avyāhata]]---without being disturbed; [[synonyms:i:indriyaḥ]]---his senses.

===== Translation =====

**Thereafter, King Yayāti became the ruler of the entire world, consisting of seven islands, and ruled the citizens exactly like a father. Because he had taken the youth of his son, his senses were unimpaired, and he enjoyed as much material happiness as he desired.**
