====== SB 9.15.29 ======

===== Text =====

> tam āpatantaṁ bhṛgu-varyam ojasā
> dhanur-dharaṁ bāṇa-paraśvadhāyudham
> aiṇeya-carmāmbaram arka-dhāmabhir
> yutaṁ jaṭābhir dadṛśe purīṁ viśan

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tam]]---that Lord Paraśurāma; [[synonyms:ā:āpatantam]]---coming after him; [[synonyms:b:bhṛgu]]-[[synonyms:v:varyam]]---the best of the Bhṛgu dynasty, Lord Paraśurāma; [[synonyms:o:ojasā]]---very fiercely; [[synonyms:d:dhanuḥ]]-[[synonyms:d:dharam]]---carrying a bow; [[synonyms:b:bāṇa]]---arrows; [[synonyms:p:paraśvadha]]---chopper; [[synonyms:ā:āyudham]]---having all these weapons; [[synonyms:a:aiṇeya]]-[[synonyms:c:carma]]---blackish deerskin; [[synonyms:a:ambaram]]---the covering of his body; [[synonyms:a:arka]]-[[synonyms:d:dhāmabhiḥ]]---appearing like the sunshine; [[synonyms:y:yutam]] [[synonyms:j:jaṭābhiḥ]]---with locks of hair; [[synonyms:d:dadṛśe]]---he saw; [[synonyms:p:purīm]]---into the capital; [[synonyms:v:viśan]]---entering.

===== Translation =====

**As King Kārtavīryārjuna entered his capital, Māhiṣmatī Purī, he saw Lord Paraśurāma, the best of the Bhṛgu dynasty, coming after him, holding a chopper, shield, bow and arrows. Lord Paraśurāma was covered with a black deerskin, and his matted locks of hair appeared like the sunshine.**
