====== SB 9.3.36 ======

===== Text =====

> sutāṁ dattvānavadyāṅgīṁ
> balāya bala-śāline
> badary-ākhyaṁ gato rājā
> taptuṁ nārāyaṇāśramam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sutām]]---his daughter; [[synonyms:d:dattvā]]---after delivering; [[synonyms:a:anavadya]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅgīm]]---having a perfect body; [[synonyms:b:balāya]]---unto Lord Baladeva; [[synonyms:b:bala]]-[[synonyms:ś:śāline]]---unto the most powerful, the supreme powerful; [[synonyms:b:badarī]]-[[synonyms:ā:ākhyam]]---named Badarikāśrama; [[synonyms:g:gataḥ]]---he went; [[synonyms:r:rājā]]---the King; [[synonyms:t:taptum]]---to perform austerities; [[synonyms:n:nārāyaṇa]]-[[synonyms:ā:āśramam]]---to the place of Nara-Nārāyaṇa.

===== Translation =====

**Thereafter, the King gave his most beautiful daughter in charity to the supremely powerful Baladeva and then retired from worldly life and went to Badarikāśrama to please Nara-Nārāyaṇa.**


Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Ninth Canto, Third Chapter, of the //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam,// entitled "The Marriage of Sukanyā and Cyavana Muni."
