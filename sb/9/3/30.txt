====== SB 9.3.30 ======

===== Text =====

> āvartamāne gāndharve
> sthito 'labdha-kṣaṇaḥ kṣaṇam
> tad-anta ādyam ānamya
> svābhiprāyaṁ nyavedayat

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āvartamāne]]---because of being engaged; [[synonyms:g:gāndharve]]---in hearing songs from the Gandharvas; [[synonyms:s:sthitaḥ]]---situated; [[synonyms:a:alabdha]]-[[synonyms:k:kṣaṇaḥ]]---there was no time to talk; [[synonyms:k:kṣaṇam]]---even a moment; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:a:ante]]---when it ended; [[synonyms:ā:ādyam]]---unto the original teacher of the universe (Lord Brahmā); [[synonyms:ā:ānamya]]---after offering obeisances; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:a:abhiprāyam]]---his own desire; [[synonyms:n:nyavedayat]]---Kakudmī submitted.

===== Translation =====

**When Kakudmī arrived there, Lord Brahmā was engaged in hearing musical performances by the Gandharvas and had not a moment to talk with him. Therefore Kakudmī waited, and at the end of the musical performances he offered his obeisances to Lord Brahmā and thus submitted his long-standing desire.**
