====== SB 9.24.21-23 ======

===== Text =====

> tasyāhukaś cāhukī ca
> kanyā caivāhukātmajau
> devakaś cograsenaś ca
> catvāro devakātmajāḥ
>
> devavān upadevaś ca
> sudevo devavardhanaḥ
> teṣāṁ svasāraḥ saptāsan
> dhṛtadevādayo nṛpa
>
> śāntidevopadevā ca
> śrīdevā devarakṣitā
> sahadevā devakī ca
> vasudeva uvāha tāḥ

[[synonyms:t:tasya]]---from him (Punarvasu); [[synonyms:ā:āhukaḥ]]---Āhuka; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:ā:āhukī]]---Āhukī; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:k:kanyā]]---a daughter; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---indeed; [[synonyms:ā:āhuka]]---of Āhuka; [[synonyms:ā:ātmajau]]---two sons; [[synonyms:d:devakaḥ]]---Devaka; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:u:ugrasenaḥ]]---Ugrasena; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:c:catvāraḥ]]---four; [[synonyms:d:devaka]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmajāḥ]]---sons of Devaka; [[synonyms:d:devavān]]---Devavān; [[synonyms:u:upadevaḥ]]---Upadeva; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:s:sudevaḥ]]---Sudeva; [[synonyms:d:devavardhanaḥ]]---Devavardhana; [[synonyms:t:teṣām]]---of all of them; [[synonyms:s:svasāraḥ]]---sisters; [[synonyms:s:sapta]]---seven; [[synonyms:ā:āsan]]---existed; [[synonyms:d:dhṛtadevā]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādayaḥ]]---headed by Dhṛtadevā; [[synonyms:n:nṛpa]]---O King (Mahārāja Parīkṣit); [[synonyms:ś:śāntidevā]]---Śāntidevā; [[synonyms:u:upadevā]]---Upadevā; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:ś:śrīdevā]]---Śrīdevā; [[synonyms:d:devarakṣitā]]---Devarakṣitā; [[synonyms:s:sahadevā]]---Sahadevā; [[synonyms:d:devakī]]---Devakī; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:v:vasudevaḥ]]---Śrī Vasudeva, the father of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:u:uvāha]]---married; [[synonyms:t:tāḥ]]---them.

===== Translation =====

**Punarvasu had a son and a daughter, named Āhuka and Āhukī respectively, and Āhuka had two sons, named Devaka and Ugrasena. Devaka had four sons, named Devavān, Upadeva, Sudeva and Devavardhana, and he also had seven daughters, named Śāntidevā, Upadevā, Śrīdevā, Devarakṣitā, Sahadevā, Devakī and Dhṛtadevā. Dhṛtadevā was the eldest. Vasudeva, the father of Kṛṣṇa, married all these sisters.**
