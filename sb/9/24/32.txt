====== SB 9.24.32 ======

===== Text =====

> sāpa durvāsaso vidyāṁ
> deva-hūtīṁ pratoṣitāt
> tasyā vīrya-parīkṣārtham
> ājuhāva raviṁ śuciḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sā]]---she (Kuntī, or Pṛthā); [[synonyms:ā:āpa]]---achieved; [[synonyms:d:durvāsasaḥ]]---from the great sage Durvāsā; [[synonyms:v:vidyām]]---mystic power; [[synonyms:d:deva]]-[[synonyms:h:hūtīm]]---calling any demigod; [[synonyms:p:pratoṣitāt]]---who was satisfied; [[synonyms:t:tasyāḥ]]---with that (particular mystic power); [[synonyms:v:vīrya]]---potency; [[synonyms:p:parīkṣa]]-[[synonyms:a:artham]]---just to examine; [[synonyms:ā:ājuhāva]]---called for; [[synonyms:r:ravim]]---the sun-god; [[synonyms:ś:śuciḥ]]---the pious (Pṛthā).

===== Translation =====

**Once when Durvāsā was a guest at the house of Pṛthā's father, Kunti, Pṛthā satisfied Durvāsā by rendering service. Therefore she received a mystic power by which she could call any demigod. To examine the potency of this mystic power, the pious Kuntī immediately called for the sun-god.**
