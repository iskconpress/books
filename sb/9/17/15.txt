====== SB 9.17.15 ======

===== Text =====

> guruṇā hūyamāne 'gnau
> balabhit tanayān rajeḥ
> avadhīd bhraṁśitān mārgān
> na kaścid avaśeṣitaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:guruṇā]]---by the spiritual master (Bṛhaspati); [[synonyms:h:hūyamāne]] [[synonyms:a:agnau]]---while oblations were being offered in the fire of sacrifice; [[synonyms:b:balabhit]]---Indra; [[synonyms:t:tanayān]]---the sons; [[synonyms:r:rajeḥ]]---of Rajī; [[synonyms:a:avadhīt]]---killed; [[synonyms:b:bhraṁśitān]]---fallen; [[synonyms:m:mārgāt]]---from the moral principles; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:k:kaścit]]---anyone; [[synonyms:a:avaśeṣitaḥ]]---remained alive.

===== Translation =====

**Thereafter, Bṛhaspati, the spiritual master of the demigods, offered oblations in the fire so that the sons of Rajī would fall from moral principles. When they fell, Lord Indra killed them easily because of their degradation. Not a single one of them remained alive.**
