====== SB 5.17.7 ======

===== Text =====


> evaṁ mālyavac-chikharān niṣpatantī tato 'nuparata-vegā ketumālam abhi cakṣuḥ pratīcyāṁ diśi sarit-patiṁ praviśati.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:evam]]---in this way; [[synonyms:m:mālyavat]]-[[synonyms:ś:śikharāt]]---from the top of Mālyavān Mountain; [[synonyms:n:niṣpatantī]]---falling down; [[synonyms:t:tataḥ]]---thereafter; [[synonyms:a:anuparata]]-[[synonyms:v:vegā]]---whose force is uninterrupted; [[synonyms:k:ketumālam]] [[synonyms:a:abhi]]---into the land known as Ketumāla-varṣa; [[synonyms:c:cakṣuḥ]]---the branch known as Cakṣu; [[synonyms:p:pratīcyām]]---in the West; [[synonyms:d:diśi]]---direction; [[synonyms:s:sarit]]-[[synonyms:p:patim]]---the ocean; [[synonyms:p:praviśati]]---enters into.

===== Translation =====

**The branch of the Ganges known as Cakṣu falls onto the summit of Mālyavān Mountain and from there cascades onto the land of Ketumāla-varṣa. The Ganges flows incessantly through Ketumāla-varṣa and in this way also reaches the ocean of salt water in the West.**
