====== SB 5.17.5 ======

===== Text =====


> tatra caturdhā bhidyamānā caturbhir nāmabhiś catur-diśam abhispandantī nada-nadī-patim evābhiniviśati sītālakanandā cakṣur bhadreti.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tatra]]---there (on top of Mount Meru); [[synonyms:c:caturdhā]]---into four branches; [[synonyms:b:bhidyamānā]]---being divided; [[synonyms:c:caturbhiḥ]]---with four; [[synonyms:n:nāmabhiḥ]]---names; [[synonyms:c:catuḥ]]-[[synonyms:d:diśam]]---the four directions (east, west, north and south); [[synonyms:a:abhispandantī]]---profusely flowing; [[synonyms:n:nada]]-[[synonyms:n:nadī]]-[[synonyms:p:patim]]---the reservoir of all great rivers (the ocean); [[synonyms:e:eva]]---certainly; [[synonyms:a:abhiniviśati]]---enters; [[synonyms:s:sītā]]-[[synonyms:a:alakanandā]]---Sītā and Alakanandā; [[synonyms:c:cakṣuḥ]]---Cakṣu; [[synonyms:b:bhadrā]]---Bhadrā; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---known by these names.

===== Translation =====

**On top of Mount Meru, the Ganges divides into four branches, each of which gushes in a different direction [east, west, north and south]. These branches, known by the names Sītā, Alakanandā, Cakṣu and Bhadrā, flow down to the ocean.**
