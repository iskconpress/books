====== SB 5.8.20 ======

===== Text =====


> api svid akṛta-sukṛtam āgatya māṁ sukhayiṣyati hariṇa-rāja-kumāro vividha-rucira-darśanīya-nija-mṛga-dāraka-vinodair asantoṣaṁ svānām apanudan.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:api]] [[synonyms:s:svit]]---whether it will; [[synonyms:a:akṛta]]-[[synonyms:s:sukṛtam]]---who has never executed any pious activities; [[synonyms:ā:āgatya]]---coming back; [[synonyms:m:mām]]---to me; [[synonyms:s:sukhayiṣyati]]---give pleasure; [[synonyms:h:hariṇa]]-[[synonyms:r:rāja]]-[[synonyms:k:kumāraḥ]]---the deer, who was just like a prince because of my taking care of it exactly like a son; [[synonyms:v:vividha]]---various; [[synonyms:r:rucira]]---very pleasing; [[synonyms:d:darśanīya]]---to be seen; [[synonyms:n:nija]]---own; [[synonyms:m:mṛga]]-[[synonyms:d:dāraka]]---befitting the calf of the deer; [[synonyms:v:vinodaiḥ]]---by pleasing activities; [[synonyms:a:asantoṣam]]---the unhappiness; [[synonyms:s:svānām]]---of his own kind; [[synonyms:a:apanudan]]---driving away.

===== Translation =====

**That deer is exactly like a prince. When will it return? When will it again display its personal activities, which are so pleasing? When will it again pacify a wounded heart like mine? I certainly must have no pious assets, otherwise the deer would have returned by now.**

===== Purport =====

Out of strong affection, the King accepted the small deer as if it were a prince. This is called //moha.// Due to his anxiety over the deer's absence, the King addressed the animal as though it were his son. Out of affection, anyone can be addressed as anything.
