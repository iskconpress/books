====== SB 5.8.5 ======

===== Text =====


> tasyā utpatantyā antarvatnyā uru-bhayāvagalito yoni-nirgato garbhaḥ srotasi nipapāta.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tasyāḥ]]---of it; [[synonyms:u:utpatantyāḥ]]---forcefully jumping up; [[synonyms:a:antarvatnyāḥ]]---having a full womb; [[synonyms:u:uru]]-[[synonyms:b:bhaya]]---due to great fear; [[synonyms:a:avagalitaḥ]]---having slipped out; [[synonyms:y:yoni]]-[[synonyms:n:nirgataḥ]]---coming out of the womb; [[synonyms:g:garbhaḥ]]---the offspring; [[synonyms:s:srotasi]]---in the flowing water; [[synonyms:n:nipapāta]]---fell down.

===== Translation =====

**The doe was pregnant, and when it jumped out of fear, the baby deer fell from its womb into the flowing waters of the river.**

===== Purport =====

There is every chance of a woman's having a miscarriage if she experiences some ecstatic emotion or is frightened. Pregnant women should therefore be spared all these external influences.
