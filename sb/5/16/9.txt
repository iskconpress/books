====== SB 5.16.9 ======

===== Text =====


> evaṁ dakṣiṇenelāvṛtaṁ niṣadho hemakūṭo himālaya iti prāg-āyatā yathā nīlādayo 'yuta-yojanotsedhā hari-varṣa-kimpuruṣa-bhāratānāṁ yathā-saṅkhyam.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:evam]]---thus; [[synonyms:d:dakṣiṇena]]---by degrees to the southern side; [[synonyms:i:ilāvṛtam]]---of Ilāvṛta-varṣa; [[synonyms:n:niṣadhaḥ]] [[synonyms:h:hema]]-[[synonyms:k:kūṭaḥ]] [[synonyms:h:himālayaḥ]]---three mountains named Niṣadha, Hemakūṭa and Himālaya; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:p:prāk]]-[[synonyms:ā:āyatāḥ]]---extended to the east; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]---just as; [[synonyms:n:nīla]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādayaḥ]]---the mountains headed by Nīla; [[synonyms:a:ayuta]]-[[synonyms:y:yojana]]-[[synonyms:u:utsedhāḥ]]---ten thousand //yojanas// high; [[synonyms:h:hari]]-[[synonyms:v:varṣa]]---the division named Hari-varṣa; [[synonyms:k:kimpuruṣa]]---the division named Kimpuruṣa; [[synonyms:b:bhāratānām]]---the division named Bhārata-varṣa; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]-[[synonyms:s:saṅkhyam]]---according to number.

===== Translation =====

**Similarly, south of Ilāvṛta-varṣa and extending from east to west are three great mountains named (from north to south) Niṣadha, Hemakūṭa and Himālaya. Each of them is 10,000 yojanas [80,000 miles] high. They mark the boundaries of the three varṣas named Hari-varṣa, Kimpuruṣa-varṣa and Bhārata-varṣa [India].**
