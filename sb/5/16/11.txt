====== SB 5.16.11 ======

===== Text =====

> mandaro merumandaraḥ supārśvaḥ kumuda ity ayuta-yojana-vistāronnāhā meroś catur-diśam avaṣṭambha-giraya upakḷptāḥ.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mandaraḥ]]---the mountain named Mandara; [[synonyms:m:meru]]-[[synonyms:m:mandaraḥ]]---the mountain named Merumandara; [[synonyms:s:supārśvaḥ]]---the mountain named Supārśva; [[synonyms:k:kumudaḥ]]---the mountain named Kumuda; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:a:ayuta]]-[[synonyms:y:yojana]]-[[synonyms:v:vistāra]]-[[synonyms:u:unnāhāḥ]]---which measure ten thousand //yojanas// high and wide; [[synonyms:m:meroḥ]]---of Sumeru; [[synonyms:c:catuḥ]]-[[synonyms:d:diśam]]---the four sides; [[synonyms:a:avaṣṭambha]]-[[synonyms:g:girayaḥ]]---mountains that are like the belts of Sumeru; [[synonyms:u:upakḷptāḥ]]---situated.

===== Translation =====

**On the four sides of the great mountain known as Sumeru are four mountains-Mandara, Merumandara, Supārśva and Kumuda---which are like its belts. The length and height of these mountains are calculated to be 10,000 yojanas [80,000 miles].**
