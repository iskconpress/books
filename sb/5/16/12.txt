====== SB 5.16.12 ======

===== Text =====


> caturṣv eteṣu cūta-jambū-kadamba-nyagrodhāś catvāraḥ pādapa-pravarāḥ parvata-ketava ivādhi-sahasra-yojanonnāhās tāvad viṭapa-vitatayaḥ śata-yojana-pariṇāhāḥ.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:c:caturṣu]]---on the four; [[synonyms:e:eteṣu]]---on these mountains, beginning with Mandara; [[synonyms:c:cūta]]-[[synonyms:j:jambū]]-[[synonyms:k:kadamba]]---of trees such as the mango, rose apple and //kadamba//; [[synonyms:n:nyagrodhāḥ]]---and the banyan tree; [[synonyms:c:catvāraḥ]]---four kinds; [[synonyms:p:pādapa]]-[[synonyms:p:pravarāḥ]]---the best of trees; [[synonyms:p:parvata]]-[[synonyms:k:ketavaḥ]]---the flagstaffs on the mountains; [[synonyms:i:iva]]---like; [[synonyms:a:adhi]]---over; [[synonyms:s:sahasra]]-[[synonyms:y:yojana]]-[[synonyms:u:unnāhāḥ]]---one thousand //yojanas// high; [[synonyms:t:tāvat]]---so much also; [[synonyms:v:viṭapa]]-[[synonyms:v:vitatayaḥ]]---the length of the branches; [[synonyms:ś:śata]]-[[synonyms:y:yojana]]---one hundred //yojanas//; [[synonyms:p:pariṇāhāḥ]]---wide.

===== Translation =====

**Standing like flagstaffs on the summits of these four mountains are a mango tree, a rose apple tree, a kadamba tree and a banyan tree. Those trees are calculated to have a width of 100 yojanas [800 miles] and a height of 1,100 yojanas [8,800 miles]. Their branches also spread to a radius of 1,100 yojanas.**
