====== SB 5.16.23 ======

===== Text =====


> yā hy upayuñjānānāṁ mukha-nirvāsito vāyuḥ samantāc chata-yojanam anuvāsayati.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yāḥ]]---which (those flows of honey); [[synonyms:h:hi]]---indeed; [[synonyms:u:upayuñjānānām]]---of those who drink; [[synonyms:m:mukha]]-[[synonyms:n:nirvāsitaḥ]] [[synonyms:v:vāyuḥ]]---the air emanating from the mouths; [[synonyms:s:samantāt]]---all around; [[synonyms:ś:śata]]-[[synonyms:y:yojanam]]---up to one hundred //yojanas// (eight hundred miles); [[synonyms:a:anuvāsayati]]---makes sweetly flavored.

===== Translation =====

**The air carrying the scent from the mouths of those who drink that honey perfumes the land for a hundred yojanas around.**
