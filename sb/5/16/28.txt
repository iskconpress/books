====== SB 5.16.28 ======

===== Text =====


> meror mūrdhani bhagavata ātma-yoner madhyata upakḷptāṁ purīm ayuta-yojana-sāhasrīṁ sama-caturasrāṁ śātakaumbhīṁ vadanti.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:meroḥ]]---of Sumeru Mountain; [[synonyms:m:mūrdhani]]---on the head; [[synonyms:b:bhagavataḥ]]---of the most powerful being; [[synonyms:ā:ātma]]-[[synonyms:y:yoneḥ]]---of Lord Brahmā; [[synonyms:m:madhyataḥ]]---in the middle; [[synonyms:u:upakḷptām]]---situated; [[synonyms:p:purīm]]---the great township; [[synonyms:a:ayuta]]-[[synonyms:y:yojana]]---ten thousand //yojanas//; [[synonyms:s:sāhasrīm]]---one thousand; [[synonyms:s:sama]]-[[synonyms:c:caturasrām]]---of the same length on all sides; [[synonyms:ś:śāta]]-[[synonyms:k:kaumbhīm]]---made entirely of gold; [[synonyms:v:vadanti]]---the great learned sages say.

===== Translation =====

**In the middle of the summit of Meru is the township of Lord Brahmā. Each of its four sides is calculated to extend for ten million yojanas [eighty million miles]. It is made entirely of gold, and therefore learned scholars and sages call it Śātakaumbhī.**
