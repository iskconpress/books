====== SB 5.21.17 ======

===== Text =====


> tathā vālikhilyā ṛṣayo 'ṅguṣṭha-parva-mātrāḥ ṣaṣṭi-sahasrāṇi purataḥ sūryaṁ sūkta-vākāya niyuktāḥ saṁstuvanti.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tathā]]---there; [[synonyms:v:vālikhilyāḥ]]---Vālikhilyas; [[synonyms:ṛ:ṛṣayaḥ]]---great sages; [[synonyms:a:aṅguṣṭha]]-[[synonyms:p:parva]]-[[synonyms:m:mātrāḥ]]---whose size is that of a thumb; [[synonyms:ṣ:ṣaṣṭi]]-[[synonyms:s:sahasrāṇi]]---sixty thousand; [[synonyms:p:purataḥ]]---in front; [[synonyms:s:sūryam]]---the sun-god; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:u:ukta]]-[[synonyms:v:vākāya]]---for speaking eloquently; [[synonyms:n:niyuktāḥ]]---engaged; [[synonyms:s:saṁstuvanti]]---offer prayers.

===== Translation =====

**There are sixty thousand saintly persons named Vālikhilyas, each the size of a thumb, who are located in front of the sun-god and who offer him eloquent prayers of glorification.**
