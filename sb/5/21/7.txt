====== SB 5.21.7 ======

===== Text =====


> evaṁ nava koṭaya eka-pañcāśal-lakṣāṇi yojanānāṁ mānasottara-giri-parivartanasyopadiśanti tasminn aindrīṁ purīṁ pūrvasmān meror devadhānīṁ nāma dakṣiṇato yāmyāṁ saṁyamanīṁ nāma paścād vāruṇīṁ nimlocanīṁ nāma uttarataḥ saumyāṁ vibhāvarīṁ nāma tāsūdaya-madhyāhnāstamaya-niśīthānīti bhūtānāṁ pravṛtti-nivṛtti-nimittāni samaya-viśeṣeṇa meroś catur-diśam.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:evam]]---thus; [[synonyms:n:nava]]---nine; [[synonyms:k:koṭayaḥ]]---ten millions; [[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:p:pañcāśat]]---fifty-one; [[synonyms:l:lakṣāṇi]]---hundred thousands; [[synonyms:y:yojanānām]]---of the //yojanas//; [[synonyms:m:mānasottara]]-[[synonyms:g:giri]]---of the mountain known as Mānasottara; [[synonyms:p:parivartanasya]]---of the circumambulation; [[synonyms:u:upadiśanti]]---they (learned scholars) teach; [[synonyms:t:tasmin]]---on that (Mānasottara Mountain); [[synonyms:a:aindrīm]]---of King Indra; [[synonyms:p:purīm]]---the city; [[synonyms:p:pūrvasmāt]]---on the eastern side; [[synonyms:m:meroḥ]]---of Sumeru Mountain; [[synonyms:d:devadhānīm]]---Devadhānī; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---of the name; [[synonyms:d:dakṣiṇataḥ]]---on the southern side; [[synonyms:y:yāmyām]]---of Yamarāja; [[synonyms:s:saṁyamanīm]]---Saṁyamanī; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---named; [[synonyms:p:paścāt]]---on the western side; [[synonyms:v:vāruṇīm]]---of Varuṇa; [[synonyms:n:nimlocanīm]]---Nimlocanī; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---named; [[synonyms:u:uttarataḥ]]---on the northern side; [[synonyms:s:saumyām]]---of the moon; [[synonyms:v:vibhāvarīm]]---Vibhāvarī; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---named; [[synonyms:t:tāsu]]---in all of them; [[synonyms:u:udaya]]---rising; [[synonyms:m:madhyāhna]]---midday; [[synonyms:a:astamaya]]---sunset; [[synonyms:n:niśīthāni]]---midnight; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:b:bhūtānām]]---of the living entities; [[synonyms:p:pravṛtti]]---of activity; [[synonyms:n:nivṛtti]]---and cessation of activity; [[synonyms:n:nimittāni]]---the causes; [[synonyms:s:samaya]]-[[synonyms:v:viśeṣeṇa]]---by the particular times; [[synonyms:m:meroḥ]]---of Sumeru Mountain; [[synonyms:c:catuḥ]]-[[synonyms:d:diśam]]---the four sides.

===== Translation =====

**Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued; My dear King, as stated before, the learned say that the sun travels over all sides of Mānasottara Mountain in a circle whose length is 95,100,000 yojanas [760,800,000 miles]. On Mānasottara Mountain, due east of Mount Sumeru, is a place known as Devadhānī, possessed by King Indra. Similarly, in the south is a place known as Saṁyamanī, possessed by Yamarāja, in the west is a place known as Nimlocanī, possessed by Varuṇa, and in the north is a place named Vibhāvarī, possessed by the moon-god. Sunrise, midday, sunset and midnight occur in all those places according to specific times, thus engaging all living entities in their various occupational duties and also making them cease such duties.**
