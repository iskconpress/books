====== SB 5.9.16 ======

===== Text =====


> atha vṛṣala-rāja-paṇiḥ puruṣa-paśor asṛg-āsavena devīṁ bhadra-kālīṁ yakṣyamāṇas tad-abhimantritam asim ati-karāla-niśitam upādade.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:atha]]---thereafter; [[synonyms:v:vṛṣala]]-[[synonyms:r:rāja]]-[[synonyms:p:paṇiḥ]]---the so-called priest of the leader of the dacoits (one of the thieves); [[synonyms:p:puruṣa]]-[[synonyms:p:paśoḥ]]---of the animalistic man for being sacrificed (Bharata Mahārāja); [[synonyms:a:asṛk]]-[[synonyms:ā:āsavena]]---with the liquor of blood; [[synonyms:d:devīm]]---to the deity; [[synonyms:b:bhadra]]-[[synonyms:k:kālīm]]---the goddess Kālī; [[synonyms:y:yakṣyamāṇaḥ]]---desiring to offer; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:a:abhimantritam]]---consecrated by the //mantra// of Bhadra Kālī; [[synonyms:a:asim]]---the sword; [[synonyms:a:ati]]-[[synonyms:k:karāla]]---very fearful; [[synonyms:n:niśitam]]---finely sharpened; [[synonyms:u:upādade]]---he took up.

===== Translation =====

**At this time, one of the thieves, acting as the chief priest, was ready to offer the blood of Jaḍa Bharata, whom they imagined to be an animal-man, to the goddess Kālī to drink as a liquor. He therefore took up a very fearsome sword, which was very sharp and, consecrating it by the mantra of Bhadra Kālī, raised it to kill Jaḍa Bharata.**
