====== SB 5.25.15 ======

===== Text =====


> etāvatīr hi rājan puṁsaḥ pravṛtti-lakṣaṇasya dharmasya vipāka-gataya uccāvacā visadṛśā yathā-praśnaṁ vyācakhye kim anyat kathayāma iti.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:etāvatīḥ]]---of such a kind; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:r:rājan]]---O King; [[synonyms:p:puṁsaḥ]]---of the human being; [[synonyms:p:pravṛtti]]-[[synonyms:l:lakṣaṇasya]]---symptomized by inclinations; [[synonyms:d:dharmasya]]---of the execution of duties; [[synonyms:v:vipāka]]-[[synonyms:g:gatayaḥ]]---the resultant destinations; [[synonyms:u:ucca]]-[[synonyms:a:avacāḥ]]---high and low; [[synonyms:v:visadṛśāḥ]]---different; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]-[[synonyms:p:praśnam]]---as you inquired; [[synonyms:v:vyācakhye]]---I have described; [[synonyms:k:kim]] [[synonyms:a:anyat]]---what else; [[synonyms:k:kathayāma]]---shall I speak; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus.

===== Translation =====

**My dear King, I have thus described how people generally act according to their different desires and, as a result, get different types of bodies in higher or lower planets. You inquired of these things from me, and I have explained to you whatever I have heard from authorities. What shall I speak of now?**


Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Fifth Canto, Twenty-fifth Chapter of the //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam,// entitled "The Glories of Lord Ananta."
