====== SB 5.26.13 ======

===== Text =====


> yas tv iha vā ugraḥ paśūn pakṣiṇo vā prāṇata uparandhayati tam apakaruṇaṁ puruṣādair api vigarhitam amutra yamānucarāḥ kumbhīpāke tapta-taile uparandhayanti.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yaḥ]]---a person who; [[synonyms:t:tu]]---but; [[synonyms:i:iha]]---in this life; [[synonyms:v:vā]]---or; [[synonyms:u:ugraḥ]]---very cruel; [[synonyms:p:paśūn]]---animals; [[synonyms:p:pakṣiṇaḥ]]---birds; [[synonyms:v:vā]]---or; [[synonyms:p:prāṇataḥ]]---in a live condition; [[synonyms:u:uparandhayati]]---cooks; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---him; [[synonyms:a:apakaruṇam]]---very cruel-hearted; [[synonyms:p:puruṣa]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādaiḥ]]---by those who eat human flesh; [[synonyms:a:api]]---even; [[synonyms:v:vigarhitam]]---condemned; [[synonyms:a:amutra]]---in the next life; [[synonyms:y:yama]]-[[synonyms:a:anucarāḥ]]---the servants of Yamarāja; [[synonyms:k:kumbhīpāke]]---in the hell known as Kumbhīpāka; [[synonyms:t:tapta]]-[[synonyms:t:taile]]---in boiling oil; [[synonyms:u:uparandhayanti]]---cook.

===== Translation =====

**For the maintenance of their bodies and the satisfaction of their tongues, cruel persons cook poor animals and birds alive. Such persons are condemned even by man-eaters. In their next lives they are carried by the Yamadūtas to the hell known as Kumbhīpāka, where they are cooked in boiling oil.**
