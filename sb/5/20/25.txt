====== SB 5.20.25 ======

===== Text =====


> tasyāpi praiyavrata evādhipatir nāmnā medhātithiḥ so 'pi vibhajya sapta varṣāṇi putra-nāmāni teṣu svātmajān purojava-manojava-pavamāna-dhūmrānīka-citrarepha-bahurūpa-viśvadhāra-saṁjñān nidhāpyādhipatīn svayaṁ bhagavaty ananta ā-veśita-matis tapovanaṁ praviveśa.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tasya]] [[synonyms:a:api]]---of that island also; [[synonyms:p:praiyavrataḥ]]---a son of Mahārāja Priyavrata; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---certainly; [[synonyms:a:adhipatiḥ]]---the ruler; [[synonyms:n:nāmnā]]---by the name; [[synonyms:m:medhā]]-[[synonyms:t:tithiḥ]]---Medhātithi; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]] [[synonyms:a:api]]---he also; [[synonyms:v:vibhajya]]---dividing; [[synonyms:s:sapta]] [[synonyms:v:varṣāṇi]]---seven divisions of the island; [[synonyms:p:putra]]-[[synonyms:n:nāmāni]]---possessing the names of his sons; [[synonyms:t:teṣu]]---in them; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmajān]]---his own sons; [[synonyms:p:purojava]]---Purojava; [[synonyms:m:manojava]]---Manojava; [[synonyms:p:pavamāna]]---Pavamāna; [[synonyms:d:dhūmrānīka]]---Dhūmrānīka; [[synonyms:c:citra]]-[[synonyms:r:repha]]---Citrarepha; [[synonyms:b:bahu]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---Bahurūpa; [[synonyms:v:viśvadhāra]]---Viśvadhāra; [[synonyms:s:saṁjñān]]---having as names; [[synonyms:n:nidhāpya]]---establishing as; [[synonyms:a:adhipatīn]]---the rulers; [[synonyms:s:svayam]]---himself; [[synonyms:b:bhagavati]]---in the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:a:anante]]---in the unlimited; [[synonyms:ā:āveśita]]-[[synonyms:m:matiḥ]]---whose mind was fully absorbed; [[synonyms:t:tapaḥ]]-[[synonyms:v:vanam]]---in the forest where meditation is performed; [[synonyms:p:praviveśa]]---he entered.

===== Translation =====

**The master of this island, also one of the sons of Priyavrata, was known as Medhātithi. He also divided his island into seven sections, named according to the names of his own sons, whom he made the kings of that island. The names of those sons are Purojava, Manojava, Pavamāna, Dhūmrānīka, Citrarepha, Bahurūpa and Viśvadhāra. After dividing the island and situating his sons as its rulers, Medhātithi personally retired, and to fix his mind completely upon the lotus feet of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, he entered a forest suitable for meditation.**
