====== SB 5.20.27 ======

===== Text =====


> tad-varṣa-puruṣā ṛtavrata-satyavrata-dānavratānuvrata-nāmāno bhagavantaṁ vāyv-ātmakaṁ prāṇāyāma-vidhūta-rajas-tamasaḥ parama-samādhinā yajante.

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:v:varṣa]]-[[synonyms:p:puruṣāḥ]]---the inhabitants of those tracts of land; [[synonyms:ṛ:ṛta]]-[[synonyms:v:vrata]]---Ṛtavrata; [[synonyms:s:satya]]-[[synonyms:v:vrata]]---Satyavrata; [[synonyms:d:dāna]]-[[synonyms:v:vrata]]---Dānavrata; [[synonyms:a:anuvrata]]---Anuvrata; [[synonyms:n:nāmānaḥ]]---having the four names; [[synonyms:b:bhagavantam]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:v:vāyu]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmakam]]---represented by the demigod Vāyu; [[synonyms:p:prāṇāyāma]]---by the practice of regulating the airs within the body; [[synonyms:v:vidhūta]]---cleansed away; [[synonyms:r:rajaḥ]]-[[synonyms:t:tamasaḥ]]---whose passion and ignorance; [[synonyms:p:parama]]---sublime; [[synonyms:s:samādhinā]]---by trance; [[synonyms:y:yajante]]---they worship.

===== Translation =====

**The inhabitants of those islands are also divided into four castes---Ṛtavrata, Satyavrata, Dānavrata and Anuvrata---which exactly resemble brāhmaṇa, kṣatriya, vaiśya and śūdra. They practice prāṇāyāma and mystic yoga, and in trance they worship the Supreme Lord in the form of Vāyu.**
