====== SB 8.24.9 ======

===== Text =====

> jñātvā tad dānavendrasya
> hayagrīvasya ceṣṭitam
> dadhāra śapharī-rūpaṁ
> bhagavān harir īśvaraḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:j:jñātvā]]---after understanding; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---that; [[synonyms:d:dānava]]-[[synonyms:i:indrasya]]---of the great demon; [[synonyms:h:hayagrīvasya]]---of Hayagrīva; [[synonyms:c:ceṣṭitam]]---activity; [[synonyms:d:dadhāra]]---accepted; [[synonyms:ś:śapharī]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpam]]---the form of a fish; [[synonyms:b:bhagavān]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:h:hariḥ]]---the Lord; [[synonyms:ī:īśvaraḥ]]---the supreme controller.

===== Translation =====

**Understanding the acts of the great demon Hayagrīva, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Hari, who is full of all opulences, assumed the form of a fish and saved the Vedas by killing the demon.**

===== Purport =====

Because everything was inundated by water, to save the //Vedas// it was necessary for the Lord to assume the form of a fish.
