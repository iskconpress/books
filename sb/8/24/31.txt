====== SB 8.24.31 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-śuka uvāca
> iti bruvāṇaṁ nṛpatiṁ jagat-patiḥ
> satyavrataṁ matsya-vapur yuga-kṣaye
> vihartu-kāmaḥ pralayārṇave 'bravīc
> cikīrṣur ekānta-jana-priyaḥ priyam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:ś:śukaḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:b:bruvāṇam]]---speaking like that; [[synonyms:n:nṛpatim]]---unto the King; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:p:patiḥ]]---the master of the entire universe; [[synonyms:s:satyavratam]]---unto Satyavrata; [[synonyms:m:matsya]]-[[synonyms:v:vapuḥ]]---the Lord, who had assumed the form of a fish; [[synonyms:y:yuga]]-[[synonyms:k:kṣaye]]---at the end of a //yuga//; [[synonyms:v:vihartu]]-[[synonyms:k:kāmaḥ]]---to enjoy His own pastimes; [[synonyms:p:pralaya]]-[[synonyms:a:arṇave]]---in the water of inundation; [[synonyms:a:abravīt]]---said; [[synonyms:c:cikīrṣuḥ]]---desiring to do; [[synonyms:e:ekānta]]-[[synonyms:j:jana]]-[[synonyms:p:priyaḥ]]---most beloved by the devotees; [[synonyms:p:priyam]]---something very beneficial.

===== Translation =====

**Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: When King Satyavrata spoke in this way, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who at the end of the yuga had assumed the form of a fish to benefit His devotee and enjoy His pastimes in the water of inundation, responded as follows.**
