====== SB 8.18.11 ======

===== Text =====

> dṛṣṭvāditis taṁ nija-garbha-sambhavaṁ
> paraṁ pumāṁsaṁ mudam āpa vismitā
> gṛhīta-dehaṁ nija-yoga-māyayā
> prajāpatiś cāha jayeti vismitaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:dṛṣṭvā]]---seeing; [[synonyms:a:aditiḥ]]---mother Aditi; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---Him (the Supreme Personality of Godhead); [[synonyms:n:nija]]-[[synonyms:g:garbha]]-[[synonyms:s:sambhavam]]---born of her own womb; [[synonyms:p:param]]---the Supreme; [[synonyms:p:pumāṁsam]]---the Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:m:mudam]]---great happiness; [[synonyms:ā:āpa]]---conceived; [[synonyms:v:vismitā]]---being very much astonished; [[synonyms:g:gṛhīta]]---accepted; [[synonyms:d:deham]]---body, or transcendental form; [[synonyms:n:nija]]-[[synonyms:y:yoga]]-[[synonyms:m:māyayā]]---by His own spiritual potency; [[synonyms:p:prajāpatiḥ]]---Kaśyapa Muni; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:ā:āha]]---said; [[synonyms:j:jaya]]---all glories; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:v:vismitaḥ]]---being astonished.

===== Translation =====

**When Aditi saw the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who had appeared from her own womb, having accepted a transcendental body by His own spiritual potency, she was struck with wonder and was very happy. Upon seeing the child, Prajāpati Kaśyapa exclaimed, "Jaya! Jaya!" in great happiness and wonder.**
