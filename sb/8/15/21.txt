====== SB 8.15.21 ======

===== Text =====

> mṛdaṅga-śaṅkhānaka-dundubhi-svanaiḥ
> satāla-vīṇā-murajeṣṭa-veṇubhiḥ
> nṛtyaiḥ savādyair upadeva-gītakair
> manoramāṁ sva-prabhayā jita-prabhām

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mṛdaṅga]]---of drums; [[synonyms:ś:śaṅkha]]---conchshells; [[synonyms:ā:ānaka]]-[[synonyms:d:dundubhi]]---and kettledrums; [[synonyms:s:svanaiḥ]]---by the sounds; [[synonyms:s:sa]]-[[synonyms:t:tāla]]---in perfect tune; [[synonyms:v:vīṇā]]---a stringed instrument; [[synonyms:m:muraja]]---a kind of drum; [[synonyms:i:iṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:v:veṇubhiḥ]]---accompanied by the very nice sound of the flute; [[synonyms:n:nṛtyaiḥ]]---with dancing; [[synonyms:s:sa]]-[[synonyms:v:vādyaiḥ]]---with concert instruments; [[synonyms:u:upadeva]]-[[synonyms:g:gītakaiḥ]]---with singing by the secondary demigods like the Gandharvas; [[synonyms:m:manoramām]]---beautiful and pleasing; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:p:prabhayā]]---by its own brilliance; [[synonyms:j:jita]]-[[synonyms:p:prabhām]]---the personification of beauty was conquered.

===== Translation =====

**The city was filled with the sounds of mṛdaṅgas, conchshells, kettledrums, flutes and well-tuned stringed instruments all playing in concert. There was constant dancing and the Gandharvas sang. The combined beauty of Indrapurī defeated beauty personified.**
