====== SB 8.15.20 ======

===== Text =====

> muktā-vitānair maṇi-hema-ketubhir
> nānā-patākā-valabhībhir āvṛtām
> śikhaṇḍi-pārāvata-bhṛṅga-nāditāṁ
> vaimānika-strī-kala-gīta-maṅgalām

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:muktā]]-[[synonyms:v:vitānaiḥ]]---by canopies decorated with pearls; [[synonyms:m:maṇi]]-[[synonyms:h:hema]]-[[synonyms:k:ketubhiḥ]]---with flags made with pearls and gold; [[synonyms:n:nānā]]-[[synonyms:p:patākā]]---possessing various kinds of flags; [[synonyms:v:valabhībhiḥ]]---with the domes of the palaces; [[synonyms:ā:āvṛtām]]---covered; [[synonyms:ś:śikhaṇḍi]]---of birds like peacocks; [[synonyms:p:pārāvata]]---pigeons; [[synonyms:b:bhṛṅga]]---bees; [[synonyms:n:nāditām]]---vibrated by the respective sounds; [[synonyms:v:vaimānika]]---getting on airplanes; [[synonyms:s:strī]]---of women; [[synonyms:k:kala]]-[[synonyms:g:gīta]]---from the choral singing; [[synonyms:m:maṅgalām]]---full of auspiciousness.

===== Translation =====

**The city was shaded by canopies decorated with pearls, and the domes of the palaces had flags of pearl and gold. The city always resounded with the vibrations of peacocks, pigeons and bees, and above the city flew airplanes full of beautiful women who constantly chanted auspicious songs that were very pleasing to the ear.**
