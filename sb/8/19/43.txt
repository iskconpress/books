====== SB 8.19.43 ======

===== Text =====

> strīṣu narma-vivāhe ca
> vṛtty-arthe prāṇa-saṅkaṭe
> go-brāhmaṇārthe hiṁsāyāṁ
> nānṛtaṁ syāj jugupsitam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:strīṣu]]---to encourage a woman and bring her under control; [[synonyms:n:narma]]-[[synonyms:v:vivāhe]]---in joking or in a marriage ceremony; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:v:vṛtti]]-[[synonyms:a:arthe]]---for earning one's livelihood, as in business; [[synonyms:p:prāṇa]]-[[synonyms:s:saṅkaṭe]]---or in time of danger; [[synonyms:g:go]]-[[synonyms:b:brāhmaṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:arthe]]---for the sake of cow protection and brahminical culture; [[synonyms:h:hiṁsāyām]]---for any person who is going to be killed because of enmity; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:a:anṛtam]]---falsity; [[synonyms:s:syāt]]---becomes; [[synonyms:j:jugupsitam]]---abominable.

===== Translation =====

**In flattering a woman to bring her under control, in joking, in a marriage ceremony, in earning one's livelihood, when one's life is in danger, in protecting cows and brahminical culture, or in protecting a person from an enemy's hand, falsity is never condemned.**


Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Eighth Canto, Nineteenth Chapter, of the //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam,// entitled, "Lord Vāmanadeva Begs Charity from Bali Mahārāja."
