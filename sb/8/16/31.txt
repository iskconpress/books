====== SB 8.16.31 ======

===== Text =====

> namo dvi-śīrṣṇe tri-pade
> catuḥ-śṛṅgāya tantave
> sapta-hastāya yajñāya
> trayī-vidyātmane namaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:namaḥ]]---I offer my respectful obeisances unto You; [[synonyms:d:dvi]]-[[synonyms:ś:śīrṣṇe]]---who have two heads; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:p:pade]]---who have three legs; [[synonyms:c:catuḥ]]-[[synonyms:ś:śṛṅgāya]]---who have four horns; [[synonyms:t:tantave]]---who expand; [[synonyms:s:sapta]]-[[synonyms:h:hastāya]]---who have seven hands; [[synonyms:y:yajñāya]]---unto the //yajña-puruṣa,// the supreme enjoyer; [[synonyms:t:trayī]]---the three modes of Vedic ritualistic ceremonies; [[synonyms:v:vidyā]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmane]]---the Personality of Godhead, the embodiment of all knowledge; [[synonyms:n:namaḥ]]---I offer my respectful obeisances unto You.

===== Translation =====

**I offer my respectful obeisances unto You, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who have two heads [prāyaṇīya and udāyanīya], three legs [savana-traya], four horns [the four Vedas] and seven hands [the seven chandas, such as Gāyatrī]. I offer my obeisances unto You, whose heart and soul are the three Vedic rituals [karma-kāṇḍa, jñāna-kāṇḍa and upāsanā-kāṇḍa] and who expand these rituals in the form of sacrifice.**
