====== SB 8.16.26 ======

===== Text =====

> sinīvālyāṁ mṛdālipya
> snāyāt kroḍa-vidīrṇayā
> yadi labhyeta vai srotasy
> etaṁ mantram udīrayet

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sinīvālyām]]---on the dark-moon day; [[synonyms:m:mṛdā]]---with dirt; [[synonyms:ā:ālipya]]---smearing the body; [[synonyms:s:snāyāt]]---one should bathe; [[synonyms:k:kroḍa]]-[[synonyms:v:vidīrṇayā]]---dug up by the tusk of a boar; [[synonyms:y:yadi]]---if; [[synonyms:l:labhyeta]]---it is available; [[synonyms:v:vai]]---indeed; [[synonyms:s:srotasi]]---in a flowing river; [[synonyms:e:etam]] [[synonyms:m:mantram]]---this //mantra//; [[synonyms:u:udīrayet]]---one should chant.

===== Translation =====

**If dirt dug up by a boar is available, on the day of the dark moon one should smear this dirt on his body and then bathe in a flowing river. While bathing, one should chant the following mantra.**
