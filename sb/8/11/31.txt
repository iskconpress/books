====== SB 8.11.31 ======

===== Text =====

> tadāpatad gagana-tale mahā-javaṁ
> vicicchide harir iṣubhiḥ sahasradhā
> tam āhanan nṛpa kuliśena kandhare
> ruṣānvitas tridaśa-patiḥ śiro haran

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tadā]]---at that time; [[synonyms:a:apatat]]---falling like a meteor; [[synonyms:g:gagana]]-[[synonyms:t:tale]]---beneath the sky or on the ground; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:j:javam]]---extremely powerful; [[synonyms:v:vicicchide]]---cut to pieces; [[synonyms:h:hariḥ]]---Indra; [[synonyms:i:iṣubhiḥ]]---by his arrows; [[synonyms:s:sahasradhā]]---into thousands of pieces; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---that Namuci; [[synonyms:ā:āhanat]]---struck; [[synonyms:n:nṛpa]]---O King; [[synonyms:k:kuliśena]]---with his thunderbolt; [[synonyms:k:kandhare]]---on the shoulder; [[synonyms:r:ruṣā]]-[[synonyms:a:anvitaḥ]]---being very angry; [[synonyms:t:tridaśa]]-[[synonyms:p:patiḥ]]---Indra, the King of the demigods; [[synonyms:ś:śiraḥ]]---the head; [[synonyms:h:haran]]---to separate.

===== Translation =====

**O King, when Indra, King of heaven, saw this very powerful spear falling toward the ground like a blazing meteor, he immediately cut it to pieces with his arrows. Then, being very angry, he struck Namuci's shoulder with his thunderbolt to cut off Namuci's head.**
