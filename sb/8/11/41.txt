====== SB 8.11.41 ======

===== Text =====

> gandharva-mukhyau jagatur
> viśvāvasu-parāvasū
> deva-dundubhayo nedur
> nartakyo nanṛtur mudā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:gandharva]]-[[synonyms:m:mukhyau]]---the two chiefs of the Gandharvas; [[synonyms:j:jagatuḥ]]---began to sing nice songs; [[synonyms:v:viśvāvasu]]---named Viśvāvasu; [[synonyms:p:parāvasū]]---named Parāvasu; [[synonyms:d:deva]]-[[synonyms:d:dundubhayaḥ]]---the kettledrums beaten by the demigods; [[synonyms:n:neduḥ]]---made their sound; [[synonyms:n:nartakyaḥ]]---the dancers known as Apsarās; [[synonyms:n:nanṛtuḥ]]---began to dance; [[synonyms:m:mudā]]---in great happiness.

===== Translation =====

**Viśvāvasu and Parāvasu, the two chiefs of the Gandharvas, sang in great happiness. The kettledrums of the demigods sounded, and the Apsarās danced in jubilation.**
