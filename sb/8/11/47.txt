====== SB 8.11.47 ======

===== Text =====

> tatrāvinaṣṭāvayavān
> vidyamāna-śirodharān
> uśanā jīvayām āsa
> saṁjīvanyā sva-vidyayā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tatra]]---on that hill; [[synonyms:a:avinaṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:a:avayavān]]---the demons who had been killed but whose bodily parts had not been lost; [[synonyms:v:vidyamāna]]-[[synonyms:ś:śirodharān]]---whose heads were still existing on their bodies; [[synonyms:u:uśanāḥ]]---Śukrācārya; [[synonyms:j:jīvayām]] [[synonyms:ā:āsa]]---brought to life; [[synonyms:s:saṁjīvanyā]]---by the Saṁjīvanī //mantra//; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:v:vidyayā]]---by his own achievement.

===== Translation =====

**There, on that hill, Śukrācārya brought to life all the dead demoniac soldiers who had not lost their heads, trunks and limbs. He achieved this by his own mantra, known as Saṁjīvanī.**
