====== SB 8.11.26 ======

===== Text =====

> tatas turāṣāḍ iṣu-baddha-pañjarād
> vinirgataḥ sāśva-ratha-dhvajāgraṇīḥ
> babhau diśaḥ khaṁ pṛthivīṁ ca rocayan
> sva-tejasā sūrya iva kṣapātyaye

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tataḥ]]---thereafter; [[synonyms:t:turāṣāṭ]]---another name of Indra; [[synonyms:i:iṣu]]-[[synonyms:b:baddha]]-[[synonyms:p:pañjarāt]]---from the cage of the network of arrows; [[synonyms:v:vinirgataḥ]]---being released; [[synonyms:s:sa]]---with; [[synonyms:a:aśva]]---horses; [[synonyms:r:ratha]]---chariot; [[synonyms:d:dhvaja]]---flag; [[synonyms:a:agraṇīḥ]]---and chariot driver; [[synonyms:b:babhau]]---became; [[synonyms:d:diśaḥ]]---all directions; [[synonyms:k:kham]]---the sky; [[synonyms:p:pṛthivīm]]---the earth; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:r:rocayan]]---pleasing everywhere; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:t:tejasā]]---by his personal effulgence; [[synonyms:s:sūryaḥ]]---the sun; [[synonyms:i:iva]]---like; [[synonyms:k:kṣapā]]-[[synonyms:a:atyaye]]---at the end of night.

===== Translation =====

**Thereafter, Indra released himself from the cage of the network of arrows. Appearing with his chariot, flag, horses and chariot driver and thus pleasing the sky, the earth and all directions, he shone effulgently like the sun at the end of night. Indra was bright and beautiful in the vision of everyone.**
