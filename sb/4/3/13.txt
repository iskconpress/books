====== SB 4.3.13 ======

> कथं सुतायाः पितृगेहकौतुकं निशम्य देहः सुरवर्य नेङ्गते ।
> अनाहुता अप्यभियन्ति सौहृदं भर्तुर्गुरोर्देहकृतश्च केतनम् ॥१३॥

===== Text =====

> kathaṁ sutāyāḥ pitṛ-geha-kautukaṁ
> niśamya dehaḥ sura-varya neṅgate
> anāhutā apy abhiyanti sauhṛdaṁ
> bhartur guror deha-kṛtaś ca ketanam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:katham]]---how; [[synonyms:s:sutāyāḥ]]---of a daughter; [[synonyms:p:pitṛ]]-[[synonyms:g:geha]]-[[synonyms:k:kautukam]]---the festival in the house of her father; [[synonyms:n:niśamya]]---hearing; [[synonyms:d:dehaḥ]]---the body; [[synonyms:s:sura]]-[[synonyms:v:varya]]---O best of the demigods; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:i:iṅgate]]---disturbed; [[synonyms:a:anāhutāḥ]]---without being called; [[synonyms:a:api]]---even; [[synonyms:a:abhiyanti]]---goes; [[synonyms:s:sauhṛdam]]---a friend; [[synonyms:b:bhartuḥ]]---of the husband; [[synonyms:g:guroḥ]]---of the spiritual master; [[synonyms:d:deha]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛtaḥ]]---of the father; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:k:ketanam]]---the house.

===== Translation =====

**O best of the demigods, how can the body of a daughter remain undisturbed when she hears that some festive event is taking place in her father's house? Even though you may be considering that I have not been invited, there is no harm if one goes to the house of one's friend, husband, spiritual master or father without invitation.**
