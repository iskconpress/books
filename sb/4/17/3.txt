====== SB 4.17.3 ======

> विदुर उवाच
> कस्माद्दधार गोरूपं धरित्री बहुरूपिणी ।
> यां दुदोह पृथुस्तत्र को वत्सो दोहनं च किम् ॥३॥

===== Text =====

> vidura uvāca
> kasmād dadhāra go-rūpaṁ
> dharitrī bahu-rūpiṇī
> yāṁ dudoha pṛthus tatra
> ko vatso dohanaṁ ca kim

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:viduraḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---Vidura inquired; [[synonyms:k:kasmāt]]---why; [[synonyms:d:dadhāra]]---took; [[synonyms:g:go]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpam]]---the shape of a cow; [[synonyms:d:dharitrī]]---the earth; [[synonyms:b:bahu]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpiṇī]]---who has many other forms; [[synonyms:y:yām]]---whom; [[synonyms:d:dudoha]]---milked; [[synonyms:p:pṛthuḥ]]---King Pṛthu; [[synonyms:t:tatra]]---there; [[synonyms:k:kaḥ]]---who; [[synonyms:v:vatsaḥ]]---the calf; [[synonyms:d:dohanam]]---the milking pot; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:k:kim]]---what.

===== Translation =====

**Vidura inquired from the great sage Maitreya: My dear brāhmaṇa, since mother earth can appear in different shapes, why did she take the shape of a cow? And when King Pṛthu milked her, who became the calf, and what was the milking pot?**
