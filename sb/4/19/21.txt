====== SB 4.19.21 ======

> अत्रिणा चोदितस्तस्मै सन्दधे विशिखं रुषा ।
> सोऽश्वं रूपं च तद्धित्वा तस्थावन्तर्हितः स्वराट् ॥२१॥

===== Text =====

> atriṇā coditas tasmai
> sandadhe viśikhaṁ ruṣā
> so 'śvaṁ rūpaṁ ca tad dhitvā
> tasthāv antarhitaḥ svarāṭ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:atriṇā]]---by the great sage Atri; [[synonyms:c:coditaḥ]]---inspired; [[synonyms:t:tasmai]]---for Lord Indra; [[synonyms:s:sandadhe]]---fixed; [[synonyms:v:viśikham]]---his arrow; [[synonyms:r:ruṣā]]---out of great anger; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---King Indra; [[synonyms:a:aśvam]]---horse; [[synonyms:r:rūpam]]---the dress of a //sannyāsī//; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---that; [[synonyms:h:hitvā]]---giving up; [[synonyms:t:tasthau]]---he remained there; [[synonyms:a:antarhitaḥ]]---invisible; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:r:rāṭ]]---the independent Indra.

===== Translation =====

**When the great sage Atri again gave directions, the son of King Pṛthu became very angry and placed an arrow on his bow. Upon seeing this, King Indra immediately abandoned the false dress of a sannyāsī and, giving up the horse, made himself invisible.**
