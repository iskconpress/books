====== SB 4.18.12 ======

> इति प्रियं हितं वाक्यं भुव आदाय भूपतिः ।
> वत्सं कृत्वा मनुं पाणावदुहत्सकलौषधीः ॥१२॥

===== Text =====

> iti priyaṁ hitaṁ vākyaṁ
> bhuva ādāya bhūpatiḥ
> vatsaṁ kṛtvā manuṁ pāṇāv
> aduhat sakalauṣadhīḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:p:priyam]]---pleasing; [[synonyms:h:hitam]]---beneficial; [[synonyms:v:vākyam]]---words; [[synonyms:b:bhuvaḥ]]---of the earth; [[synonyms:ā:ādāya]]---taking into consideration; [[synonyms:b:bhū]]-[[synonyms:p:patiḥ]]---the King; [[synonyms:v:vatsam]]---calf; [[synonyms:k:kṛtvā]]---making; [[synonyms:m:manum]]---Svāyambhuva Manu; [[synonyms:p:pāṇau]]---in his hands; [[synonyms:a:aduhat]]---milked; [[synonyms:s:sakala]]---all; [[synonyms:o:oṣadhīḥ]]---herbs and grains.

===== Translation =====

**After hearing the auspicious and pleasing words of the planet earth, the King accepted them. He then transformed Svāyambhuva Manu into a calf and milked all the herbs and grains from the earth in the form of a cow, keeping them in his cupped hands.**
