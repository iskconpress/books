====== SB 4.10.21 ======

> अपश्यमानः स तदाततायिनं महामृधे कञ्चन मानवोत्तमः ।
> पुरीं दिदृक्षन्नपि नाविशद्द्विषां न मायिनां वेद चिकीर्षितं जनः ॥२१॥

===== Text =====

> apaśyamānaḥ sa tadātatāyinaṁ
> mahā-mṛdhe kañcana mānavottamaḥ
> purīṁ didṛkṣann api nāviśad dviṣāṁ
> na māyināṁ veda cikīrṣitaṁ janaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:apaśyamānaḥ]]---while not observing; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---Dhruva; [[synonyms:t:tadā]]---at that time; [[synonyms:ā:ātatāyinam]]---armed opposing soldiers; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:m:mṛdhe]]---in that great battlefield; [[synonyms:k:kañcana]]---any; [[synonyms:m:mānava]]-[[synonyms:u:uttamaḥ]]---the best of the human beings; [[synonyms:p:purīm]]---the city; [[synonyms:d:didṛkṣan]]---wishing to see; [[synonyms:a:api]]---although; [[synonyms:n:na]] [[synonyms:ā:āviśat]]---did not enter; [[synonyms:d:dviṣām]]---of the enemies; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:m:māyinām]]---of the mystics; [[synonyms:v:veda]]---knows; [[synonyms:c:cikīrṣitam]]---the plans; [[synonyms:j:janaḥ]]---anyone.

===== Translation =====

**Dhruva Mahārāja, the best of human beings, observed that in that great battlefield not one of the opposing soldiers was left standing with proper weapons. He then desired to see the city of Alakāpurī, but he thought to himself, "No one knows the plans of the mystic Yakṣas."**
