====== SB 4.6.26 ======

> ययोस्तत्स्नानविभ्रष्ट नवकुङ्कुमपिञ्जरम् ।
> वितृषोऽपि पिबन्त्यम्भः पाययन्तो गजा गजीः ॥२६॥

===== Text =====

> yayos tat-snāna-vibhraṣṭa-
> nava-kuṅkuma-piñjaram
> vitṛṣo 'pi pibanty ambhaḥ
> pāyayanto gajā gajīḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yayoḥ]]---in both of which rivers; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:s:snāna]]---by the bathing of them (the damsels of the heavenly planets); [[synonyms:v:vibhraṣṭa]]---fallen off; [[synonyms:n:nava]]---fresh; [[synonyms:k:kuṅkuma]]---with //kuṅkuma// powder; [[synonyms:p:piñjaram]]---yellow; [[synonyms:v:vitṛṣaḥ]]---not being thirsty; [[synonyms:a:api]]---even; [[synonyms:p:pibanti]]---drink; [[synonyms:a:ambhaḥ]]---the water; [[synonyms:p:pāyayantaḥ]]---causing to drink; [[synonyms:g:gajāḥ]]---the elephants; [[synonyms:g:gajīḥ]]---the female elephants.

===== Translation =====

**After the damsels of the heavenly planets bathe in the water, it becomes yellowish and fragrant due to the kuṅkuma from their bodies. Thus the elephants come to bathe there with their wives, the she-elephants, and they also drink the water, although they are not thirsty.**
