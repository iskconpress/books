====== SB 4.13.29 ======

> मैत्रेय उवाच
> अङ्गो द्विजवचः श्रुत्वा यजमानः सुदुर्मनाः ।
> तत्प्रष्टुं व्यसृजद्वाचं सदस्यांस्तदनुज्ञया ॥२९॥

===== Text =====

> maitreya uvāca
> aṅgo dvija-vacaḥ śrutvā
> yajamānaḥ sudurmanāḥ
> tat praṣṭuṁ vyasṛjad vācaṁ
> sadasyāṁs tad-anujñayā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:maitreyaḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---the great sage Maitreya answered; [[synonyms:a:aṅgaḥ]]---King Aṅga; [[synonyms:d:dvija]]-[[synonyms:v:vacaḥ]]---the //brāhmaṇas'// words; [[synonyms:ś:śrutvā]]---after hearing; [[synonyms:y:yajamānaḥ]]---the performer of the sacrifice; [[synonyms:s:sudurmanāḥ]]---very much aggrieved in mind; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---about that; [[synonyms:p:praṣṭum]]---in order to inquire; [[synonyms:v:vyasṛjat]] [[synonyms:v:vācam]]---he spoke; [[synonyms:s:sadasyān]]---to the priests; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---their; [[synonyms:a:anujñayā]]---taking permission.

===== Translation =====

**Maitreya explained that King Aṅga, after hearing the statements of the priests, was greatly aggrieved. At that time he took permission from the priests to break his silence and inquired from all the priests who were present in the sacrificial arena.**
