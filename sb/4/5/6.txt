====== SB 4.5.6 ======

> अन्वीयमानः स तु रुद्रपार्षदैर्भृशं नदद्भिर्व्यनदत्सुभैरवम् ।
> उद्यम्य शूलं जगदन्तकान्तकं सम्प्राद्रवद्घोषणभूषणाङ्घ्रिः ॥६॥

===== Text =====

> anvīyamānaḥ sa tu rudra-pārṣadair
> bhṛśaṁ nadadbhir vyanadat subhairavam
> udyamya śūlaṁ jagad-antakāntakaṁ
> samprādravad ghoṣaṇa-bhūṣaṇāṅghriḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:anvīyamānaḥ]]---being followed; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---he (Vīrabhadra); [[synonyms:t:tu]]---but; [[synonyms:r:rudra]]-[[synonyms:p:pārṣadaiḥ]]---by the soldiers of Lord Śiva; [[synonyms:b:bhṛśam]]---tumultuously; [[synonyms:n:nadadbhiḥ]]---roaring; [[synonyms:v:vyanadat]]---sounded; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:b:bhairavam]]---very fearful; [[synonyms:u:udyamya]]---carrying; [[synonyms:ś:śūlam]]---a trident; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:a:antaka]]---death; [[synonyms:a:antakam]]---killing; [[synonyms:s:samprādravat]]---hurried towards (the sacrifice of Dakṣa); [[synonyms:g:ghoṣaṇa]]---roaring; [[synonyms:b:bhūṣaṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅghriḥ]]---with bangles on his legs.

===== Translation =====

**Many other soldiers of Lord Śiva followed the fierce personality in a tumultuous uproar. He carried a great trident, fearful enough to kill even death, and on his legs he wore bangles which seemed to roar.**
