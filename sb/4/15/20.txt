====== SB 4.15.20 ======

> सिन्धवः पर्वता नद्यो रथवीथीर्महात्मनः ।
> सूतोऽथ मागधो वन्दी तं स्तोतुमुपतस्थिरे ॥२०॥

===== Text =====

> sindhavaḥ parvatā nadyo
> ratha-vīthīr mahātmanaḥ
> sūto 'tha māgadho vandī
> taṁ stotum upatasthire

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sindhavaḥ]]---the seas; [[synonyms:p:parvatāḥ]]---the mountains; [[synonyms:n:nadyaḥ]]---the rivers; [[synonyms:r:ratha]]-[[synonyms:v:vīthīḥ]]---the paths for the chariot to pass; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmanaḥ]]---of the great soul; [[synonyms:s:sūtaḥ]]---a professional who offers praises; [[synonyms:a:atha]]---then; [[synonyms:m:māgadhaḥ]]---a professional bard; [[synonyms:v:vandī]]---a professional who offers prayers; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---him; [[synonyms:s:stotum]]---to praise; [[synonyms:u:upatasthire]]---presented themselves.

===== Translation =====

**The seas, mountains and rivers gave him room to drive his chariot without impediments, and a sūta, a māgadha and a vandī offered prayers and praises. They all presented themselves before him to perform their respective duties.**
