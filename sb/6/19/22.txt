====== SB 6.19.22 ======

===== Text =====

> śvo-bhūte 'pa upaspṛśya
> kṛṣṇam abhyarcya pūrvavat
> payaḥ-śṛtena juhuyāc
> caruṇā saha sarpiṣā
> pāka-yajña-vidhānena
> dvādaśaivāhutīḥ patiḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śvaḥ]]-[[synonyms:b:bhūte]]---on the following morning; [[synonyms:a:apaḥ]]---water; [[synonyms:u:upaspṛśya]]---contacting; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇam]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:a:abhyarcya]]---worshiping; [[synonyms:p:pūrva]]-[[synonyms:v:vat]]---as previously; [[synonyms:p:payaḥ]]-[[synonyms:ś:śṛtena]]---with boiled milk; [[synonyms:j:juhuyāt]]---one should offer; [[synonyms:c:caruṇā]]---with an offering of sweet rice; [[synonyms:s:saha]]---with; [[synonyms:s:sarpiṣā]]---ghee; [[synonyms:p:pāka]]-[[synonyms:y:yajña]]-[[synonyms:v:vidhānena]]---according to the injunctions of the //Gṛhya-sūtras//; [[synonyms:d:dvādaśa]]---twelve; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---indeed; [[synonyms:ā:āhutīḥ]]---oblations; [[synonyms:p:patiḥ]]---the husband.

===== Translation =====

**On the morning of the next day, one should wash oneself, and after worshiping Lord Kṛṣṇa as before, one should cook as one cooks for festivals as stated in the Gṛhya-sūtras. Sweet rice should be cooked with ghee, and with this preparation the husband should offer oblations to the fire twelve times.**
