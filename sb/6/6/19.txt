====== SB 6.6.19 ======

===== Text =====

> prajāpater aṅgirasaḥ
> svadhā patnī pitṝn atha
> atharvāṅgirasaṁ vedaṁ
> putratve cākarot satī

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:prajāpateḥ]] [[synonyms:a:aṅgirasaḥ]]---of another //prajāpati,// known as Aṅgirā; [[synonyms:s:svadhā]]---Svadhā; [[synonyms:p:patnī]]---his wife; [[synonyms:p:pitṝn]]---the Pitās; [[synonyms:a:atha]]---thereafter; [[synonyms:a:atharva]]-[[synonyms:ā:āṅgirasam]]---Atharvāṅgirasa; [[synonyms:v:vedam]]---the personified //Veda//; [[synonyms:p:putratve]]---as the son; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:a:akarot]]---accepted; [[synonyms:s:satī]]---Satī.

===== Translation =====

**The prajāpati Aṅgirā had two wives, named Svadhā and Satī. The wife named Svadhā accepted all the Pitās as her sons, and Satī accepted the Atharvāṅgirasa Veda as her son.**
