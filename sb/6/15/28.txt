====== SB 6.15.28 ======

===== Text =====

> yat-pāda-mūlam upasṛtya narendra pūrve
> śarvādayo bhramam imaṁ dvitayaṁ visṛjya
> sadyas tadīyam atulānadhikaṁ mahitvaṁ
> prāpur bhavān api paraṁ na cirād upaiti

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yat]]-[[synonyms:p:pāda]]-[[synonyms:m:mūlam]]---the lotus feet of whom (Lord Saṅkarṣaṇa); [[synonyms:u:upasṛtya]]---obtaining shelter at; [[synonyms:n:nara]]-[[synonyms:i:indra]]---O King; [[synonyms:p:pūrve]]---formerly; [[synonyms:ś:śarva]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādayaḥ]]---great demigods like Lord Mahādeva; [[synonyms:b:bhramam]]---illusion; [[synonyms:i:imam]]---this; [[synonyms:d:dvitayam]]---consisting of duality; [[synonyms:v:visṛjya]]---giving up; [[synonyms:s:sadyaḥ]]---immediately; [[synonyms:t:tadīyam]]---His; [[synonyms:a:atula]]---unequaled; [[synonyms:a:anadhikam]]---unsurpassed; [[synonyms:m:mahitvam]]---glories; [[synonyms:p:prāpuḥ]]---achieved; [[synonyms:b:bhavān]]---yourself; [[synonyms:a:api]]---also; [[synonyms:p:param]]---the supreme abode; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:c:cirāt]]---after a long time; [[synonyms:u:upaiti]]---will obtain.

===== Translation =====

**My dear King, in former days Lord Śiva and other demigods took shelter of the lotus feet of Saṅkarṣaṇa. Thus they immediately got free from the illusion of duality and achieved unequaled and unsurpassed glories in spiritual life. You will very soon attain that very same position.**
