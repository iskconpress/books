====== SB 6.16.60 ======

===== Text =====

> sukhāya duḥkha-mokṣāya
> kurvāte dampatī kriyāḥ
> tato 'nivṛttir aprāptir
> duḥkhasya ca sukhasya ca

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sukhāya]]---for happiness; [[synonyms:d:duḥkha]]-[[synonyms:m:mokṣāya]]---for release from the unhappy state; [[synonyms:k:kurvāte]]---perform; [[synonyms:d:dam]]-[[synonyms:p:patī]]---the wife and husband; [[synonyms:k:kriyāḥ]]---activities; [[synonyms:t:tataḥ]]---from that; [[synonyms:a:anivṛttiḥ]]---no cessation; [[synonyms:a:aprāptiḥ]]---no achievement; [[synonyms:d:duḥkhasya]]---of distress; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:s:sukhasya]]---of happiness; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also.

===== Translation =====

**As husband and wife, a man and woman plan together to attain happiness and decrease unhappiness, working jointly in many ways, but because their activities are full of desires, these activities are never a source of happiness, and they never diminish distress. On the contrary, they are a cause of great unhappiness.**
