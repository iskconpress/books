====== SB 6.16.21 ======

===== Text =====

> vacasy uparate 'prāpya
> ya eko manasā saha
> anāma-rūpaś cin-mātraḥ
> so 'vyān naḥ sad-asat-paraḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vacasi]]---when the words; [[synonyms:u:uparate]]---cease; [[synonyms:a:aprāpya]]---not achieving the goal; [[synonyms:y:yaḥ]]---He who; [[synonyms:e:ekaḥ]]---one without a second; [[synonyms:m:manasā]]---the mind; [[synonyms:s:saha]]---with; [[synonyms:a:anāma]]---with no material name; [[synonyms:r:rūpaḥ]]---or material form; [[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:m:mātraḥ]]---totally spiritual; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---He; [[synonyms:a:avyāt]]---may kindly protect; [[synonyms:n:naḥ]]---us; [[synonyms:s:sat]]-[[synonyms:a:asat]]-[[synonyms:p:paraḥ]]---who is the cause of all causes (the supreme cause).

===== Translation =====

**The words and mind of the conditioned soul cannot approach the Supreme Personality of Godhead, for material names and forms are not applicable to the Lord, who is entirely spiritual, beyond the conception of gross and subtle forms. The impersonal Brahman is another of His forms. May He, by His pleasure, protect us.**

===== Purport =====

The impersonal Brahman, which is the effulgence of the Lord, is described in this verse.
