====== SB 6.9.23 ======

===== Text =====

> yasyoru-śṛṅge jagatīṁ sva-nāvaṁ
> manur yathābadhya tatāra durgam
> sa eva nas tvāṣṭra-bhayād durantāt
> trātāśritān vāricaro 'pi nūnam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yasya]]---of whom; [[synonyms:u:uru]]---very strong and high; [[synonyms:ś:śṛṅge]]---on the horn; [[synonyms:j:jagatīm]]---in the form of the world; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:n:nāvam]]---his own boat; [[synonyms:m:manuḥ]]---Manu, King Satyavrata; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]---just as; [[synonyms:ā:ābadhya]]---binding; [[synonyms:t:tatāra]]---crossed; [[synonyms:d:durgam]]---the very difficult to cross (inundation); [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---He (the Supreme Personality of Godhead); [[synonyms:e:eva]]---certainly; [[synonyms:n:naḥ]]---us; [[synonyms:t:tvāṣṭra]]-[[synonyms:b:bhayāt]]---from fear of the son of Tvaṣṭā; [[synonyms:d:durantāt]]---endless; [[synonyms:t:trātā]]---deliverer; [[synonyms:ā:āśritān]]---dependents (like us); [[synonyms:v:vāri]]-[[synonyms:c:caraḥ]] [[synonyms:a:api]]---although taking the form of a fish; [[synonyms:n:nūnam]]---indeed.

===== Translation =====

**The Manu named King Satyavrata formerly saved himself by tying the small boat of the entire world to the horn of the Matsya avatāra, the fish incarnation. By the grace of the Matsya avatāra, Manu saved himself from the great danger of the flood. May that same fish incarnation save us from the great and fearful danger caused by the son of Tvaṣṭā.**
