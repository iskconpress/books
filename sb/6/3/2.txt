====== SB 6.3.2 ======

===== Text =====

> yamasya devasya na daṇḍa-bhaṅgaḥ
> kutaścanarṣe śruta-pūrva āsīt
> etan mune vṛścati loka-saṁśayaṁ
> na hi tvad-anya iti me viniścitam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yamasya]]---of Yamarāja; [[synonyms:d:devasya]]---the demigod in charge of judgment; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:d:daṇḍa]]-[[synonyms:b:bhaṅgaḥ]]---the breaking of the order; [[synonyms:k:kutaścana]]---from anywhere; [[synonyms:ṛ:ṛṣe]]---O great sage; [[synonyms:ś:śruta]]-[[synonyms:p:pūrvaḥ]]---heard before; [[synonyms:ā:āsīt]]---was; [[synonyms:e:etat]]---this; [[synonyms:m:mune]]---O great sage; [[synonyms:v:vṛścati]]---can eradicate; [[synonyms:l:loka]]-[[synonyms:s:saṁśayam]]---the doubt of people; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---indeed; [[synonyms:t:tvat]]-[[synonyms:a:anyaḥ]]---anyone other than you; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:m:me]]---by me; [[synonyms:v:viniścitam]]---concluded.

===== Translation =====

**O great sage, never before has it been heard anywhere that an order from Yamarāja has been baffled. Therefore I think that people will have doubts about this that no one but you can eradicate. Since that is my firm conviction, kindly explain the reasons for these events.**
