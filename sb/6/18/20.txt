====== SB 6.18.20 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-rājovāca
> kathaṁ ta āsuraṁ bhāvam
> apohyautpattikaṁ guro
> indreṇa prāpitāḥ sātmyaṁ
> kiṁ tat sādhu kṛtaṁ hi taiḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:r:rājā]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---King Parīkṣit said; [[synonyms:k:katham]]---why; [[synonyms:t:te]]---they; [[synonyms:ā:āsuram]]---demoniac; [[synonyms:b:bhāvam]]---mentality; [[synonyms:a:apohya]]---giving up; [[synonyms:a:autpattikam]]---due to birth; [[synonyms:g:guro]]---my dear lord; [[synonyms:i:indreṇa]]---by Indra; [[synonyms:p:prāpitāḥ]]---were converted; [[synonyms:s:sa]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmyam]]---to demigods; [[synonyms:k:kim]]---whether; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---therefore; [[synonyms:s:sādhu]]---pious activities; [[synonyms:k:kṛtam]]---performed; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---indeed; [[synonyms:t:taiḥ]]---by them.

===== Translation =====

**King Parīkṣit inquired: My dear lord, due to their birth, the forty-nine Maruts must have been obsessed with a demoniac mentality. Why did Indra, the King of heaven, convert them into demigods? Did they perform any rituals or pious activities?**
