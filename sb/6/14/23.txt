====== SB 6.14.23 ======

===== Text =====

> citraketur uvāca
> bhagavan kiṁ na viditaṁ
> tapo-jñāna-samādhibhiḥ
> yogināṁ dhvasta-pāpānāṁ
> bahir antaḥ śarīriṣu

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:c:citraketuḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---King Citraketu replied; [[synonyms:b:bhagavan]]---O most powerful sage; [[synonyms:k:kim]]---what; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:v:viditam]]---is understood; [[synonyms:t:tapaḥ]]---by austerity; [[synonyms:j:jñāna]]---knowledge; [[synonyms:s:samādhibhiḥ]]---and by //samādhi// (trance, transcendental meditation); [[synonyms:y:yoginām]]---by the great //yogīs// or devotees; [[synonyms:d:dhvasta]]-[[synonyms:p:pāpānām]]---who are fully freed from all sinful reactions; [[synonyms:b:bahiḥ]]---externally; [[synonyms:a:antaḥ]]---internally; [[synonyms:ś:śarīriṣu]]---in conditioned souls, who have material bodies.

===== Translation =====

**King Citraketu said: O great lord Aṅgirā, because of austerity, knowledge and transcendental samādhi, you are freed from all the reactions of sinful life. Therefore, as a perfect yogī, you can understand everything external and internal regarding embodied, conditioned souls like us.**
