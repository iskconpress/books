====== SB 6.14.58 ======

===== Text =====

> nāhaṁ tanūja dadṛśe hata-maṅgalā te
> mugdha-smitaṁ mudita-vīkṣaṇam ānanābjam
> kiṁ vā gato 'sy apunar-anvayam anya-lokaṁ
> nīto 'ghṛṇena na śṛṇomi kalā giras te

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:t:tanū]]-[[synonyms:j:ja]]---my dear son (born of my body); [[synonyms:d:dadṛśe]]---saw; [[synonyms:h:hata]]-[[synonyms:m:maṅgalā]]---because of my being the most unfortunate; [[synonyms:t:te]]---your; [[synonyms:m:mugdha]]-[[synonyms:s:smitam]]---with charming smiling; [[synonyms:m:mudita]]-[[synonyms:v:vīkṣaṇam]]---with closed eyes; [[synonyms:ā:ānana]]-[[synonyms:a:abjam]]---lotus face; [[synonyms:k:kiṁ]] [[synonyms:v:vā]]---whether; [[synonyms:g:gataḥ]]---gone away; [[synonyms:a:asi]]---you are; [[synonyms:a:a]]-[[synonyms:p:punaḥ]]-[[synonyms:a:anvayam]]---from which one does not return; [[synonyms:a:anya]]-[[synonyms:l:lokam]]---to another planet, or the planet of Yamarāja; [[synonyms:n:nītaḥ]]---having been taken away; [[synonyms:a:aghṛṇena]]---by the cruel Yamarāja; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:ś:śṛṇomi]]---I can hear; [[synonyms:k:kalāḥ]]---very pleasing; [[synonyms:g:giraḥ]]---utterances; [[synonyms:t:te]]---your.

===== Translation =====

**My dear son, I am certainly most unfortunate, for I can no longer see your mild smiling. You have closed your eyes forever. I therefore conclude that you have been taken from this planet to another, from which you will not return. My dear son, I can no longer hear your pleasing voice.**
