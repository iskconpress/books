====== SB 6.14.43 ======

===== Text =====

> vidveṣa-naṣṭa-matayaḥ
> striyo dāruṇa-cetasaḥ
> garaṁ daduḥ kumārāya
> durmarṣā nṛpatiṁ prati

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vidveṣa]]-[[synonyms:n:naṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:m:matayaḥ]]---whose intelligence was lost in envy; [[synonyms:s:striyaḥ]]---the women; [[synonyms:d:dāruṇa]]-[[synonyms:c:cetasaḥ]]---being very hardhearted; [[synonyms:g:garam]]---poison; [[synonyms:d:daduḥ]]---administered; [[synonyms:k:kumārāya]]---unto the boy; [[synonyms:d:durmarṣāḥ]]---being intolerant; [[synonyms:n:nṛpatim]]---the King; [[synonyms:p:prati]]---upon.

===== Translation =====

**As their envy increased, they lost their intelligence. Being extremely hardhearted and unable to tolerate the King's neglect, they finally administered poison to the son.**
