====== SB 6.14.45 ======

===== Text =====

> śayānaṁ suciraṁ bālam
> upadhārya manīṣiṇī
> putram ānaya me bhadre
> iti dhātrīm acodayat

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śayānam]]---lying down; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:c:ciram]]---for a long time; [[synonyms:b:bālam]]---the son; [[synonyms:u:upadhārya]]---thinking; [[synonyms:m:manīṣiṇī]]---very intelligent; [[synonyms:p:putram]]---the son; [[synonyms:ā:ānaya]]---bring; [[synonyms:m:me]]---unto me; [[synonyms:b:bhadre]]---O gentle friend; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:d:dhātrīm]]---unto the nurse; [[synonyms:a:acodayat]]---gave the order.

===== Translation =====

**Thinking that her child had been sleeping for a long time, Queen Kṛtadyuti, who was certainly very intelligent, ordered the nurse, "My dear friend, please bring my son here."**
