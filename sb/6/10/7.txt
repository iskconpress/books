====== SB 6.10.7 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-ṛṣir uvāca
> dharmaṁ vaḥ śrotu-kāmena
> yūyaṁ me pratyudāhṛtāḥ
> eṣa vaḥ priyam ātmānaṁ
> tyajantaṁ santyajāmy aham

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:ṛ:ṛṣiḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---the great saint Dadhīci said; [[synonyms:d:dharmam]]---the principles of religion; [[synonyms:v:vaḥ]]---from you; [[synonyms:ś:śrotu]]-[[synonyms:k:kāmena]]---by the desire to hear; [[synonyms:y:yūyam]]---you; [[synonyms:m:me]]---by me; [[synonyms:p:pratyudāhṛtāḥ]]---replied to the contrary; [[synonyms:e:eṣaḥ]]---this; [[synonyms:v:vaḥ]]---for you; [[synonyms:p:priyam]]---dear; [[synonyms:ā:ātmānam]]---body; [[synonyms:t:tyajantam]]---leaving me anyway, today or tomorrow; [[synonyms:s:santyajāmi]]---give up; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I.

===== Translation =====

**The great sage Dadhīci said: Just to hear from you about religious principles, I refused to offer my body at your request. Now, although my body is extremely dear to me, I must give it up for your better purposes since I know that it will leave me today or tomorrow.**
