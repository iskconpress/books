====== SB 6.10.27 ======

===== Text =====

> tān akṣatān svastimato niśāmya
> śastrāstra-pūgair atha vṛtra-nāthāḥ
> drumair dṛṣadbhir vividhādri-śṛṅgair
> avikṣatāṁs tatrasur indra-sainikān

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tān]]---them (the soldiers of the demigods); [[synonyms:a:akṣatān]]---not injured; [[synonyms:s:svasti]]-[[synonyms:m:mataḥ]]---being very healthy; [[synonyms:n:niśāmya]]---seeing; [[synonyms:ś:śastra]]-[[synonyms:a:astra]]-[[synonyms:p:pūgaiḥ]]---by the bunches of weapons and //mantras//; [[synonyms:a:atha]]---thereupon; [[synonyms:v:vṛtra]]-[[synonyms:n:nāthāḥ]]---the soldiers led by Vṛtrāsura; [[synonyms:d:drumaiḥ]]---by the trees; [[synonyms:d:dṛṣadbhiḥ]]---by the stones; [[synonyms:v:vividha]]---various; [[synonyms:a:adri]]---of mountain s; [[synonyms:ś:śṛṅgaiḥ]]---by the peaks; [[synonyms:a:avikṣatān]]---not injured; [[synonyms:t:tatrasuḥ]]---became afraid; [[synonyms:i:indra]]-[[synonyms:s:sainikān]]---the soldiers of King Indra.

===== Translation =====

**When the soldiers of the demons, commanded by Vṛtrāsura, saw that the soldiers of King Indra were quite well, having not been injured at all by their volleys of weapons, not even by the trees, stones and mountain peaks, the demons were very much afraid.**
