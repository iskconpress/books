====== SB 6.11.10 ======

===== Text =====

> sa indra-śatruḥ kupito bhṛśaṁ tayā
> mahendra-vāhaṁ gadayoru-vikramaḥ
> jaghāna kumbha-sthala unnadan mṛdhe
> tat karma sarve samapūjayan nṛpa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---that; [[synonyms:i:indra]]-[[synonyms:ś:śatruḥ]]---Vṛtrāsura; [[synonyms:k:kupitaḥ]]---being angry; [[synonyms:b:bhṛśam]]---very much; [[synonyms:t:tayā]]---with that; [[synonyms:m:mahendra]]-[[synonyms:v:vāham]]---the elephant who is the carrier of Indra; [[synonyms:g:gadayā]]---by the club; [[synonyms:u:uru]]-[[synonyms:v:vikramaḥ]]---who is famous for his great strength; [[synonyms:j:jaghāna]]---struck; [[synonyms:k:kumbha]]-[[synonyms:s:sthale]]---on the head; [[synonyms:u:unnadan]]---roaring loudly; [[synonyms:m:mṛdhe]]---in that fight; [[synonyms:t:tat]] [[synonyms:k:karma]]---that action (striking the head of Indra's elephant with the club in his left hand); [[synonyms:s:sarve]]---all the soldiers (on both sides); [[synonyms:s:samapūjayan]]---glorified; [[synonyms:n:nṛpa]]---O King Parīkṣit.

===== Translation =====

**O King Parīkṣit, the powerful Vṛtrāsura, the enemy of King Indra, angrily struck the head of Indra's elephant with that club, making a tumultuous sound on the battlefield. For this heroic deed, the soldiers on both sides glorified him.**
