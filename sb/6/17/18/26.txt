====== SB 6.18.26 ======

===== Text =====

> āśāsānasya tasyedaṁ
> dhruvam unnaddha-cetasaḥ
> mada-śoṣaka indrasya
> bhūyād yena suto hi me

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āśāsānasya]]---thinking; [[synonyms:t:tasya]]---of him; [[synonyms:i:idam]]---this (body); [[synonyms:d:dhruvam]]---eternal; [[synonyms:u:unnaddha]]-[[synonyms:c:cetasaḥ]]---whose mind is unrestrained; [[synonyms:m:mada]]-[[synonyms:ś:śoṣakaḥ]]---who can remove the madness; [[synonyms:i:indrasya]]---of Indra; [[synonyms:b:bhūyāt]]---may there be; [[synonyms:y:yena]]---by which; [[synonyms:s:sutaḥ]]---a son; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:m:me]]---of me.

===== Translation =====

**Diti thought: Indra considers his body eternal, and thus he has become unrestrained. I therefore wish to have a son who can remove Indra's madness. Let me adopt some means to help me in this.**

===== Purport =====

One who is in the bodily conception of life is compared in the //śāstras// to animals like cows and asses. Diti wanted to punish Indra, who had become like a lower animal.
