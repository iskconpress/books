====== SB 6.17.25 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-śuka uvāca
> iti prasādya giriśau
> citraketur arindama
> jagāma sva-vimānena
> paśyatoḥ smayatos tayoḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:ś:śukaḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:p:prasādya]]---after satisfying; [[synonyms:g:giriśau]]---Lord Śiva and his wife, Pārvatī; [[synonyms:c:citraketuḥ]]---King Citraketu; [[synonyms:a:arim]]-[[synonyms:d:dama]]---O King Parīkṣit, who are always able to subdue the enemy; [[synonyms:j:jagāma]]---went away; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:v:vimānena]]---by his own airplane; [[synonyms:p:paśyatoḥ]]---were watching; [[synonyms:s:smayatoḥ]]---were smiling; [[synonyms:t:tayoḥ]]---while Lord Śiva and Pārvatī.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: O King Parīkṣit, subduer of the enemy, after Citraketu satisfied Lord Śiva and his wife, Pārvatī, he boarded his airplane and left as they looked on. When Lord Śiva and Pārvatī saw that Citraketu, although informed of the curse, was unafraid, they smiled, being fully astonished by his behavior.**
