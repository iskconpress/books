====== SB 2.1.34 ======

> ईशस्य केशान्विदुरम्बुवाहान्वासस्तु सन्ध्यां कुरुवर्य भूम्नः ।
> अव्यक्तमाहुर्हृदयं मनश्चस चन्द्रमाः सर्वविकारकोशः ॥३४॥

===== Text =====

> īśasya keśān vidur ambuvāhān
> vāsas tu sandhyāṁ kuru-varya bhūmnaḥ
> avyaktam āhur hṛdayaṁ manaś ca
> sa candramāḥ sarva-vikāra-kośaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ī:īśasya]]---of the supreme controller; [[synonyms:k:keśān]]---hairs on the head; [[synonyms:v:viduḥ]]---you may know it from me; [[synonyms:a:ambu]]-[[synonyms:v:vāhān]]---the clouds which carry water; [[synonyms:v:vāsaḥ]] [[synonyms:t:tu]]---the dress; [[synonyms:s:sandhyām]]---termination of day and night; [[synonyms:k:kuru]]-[[synonyms:v:varya]]---O best of the Kurus; [[synonyms:b:bhūmnaḥ]]---of the Almighty; [[synonyms:a:avyaktam]]---the prime cause of material creation; [[synonyms:ā:āhuḥ]]---it is said; [[synonyms:h:hṛdayam]]---intelligence; [[synonyms:m:manaḥ]] [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and the mind; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---He; [[synonyms:c:candramāḥ]]---the moon; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:v:vikāra]]-[[synonyms:k:kośaḥ]]---the reservoir of all changes.

===== Translation =====

**O best amongst the Kurus, the clouds which carry water are the hairs on His head, the terminations of days or nights are His dress, and the supreme cause of material creation is His intelligence. His mind is the moon, the reservoir of all changes.**
