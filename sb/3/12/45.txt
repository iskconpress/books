====== SB 3.12.45 ======

> तस्योष्णिगासील्लोमभ्यो गायत्री च त्वचो विभोः ।
> त्रिष्टुम्मांसात्स्नुतोऽनुष्टुब्जगत्यस्थ्नः प्रजापतेः ॥४५॥

===== Text =====

> tasyoṣṇig āsīl lomabhyo
> gāyatrī ca tvaco vibhoḥ
> triṣṭum māṁsāt snuto 'nuṣṭub
> jagaty asthnaḥ prajāpateḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tasya]]---his; [[synonyms:u:uṣṇik]]---one of the Vedic meters; [[synonyms:ā:āsīt]]---generated; [[synonyms:l:lomabhyaḥ]]---from the hairs on the body; [[synonyms:g:gāyatrī]]---the principal Vedic hymn; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:t:tvacaḥ]]---from the skin; [[synonyms:v:vibhoḥ]]---of the Lord; [[synonyms:t:triṣṭup]]---a particular type of poetic meter; [[synonyms:m:māṁsāt]]---from the flesh; [[synonyms:s:snutaḥ]]---from the sinews; [[synonyms:a:anuṣṭup]]---another type of poetic meter; [[synonyms:j:jagatī]]---another type of poetic meter; [[synonyms:a:asthnaḥ]]---from the bones; [[synonyms:p:prajāpateḥ]]---of the father of the living entities.

===== Translation =====

**Thereafter the art of literary expression, uṣṇik, was generated from the hairs on the body of the almighty Prajāpati. The principal Vedic hymn, gāyatrī, was generated from the skin, triṣṭup from the flesh, anuṣṭup from the veins, and jagatī from the bones of the lord of the living entities.**
