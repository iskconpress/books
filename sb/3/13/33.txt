====== SB 3.13.33 ======

> तमालनीलं सितदन्तकोट्या क्ष्मामुत्क्षिपन्तं गजलीलयाङ्ग ।
> प्रज्ञाय बद्धाञ्जलयोऽनुवाकैर्विरिञ्चिमुख्या उपतस्थुरीशम् ॥३३॥

===== Text =====

> tamāla-nīlaṁ sita-danta-koṭyā
> kṣmām utkṣipantaṁ gaja-līlayāṅga
> prajñāya baddhāñjalayo 'nuvākair
> viriñci-mukhyā upatasthur īśam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tamāla]]---a blue tree named the //tamāla//; [[synonyms:n:nīlam]]---bluish; [[synonyms:s:sita]]---white; [[synonyms:d:danta]]---tusks; [[synonyms:k:koṭyā]]---with the curved edge; [[synonyms:k:kṣmām]]---the earth; [[synonyms:u:utkṣipantam]]---while suspending; [[synonyms:g:gaja]]-[[synonyms:l:līlayā]]---playing like an elephant; [[synonyms:a:aṅga]]---O Vidura; [[synonyms:p:prajñāya]]---after knowing it well; [[synonyms:b:baddha]]---folded; [[synonyms:a:añjalayaḥ]]---hands; [[synonyms:a:anuvākaiḥ]]---by Vedic hymns; [[synonyms:v:viriñci]]---Brahmā; [[synonyms:m:mukhyāḥ]]---headed by; [[synonyms:u:upatasthuḥ]]---offered prayers; [[synonyms:ī:īśam]]---unto the Supreme Lord.

===== Translation =====

**Then the Lord, playing like an elephant, suspended the earth on the edge of His curved white tusks. He assumed a bluish complexion like that of a tamāla tree, and thus the sages, headed by Brahmā, could understand Him to be the Supreme Personality of Godhead and offered respectful obeisances unto the Lord.**
