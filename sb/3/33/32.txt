====== SB 3.33.32 ======

> तस्यास्तद्योगविधुत मार्त्यं मर्त्यमभूत्सरित् ।
> स्रोतसां प्रवरा सौम्य सिद्धिदा सिद्धसेविता ॥३२॥

===== Text =====

> tasyās tad yoga-vidhuta-
> mārtyaṁ martyam abhūt sarit
> srotasāṁ pravarā saumya
> siddhidā siddha-sevitā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tasyāḥ]]---of Devahūti; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---that; [[synonyms:y:yoga]]---by //yoga// practice; [[synonyms:v:vidhuta]]---relinquished; [[synonyms:m:mārtyam]]---material elements; [[synonyms:m:martyam]]---her mortal body; [[synonyms:a:abhūt]]---became; [[synonyms:s:sarit]]---a river; [[synonyms:s:srotasām]]---of all rivers; [[synonyms:p:pravarā]]---the foremost; [[synonyms:s:saumya]]---O gentle Vidura; [[synonyms:s:siddhi]]-[[synonyms:d:dā]]---conferring perfection; [[synonyms:s:siddha]]---by persons desiring perfection; [[synonyms:s:sevitā]]---resorted to.

===== Translation =====

**Dear Vidura, the material elements of her body have melted into water and are now a flowing river, which is the most sacred of all rivers. Anyone who bathes in that river also attains perfection, and therefore all persons who desire perfection go bathe there.**
