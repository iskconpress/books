====== SB 3.11.25 ======

> मन्वन्तरेषु मनवस्तद्वंश्या ऋषयः सुराः ।
> भवन्ति चैव युगपत्सुरेशाश्चानु ये च तान् ॥२५॥

===== Text =====

> manvantareṣu manavas
> tad-vaṁśyā ṛṣayaḥ surāḥ
> bhavanti caiva yugapat
> sureśāś cānu ye ca tān

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:manu]]-[[synonyms:a:antareṣu]]---after the dissolution of each and every Manu; [[synonyms:m:manavaḥ]]---other Manus; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:v:vaṁśyāḥ]]---and their descendants; [[synonyms:ṛ:ṛṣayaḥ]]---the seven famous sages; [[synonyms:s:surāḥ]]---devotees of the Lord; [[synonyms:b:bhavanti]]---flourish; [[synonyms:c:ca]] [[synonyms:e:eva]]---also all of them; [[synonyms:y:yugapat]]---simultaneously; [[synonyms:s:sura]]-[[synonyms:ī:īśāḥ]]---demigods like Indra; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:a:anu]]---followers; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---all; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:t:tān]]---them.

===== Translation =====

**After the dissolution of each and every Manu, the next Manu comes in order, along with his descendants, who rule over the different planets; but the seven famous sages, and demigods like Indra and their followers, such as the Gandharvas, all appear simultaneously with Manu.**

===== Purport =====

There are fourteen Manus in one day of Brahmā, and each of them has different descendants.
