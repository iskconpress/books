====== SB 3.1.29 ======

> कच्चित्सुखं सात्वतवृष्णिभोज दाशार्हकाणामधिपः स आस्ते ।
> यमभ्यषिञ्चच्छतपत्रनेत्रो नृपासनाशां परिहृत्य दूरात् ॥२९॥

===== Text =====

> kaccit sukhaṁ sātvata-vṛṣṇi-bhoja-
> dāśārhakāṇām adhipaḥ sa āste
> yam abhyaṣiñcac chata-patra-netro
> nṛpāsanāśāṁ parihṛtya dūrāt

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kaccit]]---whether; [[synonyms:s:sukham]]---is all well; [[synonyms:s:sātvata]]---the Sātvata race; [[synonyms:v:vṛṣṇi]]---the Vṛṣṇi dynasty; [[synonyms:b:bhoja]]---the Bhoja dynasty; [[synonyms:d:dāśārhakāṇām]]---the Dāśārha race; [[synonyms:a:adhipaḥ]]---King Ugrasena; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---he; [[synonyms:ā:āste]]---does exist; [[synonyms:y:yam]]---whom; [[synonyms:a:abhyaṣiñcat]]---installed; [[synonyms:ś:śata]]-[[synonyms:p:patra]]-[[synonyms:n:netraḥ]]---Lord Śrī Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:n:nṛpa]]-[[synonyms:ā:āsana]]-[[synonyms:ā:āśām]]---hope of the royal throne; [[synonyms:p:parihṛtya]]---giving up; [[synonyms:d:dūrāt]]---at a distant place.

===== Translation =====

**O my friend, [tell me] whether Ugrasena, the King of the Sātvatas, Vṛṣṇis, Bhojas and Dāśārhas, is now doing well. He went far away from his kingdom, leaving aside all hopes of his royal throne, but Lord Kṛṣṇa again installed him.**
