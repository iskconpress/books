====== SB 10.13.21 ======

===== Text =====

> tat-tad-vatsān pṛthaṅ nītvā
> tat-tad-goṣṭhe niveśya saḥ
> tat-tad-ātmābhavad rājaṁs
> tat-tat-sadma praviṣṭavān

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:v:vatsān]]---the calves, which belonged to different cows; [[synonyms:p:pṛthak]]---separately; [[synonyms:n:nītvā]]---bringing; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:g:goṣṭhe]]---to their respective cow sheds; [[synonyms:n:niveśya]]---entering; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmā]]---as originally different individual souls; [[synonyms:a:abhavat]]---He expanded Himself in that way; [[synonyms:r:rājan]]---O King Parīkṣit; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:s:sadma]]---their respective houses; [[synonyms:p:praviṣṭavān]]---entered (Kṛṣṇa thus entered everywhere).

===== Translation =====

**O Mahārāja Parīkṣit, Kṛṣṇa, who had divided Himself as different calves and also as different cowherd boys, entered different cow sheds as the calves and then different homes as different boys.**

===== Purport =====

Kṛṣṇa had many, many friends, of whom Śrīdāmā, Sudāmā and Subala were prominent. Thus Kṛṣṇa Himself became Śrīdāmā, Sudāmā and Subala and entered their respective houses with their respective calves.
