~~Title: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.3.41~~

====== SB 10.3.41 ======

===== Text =====

> adṛṣṭvānyatamaṁ loke
> śīlaudārya-guṇaiḥ samam
> ahaṁ suto vām abhavaṁ
> pṛśnigarbha iti śrutaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:adṛṣṭvā]]---not finding; [[synonyms:a:anyatamam]]---anyone else; [[synonyms:l:loke]]---in this world; [[synonyms:ś:śīla]]-[[synonyms:a:audārya]]-[[synonyms:g:guṇaiḥ]]---with the transcendental qualities of good character and magnanimity; [[synonyms:s:samam]]---equal to you; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:s:sutaḥ]]---the son; [[synonyms:v:vām]]---of both of you; [[synonyms:a:abhavam]]---became; [[synonyms:p:pṛśni]]-[[synonyms:g:garbhaḥ]]---celebrated as born of Pṛśni; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:ś:śrutaḥ]]---I am known.

===== Translation =====

**Since I found no one else as highly elevated as you in simplicity and other qualities of good character, I appeared in this world as Pṛśnigarbha, or one who is celebrated as having taken birth from Pṛśni.**

===== Purport =====

In the Tretā-yuga the Lord appeared as Pṛśnigarbha. Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī Ṭhākura says, //pṛśnigarbha iti so 'yaṁ tretā-yugāvatāro lakṣyate.//
