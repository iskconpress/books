====== SB 10.8.27 ======

===== Text =====

> tatas tu bhagavān kṛṣṇo
> vayasyair vraja-bālakaiḥ
> saha-rāmo vraja-strīṇāṁ
> cikrīḍe janayan mudam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tataḥ]]---thereafter; [[synonyms:t:tu]]---but; [[synonyms:b:bhagavān]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇaḥ]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:v:vayasyaiḥ]]---with Their playmates; [[synonyms:v:vraja]]-[[synonyms:b:bālakaiḥ]]---with other small children in Vraja; [[synonyms:s:saha]]-[[synonyms:r:rāmaḥ]]---along with Balarāma; [[synonyms:v:vraja]]-[[synonyms:s:strīṇām]]---of all the ladies of Vraja; [[synonyms:c:cikrīḍe]]---played very happily; [[synonyms:j:janayan]]---awakening; [[synonyms:m:mudam]]---transcendental bliss.

===== Translation =====

**Thereafter, Lord Kṛṣṇa, along with Balarāma, began to play with the other children of the cowherd men, thus awakening the transcendental bliss of the cowherd women.**

===== Purport =====

The word //saha-rāmaḥ,// meaning "along with Balarāma," is significant in this verse. In such transcendental pastimes, Kṛṣṇa is the chief hero, and Balarāma provides additional help.
