====== SB 10.12.1 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-śuka uvāca
> kvacid vanāśāya mano dadhad vrajāt
> prātaḥ samutthāya vayasya-vatsapān
> prabodhayañ chṛṅga-raveṇa cāruṇā
> vinirgato vatsa-puraḥsaro hariḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:ś:śukaḥ]] [[synonyms:u:uvāca]]---Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; [[synonyms:k:kvacit]]---one day; [[synonyms:v:vana]]-[[synonyms:ā:āśāya]]---just to enjoy a picnic in the forest; [[synonyms:m:manaḥ]]---mind; [[synonyms:d:dadhat]]---gave attention; [[synonyms:v:vrajāt]]---and went out of Vrajabhūmi; [[synonyms:p:prātaḥ]]---early in the morning; [[synonyms:s:samutthāya]]---waking up; [[synonyms:v:vayasya]]-[[synonyms:v:vatsa]]-[[synonyms:p:pān]]---the cowherd boys and the calves; [[synonyms:p:prabodhayan]]---to get everyone to rise, waking up and informing them; [[synonyms:ś:śṛṅga]]-[[synonyms:r:raveṇa]]---by sounding the bugle made of horn; [[synonyms:c:cāruṇā]]---very beautiful; [[synonyms:v:vinirgataḥ]]---came out of Vrajabhūmi; [[synonyms:v:vatsa]]-[[synonyms:p:puraḥsaraḥ]]---keeping the respective groups of calves in front; [[synonyms:h:hariḥ]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

===== Translation =====

**Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: O King, one day Kṛṣṇa decided to take His breakfast as a picnic in the forest. Having risen early in the morning, He blew His bugle made of horn and woke all the cowherd boys and calves with its beautiful sound. Then Kṛṣṇa and the boys, keeping their respective groups of calves before them, proceeded from Vrajabhūmi to the forest.**
