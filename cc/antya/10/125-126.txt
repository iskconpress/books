====== CC Antya 10.125-126 ======

===== Text =====

> yadyapi māsekera vāsi mukuta nārikela
> amṛta-guṭikādi, pānādi sakala

> tathāpi nūtana-prāya saba dravyera svāda
> 'vāsi' visvāda nahe sei prabhura prasāda

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yadyapi]]---although; [[synonyms:m:māsekera]]---one month; [[synonyms:v:vāsi]]---remaining; [[synonyms:m:mukuta]] [[synonyms:n:nārikela]]---a very hard sweet preparation of coconut; [[synonyms:a:amṛta]]-[[synonyms:g:guṭikā]]---//amṛta-guṭikā// sweetballs; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---etc.; [[synonyms:p:pānā]]---sweet drinks; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---and so on; [[synonyms:s:sakala]]---all; [[synonyms:t:tathāpi]]---still; [[synonyms:n:nūtana]]-[[synonyms:p:prāya]]---as if fresh; [[synonyms:s:saba]] [[synonyms:d:dravyera]]---of every preparation; [[synonyms:s:svāda]]---the taste; [[synonyms:v:vāsi]]---stale; [[synonyms:v:visvāda]]---tasteless; [[synonyms:n:nahe]]---were not; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---that; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]] [[synonyms:p:prasāda]]---the mercy of the Lord.

===== Translation =====

**The hard sweets made of coconut, mukuta nārikela, the sweetballs, the many kinds of sweet drinks and all the other preparations were at least a month old, but although they were old, they had not become tasteless or stale. Indeed, they had all stayed fresh. That is the mercy of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.**
