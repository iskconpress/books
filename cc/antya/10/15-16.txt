====== CC Antya 10.15-16 ======

===== Text =====

> āmra-kāśandi, ādā-kāśandi jhāla-kāśandi nāma
> nembu-ādā āmra-koli vividha vidhāna
>
> āmsi, āma-khaṇḍa, tailāmra, āma-sattā
> yatna kari' guṇḍā kari' purāṇa sukutā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āmra]]-[[synonyms:k:kāśandi]]---//āmra-kāśandi//; [[synonyms:ā:ādā]]-[[synonyms:k:kāśandi]]---//ādā-kāśandi//; [[synonyms:j:jhāla]]-[[synonyms:k:kāśandi]]---//jhāla-kāśandi//; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---named; [[synonyms:n:nembu]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādā]]---a preparation made with lime and ginger; [[synonyms:ā:āmra]]-[[synonyms:k:koli]]---//āmra-koli//; [[synonyms:v:vividha]] [[synonyms:v:vidhāna]]---various preparations; [[synonyms:ā:āmsi]]---āmsi; [[synonyms:ā:āma]]-[[synonyms:k:khaṇḍa]]---//āma-khaṇḍa//; [[synonyms:t:tailāmra]]---mango within mustard oil; [[synonyms:ā:āma]]-[[synonyms:s:sattā]]---//āma-sattā//; [[synonyms:y:yatna]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---with great attention; [[synonyms:g:guṇḍā]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---making into a powder; [[synonyms:p:purāṇa]] [[synonyms:s:sukutā]]---dried bitter vegetables such as bitter melon.

===== Translation =====

**These are the names of some of the pickles and condiments in the bags of Rāghava Paṇḍita: āmra-kāśandi, ādā-kāśandi, jhāla-kāśandi, nembu-ādā, āmra-koli, āmsi, āma-khaṇḍa, tailāmra and āma-sattā. With great attention, Damayantī also made dried bitter vegetables into a powder.**
