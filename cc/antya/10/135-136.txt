====== CC Antya 10.135-136 ======

===== Text =====

> maricera jhāla, āra madhurāmla āra
> ādā, lavaṇa, lembu, dugdha, dadhi, khaṇḍa-sāra
>
> śāka dui-cāri, āra sukutāra jhola
> nimba-vārtākī, āra bhṛṣṭa-paṭola

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:maricera]] [[synonyms:j:jhāla]]---a pungent preparation with black pepper; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---as well as; [[synonyms:m:madhurāmla]]---a sweet-and-sour preparation; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---also; [[synonyms:ā:ādā]]---ginger; [[synonyms:l:lavaṇa]]---salted preparations; [[synonyms:l:lembu]]---lime; [[synonyms:d:dugdha]]---milk; [[synonyms:d:dadhi]]---yogurt; [[synonyms:k:khaṇḍa]]-[[synonyms:s:sāra]]---cheese; [[synonyms:ś:śāka]] [[synonyms:d:dui]]-[[synonyms:c:cāri]]---spinach of two to four kinds; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:s:sukutāra]] [[synonyms:j:jhola]]---a soup made of bitter melon; [[synonyms:n:nimba]]-[[synonyms:v:vārtākī]]---eggplant mixed with //nimba// leaves; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:b:bhṛṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:p:paṭola]]---fried //paṭola.//

===== Translation =====

**They offered pungent preparations made with black pepper, sweet-and-sour preparations, ginger, salty preparations, limes, milk, yogurt, cheese, two or four kinds of spinach, soup made with bitter melon, eggplant mixed with nimba flowers, and fried paṭola.**
