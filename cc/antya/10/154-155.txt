====== CC Antya 10.154-155 ======

===== Text =====

> gopīnāthācārya, jagadānanda, kāśīśvara
> bhagavān, rāmabhadrācārya, śaṅkara, vakreśvara
>
> madhye madhye ghara-bhāte kare nimantraṇa
> anyera nimantraṇe prasāde kauḍi dui-paṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:gopīnātha]]-[[synonyms:ā:ācārya]]---Gopīnātha Ācārya; [[synonyms:j:jagadānanda]]---Jagadānanda Paṇḍita; [[synonyms:k:kāśīśvara]]---Kāśīśvara; [[synonyms:b:bhagavān]]---Bhagavān; [[synonyms:r:rāmabhadra]]-[[synonyms:ā:ācārya]]---Rāmabhadrācārya; [[synonyms:ś:śaṅkara]]---Śaṅkara; [[synonyms:v:vakreśvara]]---Vakreśvara; [[synonyms:m:madhye]] [[synonyms:m:madhye]]---at intervals; [[synonyms:g:ghara]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāte]]---with rice at home; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:n:nimantraṇa]]---invite; [[synonyms:a:anyera]] [[synonyms:n:nimantraṇa]]---for others' invitations; [[synonyms:p:prasāde]]---//prasāda//; [[synonyms:k:kauḍi]] [[synonyms:d:dui]]-[[synonyms:p:paṇa]]---two //panas// of //conchshells// (160 conchshells).

===== Translation =====

**Gopīnātha Ācārya, Jagadānanda, Kāśīśvara, Bhagavān, Rāmabhadrācārya, Śaṅkara and Vakreśvara, who were all brāhmaṇas, extended invitations to Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and offered Him food cooked at home, whereas other devotees would pay two paṇas of small conchshells to purchase Jagannātha's prasāda and then invite the Lord.**
