====== CC Antya 10.29-30 ======

===== Text =====

> śāli-dhānyera taṇḍula-bhājā cūrṇa kariyā
> ghṛta-sikta cūrṇa kailā cini-pāka diyā
>
> karpūra, marica, lavaṅga, elāci, rasavāsa
> cūrṇa diyā nāḍu kailā parama suvāsa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śāli]]-[[synonyms:d:dhānyera]]---of rice of a fine quality; [[synonyms:t:taṇḍula]]---the grains; [[synonyms:b:bhājā]]---being fried; [[synonyms:c:cūrṇa]] [[synonyms:k:kariyā]]---making it into a powder; [[synonyms:g:ghṛta]]-[[synonyms:s:sikta]]---moistened with ghee; [[synonyms:c:cūrṇa]]---the powder; [[synonyms:k:kailā]]---made; [[synonyms:c:cini]]-[[synonyms:p:pāka]] [[synonyms:d:diyā]]---by cooking with sugar; [[synonyms:k:karpūra]]---camphor; [[synonyms:m:marica]]---black pepper; [[synonyms:l:lavaṅga]]---cloves; [[synonyms:e:elāci]]---cardamom; [[synonyms:r:rasa]]-[[synonyms:v:vāsa]]---and other spices; [[synonyms:c:cūrṇa]]---to the powder; [[synonyms:d:diyā]]---adding; [[synonyms:n:nāḍu]]---round sweetmeats; [[synonyms:k:kailā]]---made; [[synonyms:p:parama]] [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:v:vāsa]]---very palatable.

===== Translation =====

**She powdered fried grains of fine rice, moistened the powder with ghee and cooked it in a solution of sugar. Then she added camphor, black pepper, cloves, cardamom and other spices and rolled it into balls that were very palatable and aromatic.**
