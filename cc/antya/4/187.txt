====== Cc. Antya 4.187 ======

===== Text =====

> 'lālyāmedhya' lālakera candana-sama bhāya
> sanātanera klede āmāra ghṛṇā nā upajāya"

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:l:lālya]]---of the maintained child; [[synonyms:a:amedhya]]---stool and urine; [[synonyms:l:lālakera]]---of the maintainer; [[synonyms:c:candana]]-[[synonyms:s:sama]]---like sandalwood pulp; [[synonyms:b:bhāya]]---appears; [[synonyms:s:sanātanera]]---of Sanātana Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:k:klede]]---unto the moisture of the sores; [[synonyms:ā:āmāra]]---My; [[synonyms:g:ghṛṇā]]---hatred; [[synonyms:n:nā]]---not; [[synonyms:u:upajāya]]---arises.

===== Translation =====

**"The stool and urine of the maintained child appear like sandalwood pulp to the mother. Similarly, when the foul moisture oozing from the itches of Sanātana touches My body, I have no hatred for him."**
