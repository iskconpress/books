====== CC Antya 4.59 ======

===== Text =====

> na sādhayati māṁ yogo
> na sāṅkhyaṁ dharma uddhava
> na svādhyāyas tapas tyāgo
> yathā bhaktir mamorjitā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:na]]---never; [[synonyms:s:sādhayati]]---causes to remain satisfied; [[synonyms:m:mām]]---Me; [[synonyms:y:yogaḥ]]---the process of control; [[synonyms:n:na]]---nor; [[synonyms:s:sāṅkhyam]]---the process of gaining philosophical knowledge about the Absolute Truth; [[synonyms:d:dharmaḥ]]---such an occupation; [[synonyms:u:uddhava]]---My dear Uddhava; [[synonyms:n:na]]---nor; [[synonyms:s:svādhyāyaḥ]]---study of the //Vedas//; [[synonyms:t:tapaḥ]]---austerities; [[synonyms:t:tyāgaḥ]]---renunciation, acceptance of //sannyāsa,// or charity; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]---as much as; [[synonyms:b:bhaktiḥ]]---devotional service; [[synonyms:m:mama]]---unto Me; [[synonyms:ū:ūrjitā]]---developed.

===== Translation =====

**[The Supreme Personality of Godhead, Kṛṣṇa, said:] " 'My dear Uddhava, neither through aṣṭāṅga-yoga [the mystic yoga system to control the senses], nor through impersonal monism or an analytical study of the Absolute Truth, nor through study of the Vedas, nor through austerities, charity or acceptance of sannyāsa can one satisfy Me as much as by developing unalloyed devotional service unto Me.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse is from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (11.14.20).
