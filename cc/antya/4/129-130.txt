====== Cc. Antya 4.129-130 ======

===== Text =====

> "yadyapio tumi hao jagat-pāvana
> tomā-sparśe pavitra haya deva-muni-gaṇa

> tathāpi bhakta-svabhāva---maryādā-rakṣaṇa
> maryādā-pālana haya sādhura bhūṣaṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yadyapio]]---although; [[synonyms:t:tumi]]---you; [[synonyms:h:hao]]---are; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:p:pāvana]]---the deliverer of the entire universe; [[synonyms:t:tomā]]---you; [[synonyms:s:sparśe]]---by touching; [[synonyms:p:pavitra]]---purified; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---becomes; [[synonyms:d:deva]]-[[synonyms:m:muni]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---the demigods and great saintly persons; [[synonyms:t:tathāpi]]---still; [[synonyms:b:bhakta]]-[[synonyms:s:svabhāva]]---the nature of a devotee; [[synonyms:m:maryādā]]---etiquette; [[synonyms:r:rakṣaṇa]]---to protect or observe; [[synonyms:m:maryādā]] [[synonyms:p:pālana]]---to maintain etiquette; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---is; [[synonyms:s:sādhura]] [[synonyms:b:bhūṣaṇa]]---ornament of devotees.

===== Translation =====

**"My dear Sanātana, although you are the deliverer of the entire universe and although even the demigods and great saints are purified by touching you, it is the characteristic of a devotee to observe and protect the Vaiṣṇava etiquette. Maintenance of the Vaiṣṇava etiquette is the ornament of a devotee.**
