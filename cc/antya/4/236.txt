====== CC Antya 4.236 ======

===== Text =====

> ei tina-guru, āra raghunātha-dāsa
> iṅhā-sabāra caraṇa vandoṅ, yāṅra muñi 'dāsa'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]]---these; [[synonyms:t:tina]]-[[synonyms:g:guru]]---three spiritual masters; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---also; [[synonyms:r:raghunātha]]-[[synonyms:d:dāsa]]---Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:i:iṅhā]]-[[synonyms:s:sabāra]]---of all of them; [[synonyms:c:caraṇa]]---the lotus feet; [[synonyms:v:vandoṅ]]---I worship; [[synonyms:y:yāṅra]]---of whom; [[synonyms:m:muñi]]---I; [[synonyms:d:dāsa]]---the servant.

===== Translation =====

**These three-Rūpa Gosvāmī, Sanātana Gosvāmī and Jīva Gosvāmī-are my spiritual masters, and so also is Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī. I therefore offer prayers at their lotus feet, for I am their servant.**
