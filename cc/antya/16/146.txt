====== CC Antya 16.146 ======

===== Text =====

> mānasa-gaṅgā, kālindī, bhuvana-pāvanī nadī,
> kṛṣṇa yadi tāte kare snāna
> veṇura jhuṭādhara-rasa, hañā lobhe paravaśa,
> sei kāle harṣe kare pāna

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mānasa]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṅgā]]---the Ganges of the celestial world; [[synonyms:k:kālindī]]---the Yamunā; [[synonyms:b:bhuvana]]---the world; [[synonyms:p:pāvanī]]---purifying; [[synonyms:n:nadī]]---rivers; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:y:yadi]]---if; [[synonyms:t:tāte]]---in those; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:s:snāna]]---takes a bath; [[synonyms:v:veṇura]]---of the flute; [[synonyms:j:jhuṭa]]-[[synonyms:a:adhara]]-[[synonyms:r:rasa]]---remnants of the juice of the lips; [[synonyms:h:hañā]]---being; [[synonyms:l:lobhe]]---by greed; [[synonyms:p:paravaśa]]---controlled; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:k:kāle]]---at that time; [[synonyms:h:harṣe]]---in jubilation; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:p:pāna]]---drink.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'When Kṛṣṇa takes His bath in universally purifying rivers like the Yamunā and the Ganges of the celestial world, the great personalities of those rivers greedily and jubilantly drink the remnants of the nectarean juice from His lips.**
