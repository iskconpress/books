====== CC Antya 16.129 ======

===== Text =====

> śuṣka bāṅśera lāṭhikhāna, eta kare apamāna,
> ei daśā karila, gosāñi
> nā sahi' ki karite pāri, tāhe rahi mauna dhari',
> corāra māke ḍāki' kāndite nāi

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śuṣka]]---dry; [[synonyms:b:bāṅśera]]---of bamboo; [[synonyms:l:lāṭhi]]-[[synonyms:k:khāna]]---a stick; [[synonyms:e:eta]]---this; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:a:apamāna]]---insults; [[synonyms:e:ei]]---this; [[synonyms:d:daśā]]---condition; [[synonyms:k:karila]]---made; [[synonyms:g:gosāñi]]---the master; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:s:sahi']]---not tolerating; [[synonyms:k:ki]]---what; [[synonyms:k:karite]] [[synonyms:p:pāri]]---can we do; [[synonyms:t:tāhe]]---at that time; [[synonyms:r:rahi]]---we remain; [[synonyms:m:mauna]] [[synonyms:d:dhari']]---keeping silent; [[synonyms:c:corāra]]---of a thief; [[synonyms:m:māke]]---for the mother; [[synonyms:ḍ:ḍāki']]---calling; [[synonyms:k:kāndite]]---to cry; [[synonyms:n:nāi]]---is not possible.

===== Translation =====

**"This flute is nothing but a dry stick of bamboo, but it becomes our master and insults us in so many ways that it forces us into a predicament. What can we do but tolerate it? The mother of a thief cannot cry loudly for justice when the thief is punished. Therefore we simply remain silent.**
