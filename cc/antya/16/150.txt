====== CC Antya 16.150 ======

===== Text =====

> eteka pralāpa kari', premāveśe gaurahari,
> saṅge lañā svarūpa-rāma-rāya
> kabhu nāce, kabhu gāya, bhāvāveśe mūrcchā yāya,
> ei-rūpe rātri-dina yāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:eteka]]---so much; [[synonyms:p:pralāpa]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---talking like a crazy man; [[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśe]]---in ecstatic love; [[synonyms:g:gaurahari]]---Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:s:saṅge]] [[synonyms:l:lañā]]---taking with Him; [[synonyms:s:svarūpa]]-[[synonyms:r:rāma]]-[[synonyms:r:rāya]]---Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī and Rāmānanda Rāya; [[synonyms:k:kabhu]] [[synonyms:n:nāce]]---sometimes dances; [[synonyms:k:kabhu]] [[synonyms:g:gāya]]---sometimes sings; [[synonyms:b:bhāva]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśe]]---in ecstatic love; [[synonyms:m:mūrcchā]] [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---becomes unconscious; [[synonyms:e:ei]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpe]]---in this way; [[synonyms:r:rātri]]-[[synonyms:d:dina]]---the whole night and day; [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---passes.

===== Translation =====

**While thus speaking like a madman, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu became full of ecstatic emotion. In the company of His two friends, Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī and Rāmānanda Rāya, He sometimes danced, sometimes sang and sometimes became unconscious in ecstatic love. Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu passed His days and nights in this way.**
