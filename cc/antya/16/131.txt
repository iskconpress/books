====== CC Antya 16.131 ======

===== Text =====

> se phelāra eka lava, nā pāya devatā saba,
> e dambhe kebā pātiyāya?
> bahu-janma puṇya kare, tabe 'sukṛti' nāma dhare,
> se 'sukṛte' tāra lava pāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:se]] [[synonyms:p:phelāra]]---of those remnants; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:l:lava]]---small particle; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:p:pāya]]---do not get; [[synonyms:d:devatā]]---the demigods; [[synonyms:s:saba]]---all; [[synonyms:e:e]] [[synonyms:d:dambhe]]---this pride; [[synonyms:k:kebā]]---who; [[synonyms:p:pātiyāya]]---can believe; [[synonyms:b:bahu]]-[[synonyms:j:janma]]---for many births; [[synonyms:p:puṇya]] [[synonyms:k:kare]]---acts piously; [[synonyms:t:tabe]]---then; [[synonyms:s:sukṛti]]---one who performs pious activities; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---the name; [[synonyms:d:dhare]]---bears; [[synonyms:s:se]]---those; [[synonyms:s:sukṛte]]---by pious activities; [[synonyms:t:tāra]]---of that; [[synonyms:l:lava]]---a fraction; [[synonyms:p:pāya]]---one can get.

===== Translation =====

**"Even after much prayer, the demigods themselves cannot obtain even a small portion of the remnants of such food. Just imagine the pride of those remnants! Only a person who has acted piously for many, many births and has thus become a devotee can obtain the remnants of such food.**
