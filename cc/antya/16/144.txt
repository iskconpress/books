====== CC Antya 16.144 ======

===== Text =====

> hena kṛṣṇādhara-sudhā, ye kaila amṛta mudhā,
> yāra āśāya gopī dhare prāṇa
> ei veṇu ayogya ati, sthāvara 'puruṣa-jāti',
> sei sudhā sadā kare pāna

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:h:hena]]---such; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:adhara]]---of Kṛṣṇa's lips; [[synonyms:s:sudhā]]---nectar; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---which; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---made; [[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---nectar; [[synonyms:m:mudhā]]---surpassed; [[synonyms:y:yāra]] [[synonyms:ā:āśāya]]---by hoping for which; [[synonyms:g:gopī]]---the //gopīs//; [[synonyms:d:dhare]] [[synonyms:p:prāṇa]]---continue to live; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:v:veṇu]]---this flute; [[synonyms:a:ayogya]]---unfit; [[synonyms:a:ati]]---completely; [[synonyms:s:sthāvara]]---dead; [[synonyms:p:puruṣa]]-[[synonyms:j:jāti]]---belonging to the male class; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:s:sudhā]]---that nectar; [[synonyms:s:sadā]]---always; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:p:pāna]]---drinks.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'This flute is utterly unfit because it is merely a dead bamboo stick. Moreover, it belongs to the male sex. Yet this flute is always drinking the nectar of Kṛṣṇa's lips, which surpasses nectarean sweetness of every description. Only in hope of that nectar do the gopīs continue to live.**
