====== Cc. Antya 16.151 ======

===== Text =====

> svarūpa, rūpa, sanātana, raghunāthera śrī-caraṇa,
> śire dhari' kari yāra āśa
> caitanya-caritāmṛta, amṛta haite parāmṛta,
> gāya dīna-hīna kṛṣṇadāsa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:svarūpa]]---Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:s:sanātana]]---Sanātana Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:r:raghunāthera]]---of Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:c:caraṇa]]---the lotus feet; [[synonyms:ś:śire]]---on the head; [[synonyms:d:dhari']]---taking; [[synonyms:k:kari]] [[synonyms:y:yāra]] [[synonyms:ā:āśa]]---hoping for their mercy; [[synonyms:c:caitanya]]-[[synonyms:c:caritāmṛta]]---the book named //Caitanya-caritāmṛta//; [[synonyms:a:amṛta]] [[synonyms:h:haite]]---than nectar; [[synonyms:p:para]]-[[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---more nectarean; [[synonyms:g:gāya]]---chants; [[synonyms:d:dīna]]-[[synonyms:h:hīna]]---the most wretched; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇadāsa]]---Kṛṣṇadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī.

===== Translation =====

**Expecting the mercy of Svarūpa, Rūpa, Sanātana and Raghunātha dāsa, and taking their lotus feet on my head, I, the most fallen Kṛṣṇadāsa, continue chanting the epic Śrī Caitanya-caritāmṛta, which is sweeter than the nectar of transcendental bliss.**


//Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports to Śrī Caitanya-caritāmṛta, Antya-līlā,  Sixteenth Chapter, describing the nectar flowing from Śrī Kṛṣṇa's lotus lips.//
