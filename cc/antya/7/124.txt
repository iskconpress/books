====== CC Antya 7.124 ======

===== Text =====

> āmāra 'hita' karena,---iho āmi māni 'duḥkha'
> kṛṣṇera upare kaila yena indra mahā-mūrkha"

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āmāra]]---my; [[synonyms:h:hita]]---benefit; [[synonyms:k:karena]]---He is doing; [[synonyms:i:iho]]---this; [[synonyms:ā:āmi]]---I; [[synonyms:m:māni]]---consider; [[synonyms:d:duḥkha]]---unhappiness; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇeraupare]]---upon Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---did; [[synonyms:y:yena]]---as; [[synonyms:i:indra]]---Indra; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:m:mūrkha]]---the great fool.

===== Translation =====

**"He is actually acting for my benefit, although I interpret His actions as insults. This is exactly like the incident in which Lord Kṛṣṇa cut down Indra, the great, puffed-up fool, to correct him."**
