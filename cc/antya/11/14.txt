====== CC Antya 11.14 ======

===== Text =====

> dine dine bāḍe vikāra, rātrye atiśaya
> cintā, udvega, pralāpādi yata śāstre kaya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:dine]] [[synonyms:d:dine]]---day after day; [[synonyms:b:bāḍe]]---increase; [[synonyms:v:vikāra]]---transformations; [[synonyms:r:rātrye]] [[synonyms:a:atiśaya]]---especially at night; [[synonyms:c:cintā]]---anxiety; [[synonyms:u:udvega]]---agitation; [[synonyms:p:pralāpa]]---talking like a madman; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---and so on; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---as many as; [[synonyms:ś:śāstre]] [[synonyms:k:kaya]]---are mentioned in the //śāstras.//

===== Translation =====

**Day after day the symptoms increased, and at night they increased even more. All these symptoms, such as transcendental anxiety, agitation, and talking like a madman, were present, just as they are described in the śāstras.**
