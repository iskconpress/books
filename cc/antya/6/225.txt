====== CC Antya 6.225 ======

===== Text =====

> vairāgī hañā kare jihvāra lālasa
> paramārtha yāya, āra haya rasera vaśa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vairāgī]] [[synonyms:h:hañā]]---being in the renounced order; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:j:jihvāra]]---of the tongue; [[synonyms:l:lālasa]]---lust; [[synonyms:p:parama]]-[[synonyms:a:artha]]---the goal of life; [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---goes; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---becomes; [[synonyms:r:rasera]] [[synonyms:v:vaśa]]---dependent on taste.

===== Translation =====

**"If a renunciant is eager for his tongue to taste different foods, his spiritual life will be lost, and he will be subservient to the tastes of his tongue.**
