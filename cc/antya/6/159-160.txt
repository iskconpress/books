====== Cc. Antya 6.159-160 ======

===== Text =====

> ei-mata cintite daive eka-dine
> bāhire devī-maṇḍape kariyāchena śayane
>
> daṇḍa-cāri rātri yabe āche avaśeṣa
> yadunandana-ācārya tabe karilā praveśa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]]-[[synonyms:m:mata]]---in this way; [[synonyms:c:cintite]]---while he was thinking; [[synonyms:d:daive]]---by chance; [[synonyms:e:eka]] [[synonyms:d:dine]]---one day; [[synonyms:b:bāhire]]---outside the house; [[synonyms:d:devī]]-[[synonyms:m:maṇḍape]]---on the Durgā platform; [[synonyms:k:kariyāchena]] [[synonyms:ś:śayane]]---was sleeping; [[synonyms:d:daṇḍa]]-[[synonyms:c:cāri]]---four //daṇḍas// (ninety-six minutes); [[synonyms:r:rātri]]---night; [[synonyms:y:yabe]]---when; [[synonyms:ā:āche]] [[synonyms:a:avaśeṣa]]---there remained; [[synonyms:y:yadunandana]]-[[synonyms:ā:ācārya]]---the priest of the name Yadunandana Ācārya; [[synonyms:t:tabe]]---then; [[synonyms:k:karilā]] [[synonyms:p:praveśa]]---entered.

===== Translation =====

**Thus Raghunātha dāsa thought deeply about how to escape, and one night while he was sleeping on the Durgā-maṇḍapa, the priest Yadunandana Ācārya entered the house when only four daṇḍas remained until the end of the night.**
