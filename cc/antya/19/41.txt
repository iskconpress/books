====== CC Antya 19.41 ======

===== Text =====

> jiniyā tamāla-dyuti, indranīla-sama kānti,
> se kāntite jagat mātāya
> śṛṅgāra-rasa-sāra chāni', tāte candra-jyotsnā sāni',
> jāni vidhi niramilā tāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:j:jiniyā]]---being victorious over; [[synonyms:t:tamāla]]-[[synonyms:d:dyuti]]---the luster of the //tamāla// tree; [[synonyms:i:indra]]-[[synonyms:n:nīla]]---the gem known as //indranīla//; [[synonyms:s:sama]] [[synonyms:k:kānti]]---the luster like; [[synonyms:s:se]] [[synonyms:k:kāntite]]---by that luster; [[synonyms:j:jagat]] [[synonyms:m:mātāya]]---the whole world becomes maddened; [[synonyms:ś:śrṅgāra]]-[[synonyms:r:rasa]]---of the mellow of conjugal love; [[synonyms:s:sāra]]---essence; [[synonyms:c:chāni']]---filtering; [[synonyms:t:tāte]]---in that; [[synonyms:c:candra]]-[[synonyms:j:jyotsnā]]---the illumination of the full moon; [[synonyms:s:sāni']]---mixing; [[synonyms:j:jāni]]---I know; [[synonyms:v:vidhi]]---Providence; [[synonyms:n:niramilā]]---made very clear; [[synonyms:t:tāya]]---that.

===== Translation =====

**"Kṛṣṇa's bodily luster shines like the indranīla gem and surpasses the luster of the tamāla tree. The luster of His body drives the entire world mad because Providence has made it transparent by refining the essence of the mellow of conjugal love and mixing it with moonshine.**
