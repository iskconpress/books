====== CC Antya 19.44 ======

===== Text =====

> 'ye-jana jīte nāhi cāya, tāre kene jīyāya',
> vidhi-prati uṭhe krodha-śoka
> vidhire kare bhartsana, kṛṣṇe dena olāhana,
> paḍi' bhāgavatera eka śloka

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:ye]]-[[synonyms:j:jana]]---that person who; [[synonyms:j:jīte]]---to live; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]] [[synonyms:c:cāya]]---does not want; [[synonyms:t:tāre]]---him; [[synonyms:k:kene]]---why; [[synonyms:j:jīyāya]]---he makes life continue; [[synonyms:v:vidhi]]-[[synonyms:p:prati]]---towards Providence; [[synonyms:u:uṭhe]]---awakens; [[synonyms:k:krodha]]-[[synonyms:ś:śoka]]---anger and lamentation; [[synonyms:v:vidhire]]---unto Providence; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:b:bhartsana]]---chastisement; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇe]]---unto Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:d:dena]]---gives; [[synonyms:o:olāhana]]---accusation; [[synonyms:p:paḍi']]---reading; [[synonyms:b:bhāgavatera]]---of //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam//; [[synonyms:e:eka]] [[synonyms:ś:śloka]]---one verse.

===== Translation =====

**"Why does Providence continue the life of one who does not wish to liver? This thought aroused anger and lamentation. Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu then**


**read a verse from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam that chastises Providence and makes an accusation against Kṛṣṇa.**
