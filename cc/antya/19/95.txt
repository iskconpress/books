====== CC Antya 19.95 ======

===== Text =====

> hema-kīlita candana, tāhā kari' gharṣaṇa,
> tāhe aguru, kuṅkuma, kastūrī
> karpūra-sane carcā aṅge, pūrva aṅgera gandha saṅge,
> mili' tāre yena kaila curi

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:h:hema]]---with gold; [[synonyms:k:kīlita]]---bedecked; [[synonyms:c:candana]]---white sandalwood; [[synonyms:t:tāhā]]---that; [[synonyms:k:kari']]---doing; [[synonyms:g:gharṣaṇa]]---rubbing; [[synonyms:t:tāhe]]---in that; [[synonyms:a:aguru]]---the //aguru// flavor; [[synonyms:k:kuṅkuma]]---//kuṅkuma//; [[synonyms:k:kastūrī]]---and musk; [[synonyms:k:karpūra]]---camphor; [[synonyms:s:sane]]---with; [[synonyms:c:carcā]]---smearing; [[synonyms:a:aṅge]]---on the body; [[synonyms:p:pūrva]]---previous; [[synonyms:a:aṅgera]]---of the body; [[synonyms:g:gandha]]---scent; [[synonyms:s:saṅge]]---with; [[synonyms:m:mili']]---combining; [[synonyms:t:tāre]]---that; [[synonyms:y:yena]]---as if; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---did; [[synonyms:c:curi]]---stealing or covering.

===== Translation =====

**"When sandalwood pulp with aguru, kuṅkuma and musk is mixed with camphor and spread on Kṛṣṇa's body, it combines with Kṛṣṇa's own original bodily perfume and seems to cover it.**

===== Purport =====

In another version, the last line of this verse reads, kāmadevera mana kaila curi. This means "the scent of all these substances mixes with the previous scent of Kṛṣṇa's body and steals away the mind of Cupid."
