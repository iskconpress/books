====== CC Antya 19.50 ======

===== Text =====

> āpanāra karma-doṣa, tore kibā kari roṣa,
> toya-moya sambandha vidūra
> ye āmāra prāṇa-nātha, ekatra rahi yāṅra sātha,
> sei kṛṣṇa ha-ilā niṭhura!

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āpanāra]] [[synonyms:k:karma]]-[[synonyms:d:doṣa]]---it is the result of My own fate; [[synonyms:t:tore]]---unto you; [[synonyms:k:kibā]]---what; [[synonyms:k:kari]] [[synonyms:r:roṣa]]---I give accusations; [[synonyms:t:toya]]-[[synonyms:m:moya]]---between you and Me; [[synonyms:s:sam]]-[[synonyms:b:bandha]]---the relationship; [[synonyms:v:vidūra]]---very far; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---the one who is; [[synonyms:ā:āmāra]]---My; [[synonyms:p:prāṇa]]-[[synonyms:n:nātha]]---life and soul; [[synonyms:e:ekatra]]---together; [[synonyms:r:rahi]]---We remain; [[synonyms:y:yāṅra]] [[synonyms:s:sātha]]---with whom; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---that Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:h:ha]]-[[synonyms:i:ilā]] [[synonyms:n:niṭhura]]---has become so cruel.

===== Translation =====

**"But this is the fault of My own destiny. Why should I needlessly accuse you? There is no intimate relationship between you and Me. Kṛṣṇa, however, is My life and soul. It is We who live together, and it is He who has become so cruel.**
