====== CC Antya 19.49 ======

===== Text =====

> 'akrūra kare tomāra doṣa, āmāya kene kara roṣa',
> ihā yadi kaha 'durācāra'
> tui akrūra-mūrti dhari', kṛṣṇa nili curi kari',
> anyera nahe aiche vyavahāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:akrūra]]---Akrūra; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:t:tomāra]] [[synonyms:d:doṣa]]---fault to you; [[synonyms:ā:āmāya]]---unto me; [[synonyms:k:kene]]---why; [[synonyms:k:kara]]---you do; [[synonyms:r:roṣa]]---anger; [[synonyms:i:ihā]]---this; [[synonyms:y:yadi]]---if; [[synonyms:k:kaha]]---you say; [[synonyms:d:durācāra]]---O misbehaved (Providence); [[synonyms:t:tui]]---you; [[synonyms:a:akrūra]]-[[synonyms:m:mūrti]] [[synonyms:d:dhari']]---taking the form of Akrūra; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:n:nili]]---you have taken; [[synonyms:c:curi]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---stealing; [[synonyms:a:anyera]]---of others; [[synonyms:n:nahe]]---there is not; [[synonyms:a:aiche]]---this type of; [[synonyms:v:vyavahāra]]---behavior.

===== Translation =====

**"O misbehaved Providence! If you reply to us, 'Akrūra is actually at fault; why are you angry with me?' then I say to you, 'Providence, you have taken the form of Akrūra and have stolen Kṛṣṇa away. No one else would behave like this.'**
