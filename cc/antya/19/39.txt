====== Cc. Antya 19.39 ======

===== Text =====

> kāhāṅ se cūḍāra ṭhāma, śikhi-piñchera uḍāna,
> nava-meghe yena indra-dhanu
> pītāmbara---taḍid-dyuti, muktā-mālā---baka-pāṅti,
> navāmbuda jini' śyāma-tanu

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:s:se]]---that; [[synonyms:c:cūḍāra]] [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭhāma]]---beauty of the helmet; [[synonyms:ś:śikhi]]-[[synonyms:p:piñchera]] [[synonyms:u:uḍāna]]---upon which there is a peacock feather; [[synonyms:n:nava]]-[[synonyms:m:meghe]]---in a new cloud; [[synonyms:y:yena]]---just like; [[synonyms:i:indra]]-[[synonyms:d:dhanu]]---the rainbow; [[synonyms:p:pīta]]-[[synonyms:a:ambara]]---yellow garments; [[synonyms:t:taḍit]]-[[synonyms:d:dyuti]]---like the illumination of lightning; [[synonyms:m:muktā]]-[[synonyms:m:mālā]]---pearl necklace; [[synonyms:b:baka]]-[[synonyms:p:pāṅti]]---like rows of ducks; [[synonyms:n:nava]]-[[synonyms:a:ambuda]]---a new rain cloud; [[synonyms:j:jini']]---conquering; [[synonyms:ś:śyāma]]-[[synonyms:t:tanu]]---the blackish body.

===== Translation =====

**"My dear friend, where is that beautiful helmet with a peacock feather upon it like a rainbow upon a new cloud? Where are those yellow garments, shining like lightning? And where is that necklace of pearls that resemble flocks of ducks flying in the sky? The blackish body of Kṛṣṇa triumphs over the new blackish rain cloud.**
