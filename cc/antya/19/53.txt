====== CC Antya 19.53 ======

===== Text =====

> ei-mata gaura-rāya, viṣāde kare hāya hāya,
> 'hā hā kṛṣṇa, tumi gelā kati?'
> gopī-bhāva hṛdaye, tāra vākye vilāpaye,
> 'govinda dāmodara mādhaveti'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]]-[[synonyms:m:mata]]---in this way; [[synonyms:g:gaura]]-[[synonyms:r:rāya]]---Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:v:viṣāde]]---in lamentation due to separation; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:h:hāya]] [[synonyms:h:hāya]]---always says "alas, alas"; [[synonyms:h:hā]] [[synonyms:h:hā]] [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---oh, oh Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:t:tumi]] [[synonyms:g:gelā]] [[synonyms:k:kati]]---where have You gone; [[synonyms:g:gopī]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāva]] [[synonyms:h:hṛdaye]]---with the ecstatic love of the //gopīs// in His heart; [[synonyms:t:tāra]] [[synonyms:v:vākye]]---in their words; [[synonyms:v:vilāpaye]]---He laments; [[synonyms:g:govinda]] [[synonyms:d:dāmodara]] [[synonyms:m:mādhava]]---O Govinda, O Dāmodara, O Mādhava; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus.

===== Translation =====

**In this way, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu lamented in the mood of separation, "Alas, alas! O Kṛṣṇa, where have You gone?" Feeling in His heart the ecstatic emotions of the gopīs, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu agonized in their words, saying, "O Govinda! O Dāmodara! O Mādhava!"**
