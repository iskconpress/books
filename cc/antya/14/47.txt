====== CC Antya 14.47 ======

===== Text =====

> daśendriye śiṣya kari', 'mahā-bāula' nāma dhari',
> śiṣya lañā karila gamana
> mora deha sva-sadana, viṣaya-bhoga mahā-dhana,
> saba chāḍi' gelā vṛndāvana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:daśa]]-[[synonyms:i:indriye]]---the ten senses; [[synonyms:ś:śiṣya]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---making disciples; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:b:bāula]]---of a great mendicant; [[synonyms:n:nāma]] [[synonyms:d:dhari']]---taking the name; [[synonyms:ś:śiṣya]] [[synonyms:l:lañā]]---taking disciples; [[synonyms:k:karila]] [[synonyms:g:gamana]]---has gone; [[synonyms:m:mora]]---My; [[synonyms:d:deha]]---body; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:s:sadana]]---own home; [[synonyms:v:viṣaya]]-[[synonyms:b:bhoga]]---material enjoyment; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:d:dhana]]---great treasure; [[synonyms:s:saba]] [[synonyms:c:chāḍi']]---leaving aside everything; [[synonyms:g:gelā]] [[synonyms:v:vṛndāvana]]---has gone to Vṛndāvana.

===== Translation =====

**"The mystic yogī of My mind has assumed the name Mahābāula and made disciples of My ten senses. Thus My mind has gone to Vṛndāvana, leaving aside the home of My body and the great treasure of material enjoyment.**

===== Purport =====

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu compares His mind to one of the mystic //yogīs// known as //bāula,// who makes at least ten disciples.
