====== Cc. Antya 14.43 ======

===== Text =====

> "śuna, bāndhava, kṛṣṇera mādhurī
> yāra lobhe mora mana, chāḍi' loka-veda-dharma,
> yogī hañā ha-ila bhikhārī

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śuna]]---please hear; [[synonyms:b:bāndhava]]---My friends; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇera]] [[synonyms:m:mādhurī]]---the sweetness of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:y:yāra]]---for which; [[synonyms:l:lobhe]]---by the greed; [[synonyms:m:mora]] [[synonyms:m:mana]]---My mind; [[synonyms:c:chāḍi']]---giving up; [[synonyms:l:loka]]-[[synonyms:v:veda]]-[[synonyms:d:dharma]]---social and Vedic religious principles; [[synonyms:y:yogī]] [[synonyms:h:hañā]]---having become a //yogī//; [[synonyms:h:ha]]-[[synonyms:i:ila]] [[synonyms:b:bhikhārī]]---has become a beggar.

===== Translation =====

**"My dear friends," He said, "please hear of Kṛṣṇa's sweetness. Because of a great desire for that sweetness, My mind has given up all social and Vedic religious principles and taken to the profession of begging, exactly like a mystic yogī.**
