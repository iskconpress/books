====== CC Antya 14.65-66 ======

===== Text =====

> eka eka hasta-pāda---dīrgha tina tina-hāta
> asthi-granthi bhinna, carma āche mātra tāta
>
> hasta, pāda, grīvā, kaṭi, asthi sandhi yata
> eka eka vitasti bhinna hañāche tata

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:eka]] [[synonyms:e:eka]]---each one; [[synonyms:h:hasta]]-[[synonyms:p:pāda]]---arms and legs; [[synonyms:d:dīrgha]]---prolonged; [[synonyms:t:tina]] [[synonyms:t:tina]]-[[synonyms:h:hāta]]---three cubits; [[synonyms:a:asthi]]-[[synonyms:g:granthi]]---joints of the bones; [[synonyms:b:bhinna]]---separated; [[synonyms:c:carma]]---skin; [[synonyms:ā:āche]]---there is; [[synonyms:m:mātra]]---only; [[synonyms:t:tāta]]---a slight temperature indicating life; [[synonyms:h:hasta]]---arms; [[synonyms:p:pāda]]---legs; [[synonyms:g:grīvā]]---neck; [[synonyms:k:kaṭi]]---waist; [[synonyms:a:asthi]]---bones; [[synonyms:s:sandhi]]---joints; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---as many; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:v:vitasti]]---about six inches; [[synonyms:b:bhinna]]---separated; [[synonyms:h:hañāche]]---were; [[synonyms:t:tata]]---so many.

===== Translation =====

**Each of His arms and legs had become three cubits long; only skin connected the separated joints. The Lord's body temperature, indicating life, was very low. All the joints in His arms, legs, neck and waist were separated by at least six inches.**
