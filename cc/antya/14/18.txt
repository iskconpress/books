====== CC Antya 14.18 ======

===== Text =====

> tribhaṅga-sundara-deha, muralī-vadana
> pītāmbara, vana-mālā, madana-mohana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:b:bhaṅga]]---curved in three places; [[synonyms:s:sundara]]---beautiful; [[synonyms:d:deha]]---body; [[synonyms:m:muralī]]-[[synonyms:v:vadana]]---with a flute to the mouth; [[synonyms:p:pīta]]-[[synonyms:a:ambara]]---with yellow garments; [[synonyms:v:vana]]-[[synonyms:m:mālā]]---forest flower garlands; [[synonyms:m:madana]]-[[synonyms:m:mohana]]---enchanting Cupid.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu saw Lord Kṛṣṇa standing with His beautiful body curved in three places, holding His flute to His lips. Wearing yellow garments and garlands of forest flowers, He was enchanting even to Cupid.**
