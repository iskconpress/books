====== Cc. Antya 14.48 ======

===== Text =====

> vṛndāvane prajā-gaṇa, yata sthāvara-jaṅgama,
> vṛkṣa-latā gṛhastha-āśrame
> tāra ghare bhikṣāṭana, phala-mūla-patrāśana,
> ei vṛtti kare śiṣya-sane

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vṛndāvane]]---in Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:p:prajā]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---the citizens; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---all; [[synonyms:s:sthāvara]]-[[synonyms:j:jaṅgama]]---nonmoving and moving; [[synonyms:v:vṛkṣa]]-[[synonyms:l:latā]]---the trees and creepers; [[synonyms:g:gṛhastha]]-[[synonyms:ā:āśrame]]---in household life; [[synonyms:t:tāra]] [[synonyms:g:ghare]]---at his house; [[synonyms:b:bhikṣā]]-[[synonyms:ā:āṭana]]---begging from door to door; [[synonyms:p:phala]]-[[synonyms:m:mūla]]-[[synonyms:p:patra]]---fruits, roots and leaves; [[synonyms:a:aśana]]---eating; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:v:vṛtti]]---this business; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---performs; [[synonyms:ś:śiṣya]]-[[synonyms:s:sane]]---with the disciples.

===== Translation =====

**"In Vṛndāvana, he goes from door to door begging alms with all his disciples. He begs from both the moving and the inert inhabitants---the citizens, the trees and the creepers. In this way he lives on fruits, roots and leaves.**
