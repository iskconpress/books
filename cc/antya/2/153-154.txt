====== CC Antya 2.153-154 ======

===== Text =====

> eka-dina jagadānanda, svarūpa, govinda
> kāśīśvara, śaṅkara, dāmodara, mukunda
>
> samudra-snāne gelā sabe, śune katho dūre
> haridāsa gāyena, yena ḍāki' kaṇṭha-svare

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:d:dina]]---one day; [[synonyms:j:jagadānanda]]---Jagadānanda; [[synonyms:s:svarūpa]]---Svarūpa; [[synonyms:g:govinda]]---Govinda; [[synonyms:k:kāśīśvara]]---Kāśīśvara; [[synonyms:ś:śaṅkara]]---Śaṅkara; [[synonyms:d:dāmodara]]---Dāmodara; [[synonyms:m:mukunda]]---Mukunda; [[synonyms:s:samudra]]-[[synonyms:s:snāne]]---bathing in the sea; [[synonyms:g:gelā]]---went; [[synonyms:s:sabe]]---all of them; [[synonyms:ś:śune]]---could hear; [[synonyms:k:katho]] [[synonyms:d:dūre]]---from a distant place; [[synonyms:h:haridāsa]] [[synonyms:g:gāyena]]---Junior Haridāsa was singing; [[synonyms:y:yena]]---as if; [[synonyms:ḍ:ḍāki']]---calling; [[synonyms:k:kaṇṭha]]-[[synonyms:s:svare]]---in his original voice.

===== Translation =====

**One day Jagadānanda, Svarūpa, Govinda, Kāśīśvara, Śaṅkara, Dāmodara and Mukunda all went to bathe in the sea. They could hear Haridāsa singing from a distant place as if calling them in his original voice.**
