====== Cc. Antya 2.48-49 ======

===== Text =====

> ācambite nṛsiṁhānanda tāhāṅi āilā
> duṅhe tāṅre mili' tabe sthāne vasāilā

> duṅhe duḥkhī dekhi' tabe kahe nṛsiṁhānanda
> 'tomā duṅhākāre kene dekhi nirānanda?'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:ācambite]]---all of a sudden; [[synonyms:n:nṛsiṁhānanda]]---Nṛsiṁhānanda; [[synonyms:t:tāhāṅi]] [[synonyms:ā:āilā]]---came there; [[synonyms:d:duṅhe]]---Śivānanda and Jagadānanda; [[synonyms:t:tāṅre]]---him; [[synonyms:m:mili']]---meeting; [[synonyms:t:tabe]]---then; [[synonyms:s:sthāne]] [[synonyms:v:vasāilā]]---caused to sit; [[synonyms:d:duṅhe]]---both; [[synonyms:d:duḥkhī]]---unhappy; [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing; [[synonyms:t:tabe]]---then; [[synonyms:k:kahe]] [[synonyms:n:nṛsiṁhānanda]]---Nṛsiṁhānanda began to speak; [[synonyms:t:tomā]] [[synonyms:d:duṅhākāre]]---both of you; [[synonyms:k:kene]]---why; [[synonyms:d:dekhi]]---I see; [[synonyms:n:nirānanda]]---unhappy.

===== Translation =====

**Suddenly Nṛsiṁhānanda arrived, and Jagadānanda and Śivānanda arranged for him to sit near them. Seeing them both so unhappy, Nṛsiṁhānanda inquired, "Why do I see that you are both despondent?"**
