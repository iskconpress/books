~~Title:Caitanya-caritāmṛta, Antya 2.24-25~~

====== CC Antya 2.24-25 ======

===== Text =====

> "āpane bolāna more, ihā yadi jāni
> āmāra iṣṭa-mantra jāni' kahena āpani
>
> tabe jāni, iṅhāte haya caitanya-āveśe"
> eta cinti' śivānanda rahilā dūra-deśe

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āpane]]---personally; [[synonyms:b:bolāna]]---calls; [[synonyms:m:more]]---me; [[synonyms:i:ihā]]---this; [[synonyms:y:yadi]]---if; [[synonyms:j:jāni]]---I understand; [[synonyms:ā:āmāra]]---my; [[synonyms:i:iṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:m:mantra]]---worshipable //mantra//; [[synonyms:j:jāni']]---knowing; [[synonyms:k:kahena]] [[synonyms:ā:āpani]]---he says himself; [[synonyms:t:tabe]] [[synonyms:j:jāni]]---then I shall understand; [[synonyms:i:iṅhāte]]---in him; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---there is; [[synonyms:c:caitanya]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśe]]---being possessed by Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:e:eta]] [[synonyms:c:cinti']]---thinking this; [[synonyms:ś:śivānanda]]---Śivānanda Sena; [[synonyms:r:rahilā]]---remained; [[synonyms:d:dūra]]-[[synonyms:d:deśe]]---a little far off.

===== Translation =====

**"If Nakula Brahmacārī personally calls me and knows my worshipable mantra, then I shall understand that he is inspired by the presence of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu." Thinking in this way, he stayed some distance apart.**
