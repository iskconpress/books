====== Cc. Antya 20.62 ======

===== Text =====

> vrajera viśuddha-prema,---yena jāmbū-nada hema,
> ātma-sukhera yāhāṅ nāhi gandha
> se prema jānā'te loke, prabhu kailā ei śloke,
> pade kailā arthera nirbandha

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vrajera]]---of Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:v:viśuddha]]-[[synonyms:p:prema]]---the pure love of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:y:yena]]---like; [[synonyms:j:jāmbū]]-[[synonyms:n:nada]] [[synonyms:h:hema]]---the golden particles found in the Jāmbū River; [[synonyms:ā:ātma]]-[[synonyms:s:sukhera]]---of personal sense gratification; [[synonyms:y:yāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]] [[synonyms:g:gandha]]---there is not even a scent; [[synonyms:s:se]] [[synonyms:p:prema]]---that love of Godhead; [[synonyms:j:jānā'te]] [[synonyms:l:loke]]---to advertise among the people; [[synonyms:p:prabhu]]---Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:k:kailā]]---has written; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:ś:śloke]]---this verse; [[synonyms:p:pade]]---in different steps; [[synonyms:k:kailā]] [[synonyms:a:arthera]] [[synonyms:n:nirbandha]]---has clarified the real meaning.

===== Translation =====

**The pure devotional service in Vṛndāvana is like the golden particles in the River Jāmbū. In Vṛndāvana there is not a trace of personal sense gratification. It is to advertise such pure love in this material world that Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu has written the previous verse and explained its meaning.**
