====== Cc. Antya 20.64 ======

===== Text =====

> pūrve aṣṭa-śloka kari' loke śikṣā dilā
> sei aṣṭa-ślokera artha āpane āsvādilā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:pūrve]]---formerly; [[synonyms:a:aṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:ś:śloka]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---composing eight verses; [[synonyms:l:loke]] [[synonyms:ś:śikṣā]] [[synonyms:d:dilā]]---gave instruction to the people in general; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---those; [[synonyms:a:aṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:ś:ślokera]]---of the eight stanzas; [[synonyms:a:artha]]---the meaning; [[synonyms:ā:āpane]] [[synonyms:ā:āsvādilā]]---personally tasted.

===== Translation =====

**The Lord had formerly composed these eight verses to teach people in general. Now He personally tasted the meaning of the verses, which are called the Śikṣāṣṭaka.**
