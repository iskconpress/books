====== Cc. Antya 20.144-146 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-kṛṣṇa-caitanya, śrī-yuta nityānanda
> śrī-advaita-ācārya, śrī-gaura-bhakta-vṛnda
>
> śrī-svarūpa, śrī-rūpa, śrī-sanātana
> śrī-guru śrī-raghunātha, śrī-jīva-caraṇa
>
> nija-śire dhari' ei sabāra caraṇa
> yāhā haite haya saba vāñchita-pūraṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:c:caitanya]]---Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:y:yuta]] [[synonyms:n:nityānanda]]---Lord Nityānanda; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:a:advaita]]-[[synonyms:ā:ācārya]]---Śrī Advaita Prabhu; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:g:gaura]]-[[synonyms:b:bhakta]]-[[synonyms:v:vṛnda]]---the devotees of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:s:svarūpa]]---Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---Śrī Rūpa Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:s:sanātana]]---Śrīla Sanātana Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:g:guru]]---the spiritual master; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:r:raghunātha]]---Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:j:jīva]]-[[synonyms:c:caraṇa]]---the lotus feet of Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:n:nija]]-[[synonyms:ś:śire]] [[synonyms:d:dhari']]---catching on my head; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:s:sabāra]] [[synonyms:c:caraṇa]]---the lotus feet of all of them; [[synonyms:y:yāhā]] [[synonyms:h:haite]]---by which action; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---there is; [[synonyms:s:saba]]---all; [[synonyms:v:vāñchita]]-[[synonyms:p:pūraṇa]]---the fulfilling of desires.

===== Translation =====

**So that my desires may be fulfilled, I place the lotus feet of these personalities on my head: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, with Lord Nityānanda, Advaita Ācārya and Their devotees, as well as Śrī Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī, Śrī Rūpa Gosvāmī, Śrī Sanātana Gosvāmī, Śrī Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī, who is my spiritual master, and Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī.**

===== Purport =====

Śrīla Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī was the instructing spiritual master of Kṛṣṇadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī and has therefore been described as //śrī-guru.//
