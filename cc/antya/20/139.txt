====== Cc. Antya 20.139 ======

===== Text =====

> bhakte śikhāite yei śikṣāṣṭaka kahilā
> sei ślokāṣṭakera artha punaḥ āsvādilā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:bhakte]]---the devotees; [[synonyms:ś:śikhāite]]---to teach; [[synonyms:y:yei]]---that which; [[synonyms:ś:śikṣā]]-[[synonyms:a:aṣṭaka]]---eight instructions; [[synonyms:k:kahilā]]---described; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:ś:śloka]]-[[synonyms:a:aṣṭakera]]---of the same eight verses; [[synonyms:a:artha]]---the meaning; [[synonyms:p:punaḥ]] [[synonyms:ā:āsvādilā]]---again He tasted.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu composed those eight stanzas to instruct the devotees, but He also personally tasted their meaning.**
