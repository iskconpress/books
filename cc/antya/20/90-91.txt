====== Cc. Antya 20.90-91 ======

===== Text =====

> āmi---ati-kṣudra jīva, pakṣī rāṅgā-ṭuni
> se yaiche tṛṣṇāya piye samudrera pānī

> taiche āmi eka kaṇa chuṅiluṅ līlāra
> ei dṛṣṭānte jāniha prabhura līlāra vistāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āmi]]---I; [[synonyms:a:ati]]-[[synonyms:k:kṣudra]] [[synonyms:j:jīva]]---a very insignificant living being; [[synonyms:p:pakṣī]] [[synonyms:r:rāṅgā]]-[[synonyms:ṭ:ṭuni]]---just like a little bird with a red beak; [[synonyms:s:se]]---he; [[synonyms:y:yaiche]]---just as; [[synonyms:t:tṛṣṇāya]]---in thirst; [[synonyms:p:piye]]---drinks; [[synonyms:s:samudrera]] [[synonyms:p:pānī]]---the water of the sea; [[synonyms:t:taiche]]---in the same way; [[synonyms:ā:āmi]]---I; [[synonyms:e:eka]] [[synonyms:k:kaṇa]]---one small particle; [[synonyms:c:chuṅiluṅ]]---touched; [[synonyms:l:līlāra]]---of the pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:d:dṛṣṭānte]]---by this example; [[synonyms:j:jāniha]]---all of you know; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:l:līlāra]] [[synonyms:v:vistāra]]---expansion of the pastimes.

===== Translation =====

**I am a very insignificant living being, like a small red-beaked bird. Just as such a bird drinks the water of the sea to quench its thirst, so I have touched only a drop of the ocean of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's pastimes. From this example, you may all understand how expansive are the pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.**
