====== CC Antya 20.142-143 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-rādhā-saha 'śrī-madana-mohana'
> śrī-rādhā-saha 'śrī-govinda'-caraṇa
>
> śrī-rādhā-saha śrīla 'śrī-gopīnātha'
> ei tina ṭhākura haya 'gauḍiyāra nātha'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:r:rādhā]]-[[synonyms:s:saha]]---with Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:m:madana]]-[[synonyms:m:mohana]]---the Deity Madana-mohanajī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:r:rādhā]]-[[synonyms:s:saha]]---with Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:g:govinda]]-[[synonyms:c:caraṇa]]---the lotus feet of Śrī Govindajī; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:r:rādhā]]-[[synonyms:s:saha]]---with Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:ś:śrīla]] [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:g:gopīnātha]]---the all-beautiful and opulent Gopīnāthajī; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:t:tina]]---all these three; [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭhākura]]---Deities; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---are; [[synonyms:g:gauḍiyāra]] [[synonyms:n:nātha]]---worshipable by all the Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇavas.

===== Translation =====

**The Vṛndāvana Deities of Madana-mohana with Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, Govinda with Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, and Gopīnātha with Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī are the life and soul of the Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇavas.**
