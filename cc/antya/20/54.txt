====== CC Antya 20.54 ======

===== Text =====

> kāntā kṛṣṇe kare roṣa, kṛṣṇa pāya santoṣa,
> sukha pāya tāḍana-bhartsane
> yathā-yogya kare māna, kṛṣṇa tāte sukha pāna,
> chāḍe māna alpa-sādhane

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kāntā]]---the beloved; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇe]]---unto Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:r:roṣa]]---shows anger; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]] [[synonyms:p:pāya]] [[synonyms:s:santoṣa]]---Kṛṣṇa becomes very happy; [[synonyms:s:sukha]] [[synonyms:p:pāya]]---obtains happiness; [[synonyms:t:tāḍana]]-[[synonyms:b:bhartsane]]---by chastisement; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]-[[synonyms:y:yogya]]---as it is suitable; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:m:māna]]---shows pride; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:t:tāte]]---in such activities; [[synonyms:s:sukha]] [[synonyms:p:pāna]]---obtains happiness; [[synonyms:c:chāḍe]] [[synonyms:m:māna]]---gives up pride; [[synonyms:a:alpa]]-[[synonyms:s:sādhane]]---by a little endeavor.

===== Translation =====

**"When a beloved gopī shows symptoms of anger toward Kṛṣṇa, Kṛṣṇa is very satisfied. Indeed, He is pleased when chastised by such a gopī. She shows her pride suitably, and Kṛṣṇa enjoys that attitude. Then she gives up her pride with a little endeavor.**
