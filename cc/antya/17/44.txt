====== Cc. Antya 17.44 ======

===== Text =====

> se śrī-mukha-bhāṣita, amṛta haite parāmṛta,
> smita-karpūra tāhāte miśrita
> śabda, artha,---dui-śakti, nānā-rasa kare vyakti,
> pratyakṣara---narma-vibhūṣita

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:se]]---that; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]---beautiful; [[synonyms:m:mukha]]---by the mouth; [[synonyms:b:bhāṣita]]---spoken; [[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---nectar; [[synonyms:h:haite]]---than; [[synonyms:p:para]]-[[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---more nectarean; [[synonyms:s:smita]]---smiling; [[synonyms:k:karpūra]]---camphor; [[synonyms:t:tāhāte]]---in that; [[synonyms:m:miśrita]]---mixed; [[synonyms:ś:śabda]]---sound; [[synonyms:a:artha]]---meaning; [[synonyms:d:dui]]-[[synonyms:ś:śakti]]---two energies; [[synonyms:n:nānā]]---various; [[synonyms:r:rasa]]---mellows; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:v:vyakti]]---express; [[synonyms:p:prati]]-[[synonyms:a:akṣara]]---every word; [[synonyms:n:narma]]-[[synonyms:v:vibhūṣita]]---full of meaning.

===== Translation =====

**"Kṛṣṇa's speech is far sweeter than nectar. Each of His jubilant words is full of meaning, and when His speech mixes with His smile, which is like camphor, the resultant sound and the deep meaning of Kṛṣṇa's words create various transcendental mellows.**
