====== CC Antya 17.45 ======

===== Text =====

> se amṛtera eka-kaṇa, karṇa-cakora-jīvana,
> karṇa-cakora jīye sei āśe
> bhāgya-vaśe kabhu pāya, abhāgye kabhu nā pāya,
> nā pāile maraye piyāse

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:se]] [[synonyms:a:amṛtera]]---of that nectar; [[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:k:kaṇa]]---one particle; [[synonyms:k:karṇa]]-[[synonyms:c:cakora]]---of the ear, which is like a //cakora// bird; [[synonyms:j:jīvana]]---the life; [[synonyms:k:karṇa]]---the ear; [[synonyms:c:cakora]]---the //cakora// bird; [[synonyms:j:jīye]]---lives; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:ā:āśe]]---with that hope; [[synonyms:b:bhāgya]]-[[synonyms:v:vaśe]]---by good fortune; [[synonyms:k:kabhu]]---sometimes; [[synonyms:p:pāya]]---gets; [[synonyms:a:abhāgye]]---by misfortune; [[synonyms:k:kabhu]]---sometimes; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:p:pāya]]---does not get; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:p:pāile]]---if does not get; [[synonyms:m:maraye]]---dies; [[synonyms:p:piyāse]]---from thirst.

===== Translation =====

**"One particle of that transcendental, blissful nectar is the life and soul of the ear, which is like a cakora bird that lives in hope of tasting that nectar. Sometimes, by good fortune, the bird can taste it, but at other times he unfortunately cannot and therefore almost dies of thirst.**
