====== Cc. Antya 17.50 ======

===== Text =====

> bhāva-śābalye rādhāra ukti, līlā-śuke haila sphūrti,
> sei bhāve paḍe eka śloka
> unmādera sāmarthye, sei ślokera kare arthe,
> yei artha nāhi jāne loka

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:bhāva]]-[[synonyms:ś:śābalye]]---in the aggregate of all ecstasies; [[synonyms:r:rādhāra]]---of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:u:ukti]]---statement; [[synonyms:l:līlā]]-[[synonyms:ś:śuke]]---in Bilvamaṅgala Ṭhākura; [[synonyms:h:haila]]---there was; [[synonyms:s:sphūrti]]---awakening; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:b:bhāve]]---in that ecstasy; [[synonyms:p:paḍe]]---recites; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:ś:śloka]]---verse; [[synonyms:u:unmādera]]---of madness; [[synonyms:s:sāmarthye]]---in the capacity; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:ś:ślokera]]---of that verse; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:a:arthe]]---describes the meaning; [[synonyms:y:yei]] [[synonyms:a:artha]]---which meaning; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---do not; [[synonyms:j:jāne]]---know; [[synonyms:l:loka]]---people.

===== Translation =====

**The aggregate of all these ecstasies awoke a statement by Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī in the mind of Bilvamaṅgala Ṭhākura [Līlā-śuka]. In the same ecstatic mood, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu now recited that verse, and on the strength of madness, He described its meaning, which is unknown to people in general.**
