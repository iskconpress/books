====== Cc. Antya 17.43 ======

===== Text =====

> nūpura-kiṅkinī-dhvani, haṁsa-sārasa jini',
> kaṅkaṇa-dhvani caṭake lājāya
> eka-bāra yei śune, vyāpi rahe' tāra kāṇe,
> anya śabda se-kāṇe nā yāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nūpura]]---of the ankle bells; [[synonyms:k:kiṅkinī]]---tinkling; [[synonyms:d:dhvani]]---the sound; [[synonyms:h:haṁsa]]---swans; [[synonyms:s:sārasa]]---cranes; [[synonyms:j:jini']]---conquering; [[synonyms:k:kaṅkaṇa]]-[[synonyms:d:dhvani]]---the sound of bangles; [[synonyms:c:caṭake]]---the //caṭaka bird//; [[synonyms:l:lājāya]]---puts to shame; [[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:b:bāra]]---once; [[synonyms:y:yei]]---one who; [[synonyms:ś:śune]]---hears; [[synonyms:v:vyāpi]]---expanding; [[synonyms:r:rahe']]---remains; [[synonyms:t:tāra]] [[synonyms:k:kāṇe]]---in his ear; [[synonyms:a:anya]]---other; [[synonyms:ś:śabda]]---sound; [[synonyms:s:se]]-[[synonyms:k:kāṇe]]---in that ear; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---does not go.

===== Translation =====

**"The tinkling of Kṛṣṇa's ankle bells surpasses the songs of even the swan and crane, and the sound of His bangles puts the singing of the caṭaka bird to shame. Having allowed these sounds to enter the ears even once, one cannot tolerate hearing anything else.**
