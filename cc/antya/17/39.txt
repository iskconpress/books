====== Cc. Antya 17.39 ======

===== Text =====

> eta kahi' krodhāveśe, bhāvera taraṅge bhāse,
> utkaṇṭhā-sāgare ḍube mana
> rādhāra utkaṇṭhā-vāṇī, paḍi' āpane vākhāni,
> kṛṣṇa-mādhurya kare āsvādana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:eta]] [[synonyms:k:kahi']]---saying this; [[synonyms:k:krodha]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśe]]---in the mood of anger; [[synonyms:b:bhāvera]] [[synonyms:t:taraṅge]]---in the waves of ecstatic love; [[synonyms:b:bhāse]]---floats; [[synonyms:u:utkaṇṭhā]]---of anxieties; [[synonyms:s:sāgare]]---in the ocean; [[synonyms:ḍ:ḍube]] [[synonyms:m:mana]]---merges the mind; [[synonyms:r:rādhāra]]---of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:u:utkaṇṭhā]]-[[synonyms:v:vāṇī]]---words of anxiety; [[synonyms:p:paḍi']]---reciting; [[synonyms:ā:āpane]]---personally; [[synonyms:v:vākhāni]]---explaining; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:m:mādhurya]]---the sweetness of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:ā:āsvādana]]---tastes.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu spoke these words in a mood of anger as He floated on waves of ecstatic love. Merged in an ocean of anxiety, He recited a verse spoken by Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī expressing the same emotion. Then He personally explained the verse and thus tasted the sweetness of Kṛṣṇa.**
