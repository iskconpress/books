====== Cc. Antya 9.48 ======

===== Text =====

> yathārtha mūlye ghoḍā laha, yebā bākī haya
> krame krame dibe, vyartha prāṇa kene laya"

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yathā]]-[[synonyms:a:artha]] [[synonyms:m:mūlye]]---for the proper price; [[synonyms:g:ghoḍā]] [[synonyms:l:laha]]---accept the horses; [[synonyms:y:yebā]]---whatever; [[synonyms:b:bākī]] [[synonyms:h:haya]]---balance is due; [[synonyms:k:krame]] [[synonyms:k:krame]]---gradually; [[synonyms:d:dibe]]---he will give; [[synonyms:v:vyartha]]---unnecessarily; [[synonyms:p:prāṇa]]---his life; [[synonyms:k:kene]]---why; [[synonyms:l:laya]]---are you taking.

===== Translation =====

**"It would be better to take the horses for a proper price and let him gradually repay the balance. Why are you killing him unnecessarily?"**
