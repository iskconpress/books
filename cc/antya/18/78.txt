====== CC Antya 18.78 ======

===== Text =====

> antar-daśāra kichu ghora, kichu bāhya-jñāna
> sei daśā kahe bhakta 'ardha-bāhya'-nāma

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:antaḥ]]-[[synonyms:d:daśāra]]---of the internal condition; [[synonyms:k:kichu]]---some; [[synonyms:g:ghora]]---deep state; [[synonyms:k:kichu]]---some; [[synonyms:b:bāhya]]-[[synonyms:j:jñāna]]---external consciousness; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:d:daśā]]---that condition; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---say; [[synonyms:b:bhakta]]---devotees; [[synonyms:a:ardha]]-[[synonyms:b:bāhya]]---half-external consciousness; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---name.

===== Translation =====

**When the Lord is deeply absorbed in internal consciousness but He nevertheless exhibits some external consciousness, devotees call His condition ardha-bāhya, or half-external consciousness.**
