====== CC Antya 18.90 ======

===== Text =====

> yata gopa-sundarī, kṛṣṇa tata rūpa dhari',
> sabāra vastra karilā haraṇe
> yamunā-jala nirmala, aṅga kare jhalamala,
> sukhe kṛṣṇa kare daraśane

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yata]]---as many; [[synonyms:g:gopa]]-[[synonyms:s:sundarī]]---beautiful //gopīs//; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:t:tata]]---that many; [[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---forms; [[synonyms:d:dhari']]---accepting; [[synonyms:s:sabāra]]---of all; [[synonyms:v:vastra]]---covering cloths; [[synonyms:k:karilā]] [[synonyms:h:haraṇe]]---took away; [[synonyms:y:yamunā]]-[[synonyms:j:jala]]---the water of the Yamunā; [[synonyms:n:nirmala]]---very clear; [[synonyms:a:aṅga]]---bodies; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:j:jhalamala]]---glitter; [[synonyms:s:sukhe]]---happily; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:d:daraśane]]---sees.

===== Translation =====

**"Kṛṣṇa expanded Himself into as many forms as there were gopīs and then took away all the garments that covered them. The water of the River Yamunā was crystal clear, and Kṛṣṇa saw the glittering bodies of the gopīs in great happiness.**
