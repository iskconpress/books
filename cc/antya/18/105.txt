====== CC Antya 18.105 ======

===== Text =====

> kharamujā, kṣīrikā, tāla, keśura, pānī-phala, mṛṇāla,
> bilva, pīlu, dāḍimbādi yata
> kona deśe kāra khyāti, vṛndāvane saba-prāpti,
> sahasra-jāti, lekhā yāya kata?

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kharamujā]]---cantaloupe; [[synonyms:k:kṣīrikā]]---//kṣīrikā// fruit; [[synonyms:t:tāla]]---palm or palmyra fruit; [[synonyms:k:keśura]]---//keśura// fruit; [[synonyms:p:pānī]]-[[synonyms:p:phala]]---a fruit produced in the water of rivers; [[synonyms:m:mṛṇāla]]---a fruit from lotus flowers; [[synonyms:b:bilva]]---bel fruit; [[synonyms:p:pīlu]]---a special fruit in Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:d:dāḍimba]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---the pomegranate and other similar fruits; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---as many as there are; [[synonyms:k:kona]] [[synonyms:d:deśe]]---in some country; [[synonyms:k:kāra]]---of which; [[synonyms:k:khyāti]]---of fame; [[synonyms:v:vṛndāvane]]---in Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:s:saba]]-[[synonyms:p:prāpti]]---obtainment of all; [[synonyms:s:sahasra]]-[[synonyms:j:jāti]]---thousands of varieties; [[synonyms:l:lekhā]] [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---one is able to write; [[synonyms:k:kata]]---how much.

===== Translation =====

**"There were cantaloupes, kṣirikās, palmfruits, keśuras, waterfruits, lotus fruits, bel, pīlu, pomegranate and many others. Some of them are variously known in different places, but in Vṛndāvana they are always available in so many thousands of varieties that no one can fully describe them.**
