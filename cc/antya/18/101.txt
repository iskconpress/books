====== CC Antya 18.101 ======

===== Text =====

> punarapi kaila snāna, śuṣka-vastra paridhāna,
> ratna-mandire kailā āgamana
> vṛndā-kṛta sambhāra, gandha-puṣpa-alaṅkāra,
> vanya-veśa karila racana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:punarapi]]---again; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---took; [[synonyms:s:snāna]]---bath; [[synonyms:ś:śuṣka]]-[[synonyms:v:vastra]]---dry cloth; [[synonyms:p:paridhāna]]---putting on; [[synonyms:r:ratna]]-[[synonyms:m:mandire]]---in a small house of jewels; [[synonyms:k:kailā]]---did; [[synonyms:ā:āgamana]]---arrival; [[synonyms:v:vṛndā]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛta]]---arranged by the //gopī// Vṛndā; [[synonyms:s:sambhāra]]---all kinds of articles; [[synonyms:g:gandha]]-[[synonyms:p:puṣpa]]-[[synonyms:a:alaṅkāra]]---scented flowers and ornaments; [[synonyms:v:vanya]]-[[synonyms:v:veśa]]---forest dress; [[synonyms:k:karila]]---did; [[synonyms:r:racana]]---arrangement.

===== Translation =====

**"Then they all bathed again, and after putting on dry clothing, they went to a small jeweled house, where the gopī Vṛndā arranged to dress them in forest clothing by decorating them with fragrant flowers, green leaves and all kinds of other ornaments.**
