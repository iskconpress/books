====== CC Antya 18.106 ======

===== Text =====

> gaṅgājala, amṛtakeli, pīyūṣagranthi, karpūrakeli,
> sarapūrī, amṛti, padmacini
> khaṇḍa-kṣīrisāra-vṛkṣa, ghare kari' nānā bhakṣya,
> rādhā yāhā kṛṣṇa lāgi' āni

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:gaṅgā]]-[[synonyms:j:jala]]---the sweetmeat //gaṅgājala//; [[synonyms:a:amṛta]]-[[synonyms:k:keli]]---a sweetmeat made of milk; [[synonyms:p:pīyūṣa]]-[[synonyms:g:granthi]]---//pīyūṣagranthi//; [[synonyms:k:karpūra]]-[[synonyms:k:keli]]---//karpūrakeli//; [[synonyms:s:sara]]-[[synonyms:p:pūrī]]---a sweet made from milk; [[synonyms:a:amṛti]]---a sweet prepared from rice flour; [[synonyms:p:padma]]-[[synonyms:c:cini]]---a sweet preparation made from lotus flowers; [[synonyms:k:khaṇḍa]]-[[synonyms:k:kṣīri]]-[[synonyms:s:sāra]]-[[synonyms:v:vṛkṣa]]---sugar sweets made in the shape of trees; [[synonyms:g:ghare]]---at home; [[synonyms:k:kari']]---making; [[synonyms:n:nānā]] [[synonyms:b:bhakṣya]]---varieties of eatables; [[synonyms:r:rādhā]]---Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:y:yāhā]]---which; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]] [[synonyms:l:lāgi']]---for Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:ā:āni]]---brought.

===== Translation =====

**"At home Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī had made various types of sweetmeats from milk and sugar, such as gaṅgājala, amṛtakeli, pīyūṣagranthi, karpūrakeli, sarapūrī, amṛti, padmacini and khaṇḍa-kṣīrisāra-vṛkṣa. She had then brought them all for Kṛṣṇa.**
