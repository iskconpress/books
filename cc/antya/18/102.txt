====== Cc. Antya 18.102 ======

===== Text =====

> vṛndāvane taru-latā, adbhuta tāhāra kathā,
> bāra-māsa dhare phula-phala
> vṛndāvane devī-gaṇa, kuñja-dāsī yata jana,
> phala pāḍi' āniyā sakala

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vṛndāvane]]---at Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:t:taru]]-[[synonyms:l:latā]]---trees and creepers; [[synonyms:a:adbhuta]]---wonderful; [[synonyms:t:tāhāra]] [[synonyms:k:kathā]]---their story; [[synonyms:b:bāra]]-[[synonyms:m:māsa]]---twelve months; [[synonyms:d:dhare]]---produce; [[synonyms:p:phula]]-[[synonyms:p:phala]]---fruits and flowers; [[synonyms:v:vṛndāvane]]---at Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:d:devī]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---all the //gopīs//; [[synonyms:k:kuñja]]-[[synonyms:d:dāsī]]---maidservants in the bowers; [[synonyms:y:yata]] [[synonyms:j:jana]]---as many persons as there are; [[synonyms:p:phala]] [[synonyms:p:pāḍi']]---picking fruits; [[synonyms:ā:āniyā]]---bringing; [[synonyms:s:sakala]]---all varieties.

===== Translation =====

**"In Vṛndāvana, the trees and creepers are wonderful because throughout the entire year they produce all kinds of fruits and flowers. The gopīs and maidservants in the bowers of Vṛndāvana pick these fruits and flowers and bring them before Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa.**
