====== CC Antya 5.65 ======

===== Text =====

> vaktā śrotā kahe śune duṅhe premāveśe
> ātma-smṛti nāhi, kāhāṅ jāniba dina-śeṣe

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vaktā]]---the speaker; [[synonyms:ś:śrotā]]---the listener; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---speaks; [[synonyms:ś:śune]]---hears; [[synonyms:d:duṅhe]]---both of them; [[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśe]]---in ecstatic love; [[synonyms:ā:ātma]]-[[synonyms:s:smṛti]] [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---there was no bodily consciousness; [[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:j:jāniba]]---can understand; [[synonyms:d:dina]]-[[synonyms:ś:śeṣe]]---the end of day.

===== Translation =====

**The speaker and listener spoke and heard in ecstatic love. Thus they forgot their bodily consciousness. How, then, could they perceive the end of the day?**
