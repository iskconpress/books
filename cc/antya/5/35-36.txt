====== Cc. Antya 5.35-36 ======

===== Text =====

> "āmi ta' sannyāsī, āpanāre virakta kari' māni
> darśana rahu dūre, 'prakṛtira' nāma yadi śuni

> tabahiṅ vikāra pāya mora tanu-mana
> prakṛti-darśane sthira haya kon jana?"

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āmi]]---I; [[synonyms:t:ta']]---certainly; [[synonyms:s:sannyāsī]]---in the renounced order of life; [[synonyms:ā:āpanāre]]---Myself; [[synonyms:v:virakta]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---having renounced everything; [[synonyms:m:māni]]---I consider; [[synonyms:d:darśana]] [[synonyms:r:rahu]] [[synonyms:d:dūre]]---what to speak of seeing; [[synonyms:p:prakṛtira]]---of a woman; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---name; [[synonyms:y:yadi]]---if; [[synonyms:ś:śuni]]---I hear; [[synonyms:t:tabahiṅ]]---immediately; [[synonyms:v:vikāra]]---transformation; [[synonyms:p:pāya]]---gets; [[synonyms:m:mora]]---My; [[synonyms:t:tanu]]-[[synonyms:m:mana]]---mind and body; [[synonyms:p:prakṛti]]-[[synonyms:d:darśane]]---by seeing a woman; [[synonyms:s:sthira]]---steady; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---is; [[synonyms:k:kon]] [[synonyms:j:jana]]---what person.

===== Translation =====

**"I am a sannyāsī," He said, "and I certainly consider Myself renounced. But not to speak of seeing a woman, if I even hear the name of a woman, I feel changes in My mind and body. Therefore who could remain unmoved by the sight of a woman? It is very difficult.**
