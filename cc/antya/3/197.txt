====== Cc. Antya 3.197 ======

===== Text =====

> tvat-sākṣātkaraṇāhlāda-
> viśuddhābdhi-sthitasya me
> sukhāni goṣpadāyante
> brāhmāṇy api jagad-guro

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tvat]]---You; [[synonyms:s:sākṣāt]]-[[synonyms:k:karaṇa]]---by meeting; [[synonyms:ā:āhlāda]]---of pleasure; [[synonyms:v:viśuddha]]---spiritually purified; [[synonyms:a:abdhi]]---in an ocean; [[synonyms:s:sthitasya]]---situated; [[synonyms:m:me]]---of me; [[synonyms:s:sukhāni]]---happiness; [[synonyms:g:goṣpadāyante]]---is like a calf's hoofprint; [[synonyms:b:brāhmāṇi]]---derived from understanding of impersonal Brahman; [[synonyms:a:api]]---also; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:g:guro]]---O master of the universe.

===== Translation =====

**"My dear Lord, O master of the universe, since I have directly seen You, my transcendental bliss has taken the shape of a great ocean. Being situated in that ocean, I now realize all other so-called happiness, including even brahmānanda, to be like the water contained in the hoofprint of a calf.' "**

===== Purport =====

This verse is quoted from the //Hari-bhakti-sudhodaya// (14.36).
