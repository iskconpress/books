====== Cc. Antya 3.4-5 ======

===== Text =====

> prabhu-sthāne nitya āise, kare namaskāra
> prabhu-sane vāt kahe prabhu-'prāṇa' tāra
>
> prabhute tāhāra prīti, prabhu dayā kare
> dāmodara tāra prīti sahite nā pāre

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:prabhu]]-[[synonyms:s:sthāne]]---to the place of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:n:nitya]]---daily; [[synonyms:ā:āise]]---comes; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:n:namaskāra]]---offers respectful obeisances; [[synonyms:p:prabhu]]-[[synonyms:s:sane]]---with the Lord; [[synonyms:v:vāt]] [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---talks; [[synonyms:p:prabhu]]-[[synonyms:p:prāṇa]] [[synonyms:t:tāra]]---his life and soul was Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:p:prabhute]]---unto the Lord; [[synonyms:t:tāhāra]] [[synonyms:p:prīti]]---his affection; [[synonyms:p:prabhu]]---the Lord; [[synonyms:d:dayā]] [[synonyms:k:kare]]---reciprocates His mercy; [[synonyms:d:dāmodara]]---Dāmodara Paṇḍita; [[synonyms:t:tāra]]---his; [[synonyms:p:prīti]]---love of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:s:sahite]] [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:p:pāre]]---could not tolerate.

===== Translation =====

**The boy came dally to Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and offered Him respectful obeisances. He was free to talk with Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu because the Lord was his life and soul, but the boy's intimacy with the Lord and the Lord's mercy toward him were intolerable to Dāmodara Paṇḍita.**
