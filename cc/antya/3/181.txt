====== Cc. Antya 3.181 ======

===== Text =====

> aṁhaḥ saṁharad akhilaṁ sakṛd
> udayād eva sakala-lokasya
> taraṇir iva timira-jaladhiṁ
> jayati jagan-maṅgalaṁ harer nāma

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:aṁhaḥ]]---the resultant action of sinful life, which causes material bondage; [[synonyms:s:saṁharat]]---completely eradicating; [[synonyms:a:akhilam]]---all; [[synonyms:s:sakṛt]]---once only; [[synonyms:u:udayāt]]---by rising; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---certainly; [[synonyms:s:sakala]]---all; [[synonyms:l:lokasya]]---of the people of the world; [[synonyms:t:taraṇiḥ]]---the sun; [[synonyms:i:iva]]---like; [[synonyms:t:timira]]---of darkness; [[synonyms:j:jala]]-[[synonyms:d:dhim]]---the ocean; [[synonyms:j:jayati]]---all glories to; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:m:maṅgalam]]---auspicious for the whole world; [[synonyms:h:hareḥ]] [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---the holy name of the Lord. name of the Lord.

===== Translation =====

**"As the rising sun immediately dissipates all the world's darkness, which is deep like an ocean, so the holy name of the Lord, if chanted once without offenses, can dissipate all the reactions of a living being's sinful life. All glories to that holy name of the Lord, which is auspicious for the entire world.' "**

===== Purport =====

This verse is found in the //Padyāvalī// (16).
