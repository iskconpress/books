====== Cc. Antya 15.9 ======

===== Text =====

> eka-mana pañca-dike pañca-guṇa ṭāne
> ṭānāṭāni prabhura mana haila ageyāne

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:m:mana]]---one mind; [[synonyms:p:pañca]]-[[synonyms:d:dike]]---in five directions; [[synonyms:p:pañca]]-[[synonyms:g:guṇa]]---the five attributes; [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭāne]]---attracted; [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭānāṭāni]]---by a tug-of-war; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:m:mana]]---the mind; [[synonyms:h:haila]]---became; [[synonyms:a:ageyāne]]---unconscious.

===== Translation =====

**Just as in a tug-of-war, the single mind of Lord Caitanya was attracted in five directions by the five transcendental attributes of Lord Kṛṣṇa. Thus the Lord became unconscious.**
