====== CC Antya 15.20 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇera vacana-mādhurī, nānā-rasa-narma-dhārī,
> tāra anyāya kathana nā yāya
> jagatera nārīra kāṇe, mādhurī-guṇe bāndhi' ṭāne,
> ṭānāṭāni kāṇera prāṇa yāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇera]]---of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:v:vacana]]-[[synonyms:m:mādhurī]]---the sweetness of speaking; [[synonyms:n:nānā]]---various; [[synonyms:r:rasa]]-[[synonyms:n:narma]]-[[synonyms:d:dhārī]]---full of joking words; [[synonyms:t:tāra]]---of that; [[synonyms:a:anyāya]]---atrocities; [[synonyms:k:kathana]]---description; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---cannot be made; [[synonyms:j:jagatera]]---of the world; [[synonyms:n:nārīra]]---of women; [[synonyms:k:kāṇe]]---in the ear; [[synonyms:m:mādhurī]]-[[synonyms:g:guṇe]]---to the attributes of sweetness; [[synonyms:b:bāndhi']]---binding; [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭāne]]---pulls; [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭānāṭāni]]---tug-of-war; [[synonyms:k:kāṇera]]---of the ear; [[synonyms:p:prāṇa]] [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---the life departs.

===== Translation =====

**"The sweetness of Kṛṣṇa's joking words plays indescribable havoc with the hearts of all women. His words bind a woman's ear to the qualities of their sweetness. Thus there is a tug-of-war, and the life of the ear departs.**
