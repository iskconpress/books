====== CC Antya 15.64 ======

===== Text =====

> nava-ghana-snigdha-varṇa, dalitāñjana-cikkaṇa,
> indīvara-nindi sukomala
> jini' upamāna-gaṇa, hare sabāra netra-mana,
> kṛṣṇa-kānti parama prabala

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nava]]-[[synonyms:g:ghana]]---a newly formed cloud; [[synonyms:s:snigdha]]---attractive; [[synonyms:v:varṇa]]---bodily complexion; [[synonyms:d:dalita]]---powdered; [[synonyms:a:añjana]]---ointment; [[synonyms:c:cikkaṇa]]---polished; [[synonyms:i:indīvara]]---a blue lotus flower; [[synonyms:n:nindi]]---defeating; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:k:komala]]---soft; [[synonyms:j:jini]]---surpassing; [[synonyms:u:upamāna]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---all comparison; [[synonyms:h:hare]]---attracts; [[synonyms:s:sabāra]]---of all; [[synonyms:n:netra]]-[[synonyms:m:mana]]---the eyes and mind; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:k:kānti]]---the complexion of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:p:parama]] [[synonyms:p:prabala]]---supremely powerful.

===== Translation =====

**Caitanya Mahāprabhu continued: "Śrī Kṛṣṇa's complexion is as polished as powdered eye ointment. It surpasses the beauty of a newly formed cloud and is softer than a blue lotus flower. Indeed, His complexion is so pleasing that it attracts the eyes and mind of everyone, and it is so powerful that it defies all comparison.**
