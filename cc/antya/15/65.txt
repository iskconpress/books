====== CC Antya 15.65 ======

===== Text =====

> kaha, sakhi, ki kari upāya?
> kṛṣṇādbhuta balāhaka, mora netra-cātaka,
> nā dekhi' piyāse mari' yāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kaha]]---please tell; [[synonyms:s:sakhi]]---My dear friend; [[synonyms:k:ki]] [[synonyms:k:kari]] [[synonyms:u:upāya]]---what shall I do; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:a:adbhuta]]---wonderful; [[synonyms:b:balāhaka]]---cloud; [[synonyms:m:mora]]---My; [[synonyms:n:netra]]---eyes; [[synonyms:c:cātaka]]---like //cātaka birds//; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---without seeing; [[synonyms:p:piyāse]]---from thirst; [[synonyms:m:mari']] [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---are dying.

===== Translation =====

**"My dear friend, please tell me what I should do. Kṛṣṇa is as attractive as a wonderful cloud, and My eyes are just like cātaka birds, which are dying of thirst because they do not see such a cloud.**
