====== Cc. Antya 15.22 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇāṅga---saurabhya-bhara, mṛga-mada-mada-hara,
> nīlotpalera hare garva-dhana
> jagat-nārīra nāsā, tāra bhitara pāte vāsā,
> nārī-gaṇe kare ākarṣaṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅga]]---the body of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:s:saurabhya]]-[[synonyms:b:bhara]]---full of fragrance; [[synonyms:m:mṛga]]-[[synonyms:m:mada]]---of musk; [[synonyms:m:mada]]-[[synonyms:h:hara]]---intoxicating power; [[synonyms:n:nīlotpalera]]---of the bluish lotus flower; [[synonyms:h:hare]]---takes away; [[synonyms:g:garva]]-[[synonyms:d:dhana]]---the pride of the treasure; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:n:nārīra]]---of the women in the world; [[synonyms:n:nāsā]]---nostrils; [[synonyms:t:tāra]] [[synonyms:b:bhitara]]---within them; [[synonyms:p:pāte]] [[synonyms:v:vāsā]]---constructs a residence; [[synonyms:n:nārī]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇe]]---women; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:ā:ākarṣaṇa]]---attracts.

===== Translation =====

**"The fragrance of Kṛṣṇa's body is more maddening than the aroma of musk, and it surpasses the fragrance of the bluish lotus flower. It enters the nostrils of all the women of the world and, making a nest there, thus attracts them.**
