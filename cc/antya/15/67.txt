====== Cc. Antya 15.67 ======

===== Text =====

> muralīra kala-dhvani, madhura garjana śuni',
> vṛndāvane nāce mayūra-caya
> akalaṅka pūrṇa-kala, lāvaṇya-jyotsnā jhalamala,
> citra-candrera tāhāte udaya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:muralīra]]---of the flute; [[synonyms:k:kala]]-[[synonyms:d:dhvani]]---the low vibration; [[synonyms:m:madhura]]---sweet; [[synonyms:g:garjana]]---thundering; [[synonyms:ś:śuni]]---hearing; [[synonyms:v:vṛndāvane]]---in Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:n:nāce]]---dance; [[synonyms:m:mayūra]]-[[synonyms:c:caya]]---the peacocks; [[synonyms:a:akalaṅka]]---spotless; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]-[[synonyms:k:kala]]---the full moon; [[synonyms:l:lāvaṇya]]---beauty; [[synonyms:j:jyotsnā]]---light; [[synonyms:j:jhalamala]]---glittering; [[synonyms:c:citra]]-[[synonyms:c:candrera]]---of the beautiful moon; [[synonyms:t:tāhāte]]---in that; [[synonyms:u:udaya]]---the rising.

===== Translation =====

**"The luster of Kṛṣṇa's body is as beautiful as a spotless full moon that has just risen, and the vibration of His flute sounds exactly like the sweet thundering of a newly formed cloud. When the peacocks in Vṛndāvana hear that vibration, they all begin to dance.**
