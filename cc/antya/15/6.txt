====== CC Antya 15.6 ======

===== Text =====

> snāna, darśana, bhojana deha-svabhāve haya
> kumārera cāka yena satata phiraya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:snāna]]---bathing; [[synonyms:d:darśana]]---visiting the temple; [[synonyms:b:bhojana]]---taking lunch; [[synonyms:d:deha]]-[[synonyms:s:svabhāve]]---by the nature of the body; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---are; [[synonyms:k:kumārera]] [[synonyms:c:cāka]]---the potter's wheel; [[synonyms:y:yena]]---as; [[synonyms:s:satata]]---always; [[synonyms:p:phiraya]]---revolves.

===== Translation =====

**Actually, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu was always merged in ecstatic emotion, but just as a potter's wheel turns without the potter's touching it, the Lord's bodily activities, like bathing, going to the temple to see Lord Jagannātha, and taking lunch, went on automatically.**
