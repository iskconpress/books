====== CC Antya 1.184 ======

===== Text =====

> naṭatā kirāta-rājaṁ
>  nihatya raṅga-sthale kalā-nidhinā
> samaye tena vidheyaṁ
> guṇavati tārā-kara-grahaṇam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:naṭatā]]---dancing on the stage; [[synonyms:k:kirāta]]-[[synonyms:r:rājam]]---the ruler of the //kirāta// (uncivilized men), Kaṁsa; [[synonyms:n:nihatya]]---killing; [[synonyms:r:raṅga]]-[[synonyms:s:sthale]]---on the stage; [[synonyms:k:kalā]]-[[synonyms:n:nidhinā]]---the master of all arts; [[synonyms:s:samaye]]---at the time; [[synonyms:t:tena]]---by Him; [[synonyms:v:vidheyam]]---to be done; [[synonyms:g:guṇa]]-[[synonyms:v:vati]]---at the qualified moment; [[synonyms:t:tārā]]-[[synonyms:k:kara]]---of the hand of Tārā (Rādhā); [[synonyms:g:grahaṇam]]---the acceptance.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'While dancing on the stage after having killed the ruler of uncivilized men [Kaṁsa], Lord Kṛṣṇa, master of all arts, will at the proper time accept the hand of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, who is qualified with all transcendental attributes.'  (Lalita-mādhava 1.11)**
