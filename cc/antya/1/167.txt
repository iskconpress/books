====== CC Antya 1.167 ======

===== Text =====

> kula-varatanu-dharma-grāva-vṛndāni bhindan
> sumukhi niśita-dīrghāpāṅga-ṭaṅka-cchaṭābhiḥ
> yugapad ayam apūrvaḥ kaḥ puro viśva-karmā
> marakata-maṇi-lakṣair goṣṭha-kakṣāṁ cinoti

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kula]]-[[synonyms:v:varatanu]]---of the family women; [[synonyms:d:dharma]]---in the form of dedication to the husband, etc.; [[synonyms:g:grāva]]-[[synonyms:v:vṛndāni]]---the stones; [[synonyms:b:bhindan]]---splitting; [[synonyms:s:sumukhi]]---O beautiful-faced one; [[synonyms:n:niśita]]---sharp; [[synonyms:d:dīrgha]]-[[synonyms:a:apāṅga]]---in the form of long outer corners of the eyes; [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭaṅka]]-[[synonyms:c:chaṭābhiḥ]]---by chisels; [[synonyms:y:yugapat]]---simultaneously; [[synonyms:a:ayam]]---this; [[synonyms:a:apūrvaḥ]]---unprecedented; [[synonyms:k:kaḥ]]---who; [[synonyms:p:puraḥ]]---in front; [[synonyms:v:viśva]]-[[synonyms:k:karmā]]---creative person; [[synonyms:m:marakata]]-[[synonyms:m:maṇi]]-[[synonyms:l:lakṣaiḥ]]---with countless emeralds; [[synonyms:g:goṣṭha]]-[[synonyms:k:kakṣām]]---a private room for meeting; [[synonyms:c:cinoti]]---He is constructing.

===== Translation =====

**'O beautiful-faced one, who is this creative person standing before us? With the sharp chisels of His loving glances, He is splitting the hard stones of many women's devotion to their husbands. And with the luster of His body, surpassing the brilliance of countless emeralds, He is simultaneously constructing private meeting places for His pastimes.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse (//Lalita-mādhava// 1.52) is spoken by Rādhārāṇī to Lalitādevī.
