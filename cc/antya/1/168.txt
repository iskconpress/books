====== CC Antya 1.168 ======

===== Text =====

> mahendra-maṇi-maṇḍalī-mada-viḍambi-deha-dyutir
> vrajendra-kula-candramāḥ sphurati ko 'pi navyo yuvā
> sakhi sthira-kulāṅganā-nikara-nīvi-bandhārgala-
> cchidākaraṇa-kautukī jayati yasya vaṁśī-dhvaniḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mahendra]]-[[synonyms:m:maṇi]]---of jewels of the name //mahendra-maṇi//; [[synonyms:m:maṇḍalī]]---of masses; [[synonyms:m:mada]]-[[synonyms:v:viḍambi]]---defeating the pride; [[synonyms:d:deha]]-[[synonyms:d:dyutiḥ]]---one whose bodily luster; [[synonyms:v:vrajendra]]-[[synonyms:k:kula]]-[[synonyms:c:candramāḥ]]---the moon of the family of Vrajarāja (Nanda Mahārāja); [[synonyms:s:sphurati]]---manifests; [[synonyms:k:kaḥ]] [[synonyms:a:api]]---some; [[synonyms:n:navyaḥ]] [[synonyms:y:yuvā]]---newly youthful person; [[synonyms:s:sakhi]]---O my dear friend; [[synonyms:s:sthira]]---steady; [[synonyms:k:kula]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅganā]]---of family ladies; [[synonyms:n:nikara]]---of groups; [[synonyms:n:nīvi]]-[[synonyms:b:bandha]]-[[synonyms:a:argala]]---of the impediments such as tightened dresses and belts; [[synonyms:c:cchidā]]-[[synonyms:k:karaṇa]]---in causing the cutting; [[synonyms:k:kautukī]]---very cunning; [[synonyms:j:jayati]]---all glories; [[synonyms:y:yasya]]---of whom; [[synonyms:v:vaṁśī]]-[[synonyms:d:dhvaniḥ]]---to the vibration of the flute.

===== Translation =====

**My dear friend, this newly youthful Lord Śrī Kṛṣṇa, the moon in the family of Nanda Mahārāja, is so beautiful that He defies the beauty of clusters of valuable jewels. All glories to the vibration of His flute, for it is cunningly breaking the patience of chaste ladies by loosening their belts and tight dresses.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse from //Lalita-mādhava// (1.49) is spoken by Lalitādevī to Rādhārāṇī.
