====== CC Antya 1.105 ======

===== Text =====

> dui śloka kahi' prabhura haila mahā-sukha
> nija-bhaktera guna kahe hañā pañca-mukha

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:dui]] [[synonyms:ś:śloka]] [[synonyms:k:kahi']]---reciting two verses; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:h:haila]]---there was; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:s:sukha]]---great pleasure; [[synonyms:n:nija]]-[[synonyms:b:bhaktera]]---of His own devotee; [[synonyms:g:guṇa]]---the qualities; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---describes; [[synonyms:h:hañā]]---as if becoming; [[synonyms:p:pañca]]-[[synonyms:m:mukha]]---five-mouthed.

===== Translation =====

**When Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu recited the two important verses, He felt great pleasure; thus, as if He had five mouths, He began to praise His devotee.**

===== Purport =====

The two verses referred to are those beginning with //priyaḥ so 'yam// (79) and //tuṇḍe tāṇḍavinī// (99).
