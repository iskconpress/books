====== Cc. Madhya 2.76 ======

===== Text =====

> guru---nānā bhāva-gaṇa, śiṣya---prabhura tanu-mana,
> nānā rīte satata nācāya
> nirveda, viṣāda, dainya, cāpalya, harṣa, dhairya, manyu,
> ei nṛtye prabhura kāla yāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:guru]]---the teacher; [[synonyms:n:nānā]]---various; [[synonyms:b:bhāva]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---ecstasies; [[synonyms:ś:śiṣya]]---disciples; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of Lord Caitanya; [[synonyms:t:tanu]]-[[synonyms:m:mana]]---body and mind; [[synonyms:n:nānā]]---various; [[synonyms:r:rīte]]---in ways; [[synonyms:s:satata]]---always; [[synonyms:n:nācāya]]---causes to dance; [[synonyms:n:nirveda]]---despondency; [[synonyms:v:viṣāda]]---moroseness; [[synonyms:d:dainya]]---humility; [[synonyms:c:cāpalya]]---restlessness; [[synonyms:h:harṣa]]---joy; [[synonyms:d:dhairya]]---endurance; [[synonyms:m:manyu]]---anger; [[synonyms:e:ei]]---this; [[synonyms:n:nṛtye]]---in dancing; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of the Lord; [[synonyms:k:kāla]]---time; [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---passes.

===== Translation =====

**As the spiritual master chastises the disciple and teaches him the art of devotional service, so all the ecstatic symptoms of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu-including despondency, moroseness, humility, restlessness, joy, endurance and anger-all instructed His body and mind. In this way, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu passed His time.**
