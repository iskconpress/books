====== CC Madhya 2.50 ======

===== Text =====

> ei mata dine dine, svarūpa-rāmānanda-sane,
> nija-bhāva karena vidita
> bāhye viṣa-jvālā haya, bhitare ānanda-maya,
> kṛṣṇa-premāra adbhuta carita

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:m:mata]]---in this way; [[synonyms:d:dine]] [[synonyms:d:dine]]---day after day; [[synonyms:s:svarūpa]]---Svarūpa Dāmodara; [[synonyms:r:rāmānanda]]---Rāmānanda Rāya; [[synonyms:s:sane]]---with; [[synonyms:n:nija]]---own; [[synonyms:b:bhāva]]---ecstasy; [[synonyms:k:karena]]---makes; [[synonyms:v:vidita]]---known; [[synonyms:b:bāhye]]---externally; [[synonyms:v:viṣa]]-[[synonyms:j:jvālā]] [[synonyms:h:haya]]---there is suffering from poisonous effects; [[synonyms:b:bhitare]]---within; [[synonyms:ā:ānanda]]-[[synonyms:m:maya]]---transcendental ecstasy; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:p:premāra]]---of love of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:a:adbhuta]]---wonderful; [[synonyms:c:carita]]---characteristic.

===== Translation =====

**In this way, Lord Caitanya used to revel in ecstasy day after day and exhibit these ecstasies before Svarūpa and Rāmānanda Rāya. Externally there appeared severe tribulation, as if He were suffering from poisonous effects, but internally He was experiencing bliss. This is characteristic of transcendental love of Kṛṣṇa.**
