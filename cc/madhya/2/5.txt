====== CC Madhya 2.5 ======

===== Text =====

> nirantara haya prabhura viraha-unmāda
> bhrama-maya ceṣṭā sadā, pralāpa-maya vāda

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nirantara]]---constantly; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---is; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of the Lord; [[synonyms:v:viraha]]---of separation; [[synonyms:u:unmāda]]---the madness; [[synonyms:b:bhrama]]-[[synonyms:m:maya]]---forgetful; [[synonyms:c:ceṣṭā]]---activities; [[synonyms:s:sadā]]---always; [[synonyms:p:pralāpa]]-[[synonyms:m:maya]]---full of delirium; [[synonyms:v:vāda]]---talking.

===== Translation =====

**The Lord constantly exhibited a state of mind reflecting the madness of separation. All His activities were based on forgetfulness, and His talks were always based on madness.**
