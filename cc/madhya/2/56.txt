====== CC Madhya 2.56 ======

===== Text =====

> kāhāṅ se tri-bhaṅga-ṭhāma, kāhāṅ sei veṇu-gāna,
> kāhāṅ sei yamunā-pulina
> kāhāṅ se rāsa-vilāsa, kāhāṅ nṛtya-gīta-hāsa,
> kāhāṅ prabhu madana-mohana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:s:se]]---that; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:b:bhaṅga]]-[[synonyms:ṭ:ṭhāma]]---figure curved in three places; [[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---//where//; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---that; [[synonyms:v:veṇu]]-[[synonyms:g:gāna]]---sweet song of the flute; [[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---that; [[synonyms:y:yamunā]]-[[synonyms:p:pulina]]---bank of the Yamunā River; [[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:s:se]]---that; [[synonyms:r:rāsa]]-[[synonyms:v:vilāsa]]---the //rāsa// dance; [[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:n:nṛtya]]-[[synonyms:g:gīta]]-[[synonyms:h:hāsa]]---dancing, music and laughing; [[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---where; [[synonyms:p:prabhu]]---My Lord; [[synonyms:m:madana]]-[[synonyms:m:mohana]]---the enchanter of Madana (Cupid).

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu used to lament by saying, "Where is Śrī Kṛṣṇa, whose form is curved in three places? Where is the sweet song of His flute, and where is the bank of the Yamunā? Where is the rāsa dance? Where is that dancing, singing, and laughing? Where is My Lord, Madana-mohana, the enchanter of Cupid?"**
