====== CC Madhya 2.49 ======

===== Text =====

> śuddha-prema-sukha-sindhu, pāi tāra eka bindu,
> sei bindu jagat ḍubāya
> kahibāra yogya naya, tathāpi bāule kaya,
> kahile vā kebā pātiyāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śuddha]]-[[synonyms:p:prema]]---unalloyed love; [[synonyms:s:sukha]]-[[synonyms:s:sindhu]]---an ocean of happiness; [[synonyms:p:pāi]]---if I get; [[synonyms:t:tāra]]---of that; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:b:bindu]]---drop; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:b:bindu]]---that drop; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]---the whole world; [[synonyms:ḍ:ḍubāya]]---drowns; [[synonyms:k:kahibāra]]---to speak; [[synonyms:y:yogya]] [[synonyms:n:naya]]---is not fit; [[synonyms:t:tathāpi]]---still; [[synonyms:b:bāule]]---a madman; [[synonyms:k:kaya]]---speaks; [[synonyms:k:kahile]]---if spoken; [[synonyms:v:vā]]---or; [[synonyms:k:kebā]] [[synonyms:p:pātiyāya]]---who believes.

===== Translation =====

**"Unalloyed love of Kṛṣṇa is like an ocean of happiness. If someone gets one drop of it, the whole world can drown in that drop. It is not befitting to express such love of Godhead, yet a madman must speak. However, even though he speaks, no one believes him."**
