====== Cc. Madhya 2.25 ======

===== Text =====

> śata vatsara paryanta, jīvera jīvana anta,
> ei vākya kaha nā vicāri'
> nārīra yauvana-dhana, yāre kṛṣṇa kare mana,
> se yauvana-dina dui-cāri

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śata]] [[synonyms:v:vatsara]] [[synonyms:p:paryanta]]---up to one hundred years; [[synonyms:j:jīvera]]---of the living entity; [[synonyms:j:jīvana]]---of the life; [[synonyms:a:anta]]---the end; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:v:vākya]]---this word; [[synonyms:k:kaha]]---you speak; [[synonyms:n:nā]]---without; [[synonyms:v:vicāri']]---making consideration; [[synonyms:n:nārīra]]---of a woman; [[synonyms:y:yauvana]]-[[synonyms:d:dhana]]---the wealth of youthfulness; [[synonyms:y:yāre]]---in which; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:m:mana]]---intention; [[synonyms:s:se]] [[synonyms:y:yauvana]]---that youthfulness; [[synonyms:d:dina]]---days; [[synonyms:d:dui]]-[[synonyms:c:cāri]]---two or four.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'A human being does not live more than a hundred years. You should also consider that the youthfulness of a woman, which is the only attraction for Kṛṣṇa, remains for only a few days.**
