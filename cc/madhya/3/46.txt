====== CC Madhya 3.46 ======

===== Text =====

> ca-i-marica-sukhta diyā saba phala-mūle
> amṛta-nindaka pañca-vidha tikta-jhāle

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:c:ca]]-[[synonyms:i:i]]-[[synonyms:m:marica]]---with black pepper and //ca-i// (a kind of spice); [[synonyms:s:sukhta]]---vegetables made bitter; [[synonyms:d:diyā]]---giving; [[synonyms:s:saba]]---all; [[synonyms:p:phala]]-[[synonyms:m:mūle]]---various kinds of fruits and roots; [[synonyms:a:amṛta]]-[[synonyms:n:nindaka]]---defying nectar; [[synonyms:p:pañca]]-[[synonyms:v:vidha]]---five kinds of; [[synonyms:t:tikta]]---bitter; [[synonyms:j:jhāle]]---and pungent.

===== Translation =====

**There was sukhta, bitter melon mixed with all kinds of vegetables, defying the taste of nectar. There were five types of bitter and pungent sukhtas.**
