====== CC Madhya 13.153 ======

===== Text =====

> sei satī premavatī, premavān sei pati,
> viyoge ye vāñche priya-hite
> nā gaṇe āpana-duḥkha, vāñche priyajana-sukha,
> sei dui mile acirāte

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:s:satī]]---that chaste wife; [[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:v:vatī]]---full of love; [[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:v:vān]]---loving; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:p:pati]]---that husband; [[synonyms:v:viyoge]]---in separation; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---who; [[synonyms:v:vāñche]]---desire; [[synonyms:p:priya]]-[[synonyms:h:hite]]---for the welfare of the other; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:g:gaṇe]]---and do not care; [[synonyms:ā:āpana]]-[[synonyms:d:duḥkha]]---for personal unhappiness; [[synonyms:v:vāñche]]---desire; [[synonyms:p:priya]]-[[synonyms:j:jana]]-[[synonyms:s:sukha]]---the happiness of the dearmost beloved; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---those; [[synonyms:d:dui]]---two; [[synonyms:m:mile]]---meet; [[synonyms:a:acirāte]]---without delay.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'A loving, chaste wife and a loving husband who desire all welfare for each other in separation and do not care for personal happiness, desire only one another's well-being. Such a pair certainly meet again without delay.**
