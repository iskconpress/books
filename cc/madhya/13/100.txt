====== Cc. Madhya 13.100 ======

===== Text =====

> subhadrā-balarāmera hṛdaye ullāsa
> nṛtya dekhi' dui janāra śrī-mukhete hāsa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:subhadrā]]---of the goddess Subhadrā; [[synonyms:b:balarāmera]]---and of Balarāma; [[synonyms:h:hṛdaye]]---in the hearts; [[synonyms:u:ullāsa]]---ecstasy; [[synonyms:n:nṛtya]]---dancing; [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing; [[synonyms:d:dui]] [[synonyms:j:janāra]]---of the two persons; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:m:mukhete]]---in the beautiful mouths; [[synonyms:h:hāsa]]---smiling.

===== Translation =====

**The goddess of fortune, Subhadrā, and Lord Balarāma both felt great happiness and ecstasy within Their hearts. Indeed, They were seen smiling at the dancing.**
