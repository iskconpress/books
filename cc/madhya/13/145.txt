====== CC Madhya 13.145 ======

===== Text =====

> nā gaṇi āpana-duḥkha, dekhi' vrajeśvarī-mukha,
> vraja-janera hṛdaya vidare
> kibā māra' vraja-vāsī, kibā jīyāo vraje āsi',
> kena jīyāo duḥkha sahāibāre?

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:g:gaṇi]]---I do not care; [[synonyms:ā:āpana]]-[[synonyms:d:duḥkha]]---My personal unhappiness; [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing; [[synonyms:v:vrajeśvarī]]-[[synonyms:m:mukha]]---the face of mother Yaśodā; [[synonyms:v:vraja]]-[[synonyms:j:janera]]---of all the inhabitants of Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:h:hṛdaya]] [[synonyms:v:vidare]]---the hearts break; [[synonyms:k:kibā]]---whether; [[synonyms:m:māra']] [[synonyms:v:vraja]]-[[synonyms:v:vāsī]]---You want to kill the inhabitants of Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:k:kibā]]---or; [[synonyms:j:jīyāo]]---You want to keep their lives; [[synonyms:v:vraje]] [[synonyms:ā:āsi']]---coming in Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:k:kena]]---why; [[synonyms:j:jīyāo]]---You let them live; [[synonyms:d:duḥkha]] [[synonyms:s:sahāibāre]]---just to cause to suffer unhappiness.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'I do not care for My personal unhappiness, but when I see the morose face of Your mother Yaśodā and the hearts of all the inhabitants of Vṛndāvana breaking because of You, I wonder whether You want to kill them all. Is it that You want to enliven them by coming there? Why is it You are simply keeping them alive in a state of suffering?**
