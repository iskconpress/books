====== Cc. Madhya 16.269 ======

===== Text =====

> bhālata' kahila,---mora eta loka saṅge
> loka dekhi' kahibe more---'ei eka ḍhaṅge'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:bhālata']] [[synonyms:k:kahila]]---he has spoken very well; [[synonyms:m:mora]]---of Me; [[synonyms:e:eta]]---so much; [[synonyms:l:loka]]---crowd; [[synonyms:s:saṅge]]---in the company; [[synonyms:l:loka]]---the people; [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing; [[synonyms:k:kahibe]] [[synonyms:m:more]]---will speak about Me; [[synonyms:e:ei]]---this; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:ḍ:ḍhaṅge]]---imposter.

===== Translation =====

**"I decided that Sanātana had spoken very well. I was certainly being followed by a large crowd, and when people would see so many men, they would surely rebuke Me, saying, 'Here is another imposter.'**
