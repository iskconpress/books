====== CC Madhya 16.140 ======

===== Text =====

> āmāra saṅge rahite cāha,---vāñcha nija-sukha
> tomāra dui dharma yāya,---āmāra haya 'duḥkha'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:āmāra]] [[synonyms:s:saṅge]]---with Me; [[synonyms:r:rahite]]---to remain; [[synonyms:c:cāha]]---you want; [[synonyms:v:vāñcha]]---you desire; [[synonyms:n:nija]]-[[synonyms:s:sukha]]---your own sense gratification; [[synonyms:t:tomāra]]---your; [[synonyms:d:dui]] [[synonyms:d:dharma]]---two principles; [[synonyms:y:yāya]]---go away; [[synonyms:ā:āmāra]]---of Me; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---there is; [[synonyms:d:duḥkha]]---unhappiness.

===== Translation =====

**"Your wanting to go with Me is simply a desire for sense gratification. In this way, you are breaking two religious principles, and because of this I am very unhappy.**
