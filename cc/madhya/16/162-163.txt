====== CC Madhya 16.162-163 ======

===== Text =====

> prabhura sei adabhuta caritra dekhiyā
> hindu-cara kahe sei yavana-pāśa giyā
>
> 'eka sannyāsī āila jagannātha ha-ite
> aneka siddha-puruṣa haya tāṅhāra sahite

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---that; [[synonyms:a:adabhutacaritra]]---wonderful characteristics; [[synonyms:d:dekhiyā]]---seeing; [[synonyms:h:hindu]]-[[synonyms:c:cara]]---the Hindu spy; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---says; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---that; [[synonyms:y:yavana]]-[[synonyms:p:pāśa]] [[synonyms:g:giyā]]---going to the Mohammedan king; [[synonyms:e:eka]] [[synonyms:s:sannyāsi]]---one mendicant; [[synonyms:ā:āila]]---has come; [[synonyms:j:jagannātha]] [[synonyms:h:ha]]-[[synonyms:i:ite]]---from Jagannātha Purī; [[synonyms:a:aneka]]---many; [[synonyms:s:siddha]]-[[synonyms:p:puruṣa]]---liberated persons; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---are; [[synonyms:t:tāṅhāra]] [[synonyms:s:sahite]]---with Him.

===== Translation =====

**The Mohammedan spy saw the wonderful characteristics of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, and when he returned to the Mohammedan governor, he told him, "A mendicant has come from Jagannātha Purī with many liberated persons.**
