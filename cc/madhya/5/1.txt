====== CC Madhya 5.1 ======

===== Text =====

> padbhyāṁ calan yaḥ pratimā-svarūpo
> brahmaṇya-devo hi śatāha-gamyam
> deśaṁ yayau vipra-kṛte 'dbhutehaṁ
> taṁ sākṣi-gopālam ahaṁ nato 'smi

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:padbhyām]]---by the two legs; [[synonyms:c:calan]]---walking; [[synonyms:y:yaḥ]]---one who; [[synonyms:p:pratimā]]---of the Deity; [[synonyms:s:svarūpaḥ]]---in the form; [[synonyms:b:brahmaṇya]]-[[synonyms:d:devaḥ]]---the Supreme Lord of brahminical culture; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:ś:śata]]-[[synonyms:ā:āha]]---in one hundred days; [[synonyms:g:gamyam]]---to be passed over; [[synonyms:d:deśam]]---the country; [[synonyms:y:yayau]]---went; [[synonyms:v:vipra]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛte]]---for the benefit of a //brāhmaṇa//; [[synonyms:a:adbhuta]]---most wonderful; [[synonyms:ī:īham]]---activity; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---unto that; [[synonyms:s:sākṣi]]-[[synonyms:g:gopālam]]---the Gopāla known as the witness Gopāla; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:n:nataḥ]] [[synonyms:a:asmi]]---offer respectful obeisances.

===== Translation =====

**I offer my respectful obeisances unto the Supreme Personality of God [brahmaṇya-deva], who appeared as Sākṣi-gopāla to benefit a brāhmaṇa. For one hundred days He traveled through the country, walking on His own legs. Thus His activities are wonderful.**
