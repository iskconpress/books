====== CC Madhya 10.157 ======

===== Text =====

> anyere anya kaha, nāhi tomāra jñāna
> bhāratī-gosāñi kene paribena cāma

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:anyere]]---another; [[synonyms:a:anya]] [[synonyms:k:kaha]]---you talk of someone else; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---there is not; [[synonyms:t:tomāra]]---your; [[synonyms:j:jñāna]]---knowledge; [[synonyms:b:bhāratī]]---Brahmānanda Bhāratī; [[synonyms:g:gosāñi]]---My spiritual master; [[synonyms:k:kene]]---why; [[synonyms:p:paribena]]---should wear; [[synonyms:c:cāma]]---skin.

===== Translation =====

**"You must be talking of someone else, for this is surely not Brahmānanda Bhāratī. You simply have no knowledge. Why should Brahmānanda Bhāratī wear a deerskin?"**
