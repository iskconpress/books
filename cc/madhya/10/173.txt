====== CC Madhya 10.173 ======

===== Text =====

> guru-śiṣya-nyāye satya śiṣyera parājaya
> bhāratī kahe,-eho nahe, anya hetu haya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:guru]]-[[synonyms:ś:śiṣya]]-[[synonyms:n:nyāye]]---when there is a logical argument between the spiritual master and the disciple; [[synonyms:s:satya]]---certainly; [[synonyms:ś:śiṣyera]]---of the disciple; [[synonyms:p:parājaya]]---defeat; [[synonyms:b:bhāratī]] [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---Brahmānanda Bhāratī said; [[synonyms:e:eho]] [[synonyms:n:nahe]]---in this case it is not the fact; [[synonyms:a:anya]] [[synonyms:h:hetu]]---another cause; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---there is.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu thus posed Himself as a disciple and accepted Brahmānanda Bhāratī as His spiritual master. He then said, "The disciple is certainly defeated in an argument with the spiritual master."**


**Brahmānanda Bhāratī immediately countered these words, saying, "This is not the cause of Your defeat. There is another cause.**
