====== Cc. Madhya 17.80 ======

===== Text =====

> mūkaṁ karoti vācālaṁ
> paṅguṁ laṅghayate girim
> yat-kṛpā tam ahaṁ vande
> paramānanda-mādhavam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mūkam]]---a person who cannot speak; [[synonyms:k:karoti]]---makes; [[synonyms:v:vācālam]]---an eloquent speaker; [[synonyms:p:paṅgum]]---a person who cannot even walk; [[synonyms:l:laṅghayate]]---causes to cross over; [[synonyms:g:girim]]---the mountain; [[synonyms:y:yat]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛpā]]---whose mercy; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---unto Him; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:v:vande]]---offer obeisances; [[synonyms:p:parama]]-[[synonyms:ā:ānanda]]---the transcendentally blissful; [[synonyms:m:mādhavam]]---Supreme Personality of Godhead.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The Supreme Personality of Godhead has the form of sac-cid-ānanda vigraha, transcendental bliss, knowledge and eternity. I offer my respectful obeisances unto He who turns the dumb into eloquent speakers and enables the lame to cross mountains. Such is the mercy of the Lord.' "**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from the //Bhāvārtha-dīpikā// commentary on //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// [[books:sb:1:1:1|(1.1.1)]].
