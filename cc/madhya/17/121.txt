====== Cc. Madhya 17.121 ======

===== Text =====

> 'vedānta' śravaṇa kara, nā yāiha tāṅra pāśa
> ucchṛṅkhala-loka-saṅge dui-loka-nāśa"

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vedānta]]---the philosophy of Vedānta; [[synonyms:ś:śravaṇa]] [[synonyms:k:kara]]---go on hearing; [[synonyms:n:nā]]---do not; [[synonyms:y:yāiha]]---go; [[synonyms:t:tāṅra]] [[synonyms:p:pāśa]]---near Him; [[synonyms:u:ucchṛṅkhala]]---upstart; [[synonyms:l:loka]]---people; [[synonyms:s:saṅge]]---in the association of; [[synonyms:d:dui]]-[[synonyms:l:loka]]-[[synonyms:n:nāśa]]---destruction in this world and the next.

===== Translation =====

**"Do not go to see Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu. Just continue hearing Vedānta. If you associate with upstarts, you will be lost in this world and in the next."**

===== Purport =====

The word //ucchṛṅkhala,// meaning "whimsical," is significant in this verse. In //Bhagavad-gītā// [[books:bg:16:23|(Bg. 16.23)]], Lord Kṛṣṇa Himself says:

> yaḥ śāstra-vidhim utsṛjya
> vartate kāma-kārataḥ
> na sa siddhim avāpnoti
> na sukhaṁ na parāṁ gatim

If one acts whimsically and does not follow the śāstric principles, he will never attain perfection, happiness or the spiritual world.
