====== Cc. Madhya 17.36 ======

===== Text =====

> dhanyāḥ sma mūḍha-matayo 'pi hariṇya etā
> yā nanda-nandanam upātta-vicitra-veśam
> ākarṇya veṇu-raṇitaṁ saha-kṛṣṇa-sārāḥ
> pūjāṁ dadhur viracitāṁ praṇayāvalokaiḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:dhanyāḥ]]---fortunate, blessed; [[synonyms:s:sma]]---certainly; [[synonyms:m:mūḍha]]-[[synonyms:m:matayaḥ]]---foolish, without good sense; [[synonyms:a:api]]---although; [[synonyms:h:hariṇyaḥ]]---she-deer; [[synonyms:e:etāḥ]]---these; [[synonyms:y:yāḥ]]---who; [[synonyms:n:nanda]]-[[synonyms:n:nandanam]]---the son of Mahārāja Nanda; [[synonyms:u:upātta]]-[[synonyms:v:vicitra]]-[[synonyms:v:veśam]]---dressed very attractively; [[synonyms:ā:ākarṇya]]---hearing; [[synonyms:v:veṇu]]-[[synonyms:r:raṇitam]]---the sound of His flute; [[synonyms:s:saha]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:s:sārāḥ]]---accompanied by the black deer (their husbands); [[synonyms:p:pūjām]] [[synonyms:d:dadhuḥ]]---they worshiped; [[synonyms:v:viracitām]]---performed; [[synonyms:p:praṇaya]]-[[synonyms:a:avalokaiḥ]]---by their affectionate glances.

===== Translation =====

**"Blessed are all these foolish deer because they have approached the son of Mahārāja Nanda, who is gorgeously dressed and is playing on His flute. Indeed, both the does and the bucks worship the Lord with looks of love and affection."**

===== Purport =====

This is a verse from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.21.11) spoken by the //gopīs// of Vṛndāvana.
