~~Title:Caitanya-caritāmṛta, Madhya 17.1~~

====== CC Madhya 17.1 ======

===== Text =====

> gacchan vṛndāvanaṁ gauro
> vyāghrebhaiṇa-khagān vane
> premonmattān sahonnṛtyān
> vidadhe kṛṣṇa-jalpinaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:gacchan]]---going; [[synonyms:v:vṛndāvanam]]---to Vṛndāvana-dhāma; [[synonyms:g:gauraḥ]]---Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:v:vyāghra]]---tigers; [[synonyms:i:ibha]]---elephants; [[synonyms:e:eṇa]]---deer; [[synonyms:k:khagān]]---and birds; [[synonyms:v:vane]]---in the forest; [[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:u:unmattān]]---maddened by ecstatic love; [[synonyms:s:saha]]---with; [[synonyms:u:unnṛtyān]]---dancing; [[synonyms:v:vidadhe]]---made; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa's name; [[synonyms:j:jalpinaḥ]]---chanting.

===== Translation =====

**On His way to Vṛndāvana, Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu passed through the forest of Jhārikhaṇḍa and made all the tigers, elephants, deer and birds dance and chant the Hare Kṛṣṇa mahā-mantra. Thus all these animals were overwhelmed by ecstatic love.**
