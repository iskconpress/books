====== CC Madhya 4.60 ======

===== Text =====

> amaṅgalā dūra kari' karāila snāna
> bahu taila diyā kaila śrī-aṅga cikkaṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:amaṅgalā]]---all inauspiciousness; [[synonyms:d:dūra]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---driving away; [[synonyms:k:karāila]]---caused; [[synonyms:s:snāna]]---bathing; [[synonyms:b:bahu]]---a great quantity; [[synonyms:t:taila]]---oil; [[synonyms:d:diyā]]---applying; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---made; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅga]]---the body; [[synonyms:c:cikkaṇa]]---glossy.

===== Translation =====

**After all inauspicious things were driven away by the chanting of the mantra, the Deity's bathing ceremony started. First the Deity was massaged with a large quantity of oil, so that His body became very glossy.**
