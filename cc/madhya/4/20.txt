====== Cc. Madhya 4.20 ======

===== Text =====

> pūrve mādhava-purīra lāgi' kṣīra kaila curi
> ataeva nāma haila 'kṣīra-corā hari'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:pūrve]]---formerly; [[synonyms:m:mādhava]]-[[synonyms:p:purīra]] [[synonyms:l:lāgi']]---for Mādhavendra Purī; [[synonyms:k:kṣīra]]---sweet rice; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---did; [[synonyms:c:curi]]---steal; [[synonyms:a:ataeva]]---therefore; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---the name; [[synonyms:h:haila]]---became; [[synonyms:k:kṣīra]]-[[synonyms:c:corā]] [[synonyms:h:hari]]---the Lord who stole a pot of sweet rice.

===== Translation =====

**Formerly the Deity had stolen a pot of sweet rice for Mādhavendra Purī; therefore He became very famous as the Lord who stole the sweet rice.**
