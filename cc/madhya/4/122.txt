====== Cc. Madhya 4.122 ======

===== Text =====

> ārati dekhiyā purī kaila namaskāra
> bāhire āilā, kāre kichu nā kahila āra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:ārati]] [[synonyms:d:dekhiyā]]---after seeing the //ārati//; [[synonyms:p:purī]]---Mādhavendra Purī; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---offered; [[synonyms:n:namaskāra]]---obeisances; [[synonyms:b:bāhire]] [[synonyms:ā:āilā]]---he went out; [[synonyms:k:kāre]]---to anyone; [[synonyms:k:kichu]]---anything; [[synonyms:n:nā]]---not; [[synonyms:k:kahila]]---say; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---more.

===== Translation =====

**After the ārati was finished, Mādhavendra Purī offered his obeisances to the Deity and then left the temple. He did not say anything more to anyone.**
