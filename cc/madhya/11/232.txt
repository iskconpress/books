====== CC Madhya 11.232 ======

===== Text =====

> darśane āveśa tāṅra dekhi' mātra jāne
> kemane caudike dekhe,-ihā nāhi jāne

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:darśane]]---while looking over; [[synonyms:ā:āveśa]]---emotional ecstasy; [[synonyms:t:tāṅra]]---His; [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing; [[synonyms:m:mātra]] [[synonyms:j:jāne]]---only knows; [[synonyms:k:kemane]]---how; [[synonyms:c:cau]]-[[synonyms:d:dike]]---on four sides; [[synonyms:d:dekhe]]---He sees; [[synonyms:i:ihā]] [[synonyms:n:nāhi]] [[synonyms:j:jāne]]---one does not know.

===== Translation =====

**Everyone who saw Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu could understand that He was performing a miracle, but they did not know how it was that He could see on all four sides.**
