====== CC Madhya 11.216 ======

===== Text =====

> aṣṭa mṛdaṅga bāje, batriśa karatāla
> hari-dhvani kare sabe, bale-bhāla, bhāla

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:aṣṭa]] [[synonyms:m:mṛdaṅga]]---eight //mṛdaṅgas//; [[synonyms:b:bāje]]---sounded; [[synonyms:b:batriśa]]---thirty-two; [[synonyms:k:karatāla]]---cymbals; [[synonyms:h:hari]]-[[synonyms:d:dhvani]]---vibrating the transcendental sound; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:s:sabe]]---every one of them; [[synonyms:b:bale]]---says; [[synonyms:b:bhāla]] [[synonyms:b:bhāla]]---very good, very good.

===== Translation =====

**In the four groups there were eight mṛdaṅgas and thirty-two cymbals. All together they began to vibrate the transcendental sound, and everyone said, "Very good! Very good!"**
