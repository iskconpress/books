====== Cc. Madhya 11.119 ======

===== Text =====

> tabe rājā aṭṭālikā haite talete āilā
> kāśī-miśra, paḍichā-pātra, duṅhe ānāilā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tabe]]---thereafter; [[synonyms:r:rājā]]---the King; [[synonyms:a:aṭṭālikā]] [[synonyms:h:haite]]---from the top of the palace; [[synonyms:t:talete]]---to the ground; [[synonyms:ā:āilā]]---came down; [[synonyms:k:kāśī]]-[[synonyms:m:miśra]]---of the name Kāśī Miśra; [[synonyms:p:paḍichā]]-[[synonyms:p:pātra]]---the inspector of the temple; [[synonyms:d:duṅhe]]---both of them; [[synonyms:ā:ānāilā]]---called for.

===== Translation =====

**After this, King Pratāparudra came down from the top of his palace to the ground and called for Kāśī Miśra and the inspector of the temple.**
