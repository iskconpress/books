====== CC Madhya 11.34 ======

===== Text =====

> cāri gosāñira kaila rāya caraṇa vandana
> yathā-yogya saba bhaktera karila milana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:c:cāri]] [[synonyms:g:gosāñira]]---of the four //gosāñis,// or spiritual masters; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---did; [[synonyms:r:rāya]]---Rāmānanda Rāya; [[synonyms:c:caraṇa]] [[synonyms:v:vandana]]---worshiping the lotus feet; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]-[[synonyms:y:yogya]]---as it is befitting; [[synonyms:s:saba]]---all; [[synonyms:b:bhaktera]]---of the devotees; [[synonyms:k:karila]]---did; [[synonyms:m:milana]]---meeting.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Rāmānanda Rāya therefore offered his obeisances to all the Lord's devotees, in particular to the four spiritual masters. Thus Rāmānanda Rāya suitably met all the devotees.**

===== Purport =====

The four spiritual masters referred to in this verse are Paramānanda Purī, Brahmānanda Bhāratī, Svarūpa Dāmodara and Lord Nityānanda.
