====== Cc. Madhya 15.210 ======

===== Text =====

> daśa-prakāra śāka, nimba-tikta-sukhta-jhola
> maricera jhāla, chānā-baḍā, baḍi ghola

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:daśa]]-[[synonyms:p:prakāra]] [[synonyms:ś:śāka]]---spinach of ten varieties; [[synonyms:n:nimba]]-[[synonyms:t:tikta]]-[[synonyms:s:sukhta]]-[[synonyms:j:jhola]]---a soup called //sukhta,// made with bitter //nimba// leaf; [[synonyms:m:maricera]] [[synonyms:j:jhāla]]---a pungent preparation made with black pepper; [[synonyms:c:chānā]]-[[synonyms:b:baḍā]]---a mild cake made of fried curd; [[synonyms:b:baḍi]] [[synonyms:g:ghola]]---buttermilk with small pieces of fried dhal.

===== Translation =====

**There were about ten kinds of spinach, a soup called sukhta, which was made with bitter nimba leaves, a pungent preparation made with black pepper, a mild cake made of fried curd, and buttermilk mixed with small fried pieces of dhal.**
