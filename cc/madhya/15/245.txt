====== CC Madhya 15.245 ======

===== Text =====

> hena-kāle 'amogha,'---bhaṭṭācāryera jāmātā
> kulīna, nindaka teṅho ṣāṭhī-kanyāra bhartā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:h:hena]]-[[synonyms:k:kāle]]---exactly at this time; [[synonyms:a:amogha]]---Amogha; [[synonyms:b:bhaṭṭācāryera]] [[synonyms:j:jāmātā]]---the son-in-law of Bhaṭṭācārya; [[synonyms:k:kulīna]]---of aristocratic birth; [[synonyms:n:nindaka]]---blasphemer; [[synonyms:t:teṅho]]---he; [[synonyms:ṣ:ṣāṭhī]]-[[synonyms:k:kanyāra]] [[synonyms:b:bhartā]]---the husband of Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya's daughter named Ṣāṭhī.

===== Translation =====

**At this time Bhaṭṭācārya had a son-in-law named Amogha, who was the husband of his daughter named Ṣāṭhī. Although born in an aristocratic brāhmaṇa family, this Amogha was a great faultfinder and blasphemer.**
