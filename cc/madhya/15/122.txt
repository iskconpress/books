====== Cc. Madhya 15.122 ======

===== Text =====

> hena-kāle eka mayūra-pucchera āḍānī
> rāja-śiropari dhare eka sevaka āni'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:h:hena]]-[[synonyms:k:kāle]]---at this time; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:m:mayūra]]-[[synonyms:p:pucchera]]---of peacock feathers; [[synonyms:ā:āḍānī]]---fan; [[synonyms:r:rāja]]-[[synonyms:ś:śira]]-[[synonyms:u:upari]]---above the head of the King; [[synonyms:d:dhare]]---holds; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:s:sevaka]]---servant; [[synonyms:ā:āni']]---bringing.

===== Translation =====

**"While the King and Mukunda dāsa were conversing, a servant brought a fan made of peacock feathers to shade the head of the King from the sun. Consequently he held the fan above the King's head.**
