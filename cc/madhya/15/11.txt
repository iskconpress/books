====== Cc. Madhya 15.11 ======

===== Text =====

> "yo 'si so 'si namo 'stu te" ei mantra paḍe
> mukha-vādya kari' prabhu hāsāya ācāryere

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yaḥ]] [[synonyms:a:asi]]---whatever You are; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]] [[synonyms:a:asi]]---so You are; [[synonyms:n:namaḥ]] [[synonyms:a:astu]] [[synonyms:t:te]]---I offer My respects unto You; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:m:mantra]] [[synonyms:p:paḍe]]---chants this //mantra//; [[synonyms:m:mukha]]-[[synonyms:v:vādya]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---making a sound within the mouth; [[synonyms:p:prabhu]]---Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:h:hāsāya]]---causes to laugh; [[synonyms:ā:ācāryere]]---Advaita Ācārya.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu would worship Advaita Ācārya by chanting the mantra, "Whatever You are, You are---but I offer My respects unto You." In addition, the Lord would make some sounds within His mouth that would make Advaita Ācārya laugh.**
