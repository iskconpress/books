====== CC Madhya 15.214 ======

===== Text =====

> bhṛṣṭa-māṣa-mudga-sūpa amṛta nindaya
> madhurāmla, baḍāmlādi amla pāṅca chaya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:bhṛṣṭa]]---fried; [[synonyms:m:māṣa]]---urd //dhal//; [[synonyms:m:mudga]]---mung //dhal//; [[synonyms:s:sūpa]]---soup; [[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---nectar; [[synonyms:n:nindaya]]---defeating; [[synonyms:m:madhura]]-[[synonyms:a:amla]]---sweet chutney; [[synonyms:b:baḍa]]-[[synonyms:a:amla]]---sour preparation made with fried //dhal//; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---and so on; [[synonyms:a:amla]]---sour; [[synonyms:p:pāṅca]] [[synonyms:c:chaya]]---five or six kinds.

===== Translation =====

**There was a soup made with fried urad dhal and mung dhal, defeating nectar. There were also sweet chutney and five or six kinds of sour preparations, beginning with baḍāmla.**
