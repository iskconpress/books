====== Cc. Madhya 18.191 ======

===== Text =====

> sac-cid-ānanda-deha, pūrṇa-brahma-svarūpa
> 'sarvātmā', 'sarvajña', nitya sarvādi-svarūpa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sat]]-[[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:ā:ānanda]]-[[synonyms:d:deha]]---transcendental, blissful, spiritual body; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]-[[synonyms:b:brahma]]-[[synonyms:s:svarūpa]]---the identification of the Absolute Truth; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmā]]---all-pervading; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:j:jña]]---omniscient; [[synonyms:n:nitya]]---eternal; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---the origin of everything; [[synonyms:s:svarūpa]]---the real form of the Lord.

===== Translation =====

**"According to the Koran, the Lord has a supreme, blissful, transcendental body. He is the Absolute Truth, the all-pervading, omniscient and eternal being. He is the origin of everything.**
