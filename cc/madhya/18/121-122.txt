====== CC Madhya 18.121-122 ======

===== Text =====

> strī-bāla-vṛddha, āra 'caṇḍāla, 'yavana'
> yei tomāra eka-bāra pāya daraśana
>
> kṛṣṇa-nāma laya, nāce hañā unmatta
> ācārya ha-ila sei, tārila jagata

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:strī]]---women; [[synonyms:b:bāla]]---children; [[synonyms:v:vṛddha]]---old men; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:c:caṇḍāla]]---the lowest of men; [[synonyms:y:yavana]]---persons who eat meat; [[synonyms:y:yei]]---anyone who; [[synonyms:t:tomāra]]---Your; [[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:b:bāra]]---once; [[synonyms:p:pāya]] [[synonyms:d:daraśana]]---gets the sight; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:n:nāma]]---the holy name of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:l:laya]]---chants; [[synonyms:n:nāce]]---dances; [[synonyms:h:hañā]] [[synonyms:u:unmatta]]---like a madman; [[synonyms:ā:ācārya]] [[synonyms:h:ha]]-[[synonyms:i:ila]]---becomes a spiritual master; [[synonyms:s:sei]]---that man; [[synonyms:t:tārila]] [[synonyms:j:jagata]]---delivers the whole world.

===== Translation =====

**"If even women, children, old men, meat-eaters or members of the lowest caste can see You even once, they immediately chant the holy name of Kṛṣṇa, dance like madmen and become spiritual masters capable of delivering the whole world.**
