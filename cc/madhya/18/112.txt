====== CC Madhya 18.112 ======

===== Text =====

> sannyāsī---cit-kaṇa jīva, kiraṇa-kaṇa-sama
> ṣaḍ-aiśvarya-pūrṇa kṛṣṇa haya sūryopama

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sannyāsī]]---a person in the renounced order of life; [[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:k:kaṇa]] [[synonyms:j:jīva]]---a small fragmental living being; [[synonyms:k:kiraṇa]]---of sunshine; [[synonyms:k:kaṇa]]---small particle; [[synonyms:s:sama]]---like; [[synonyms:ṣ:ṣaṭ]]-[[synonyms:a:aiśvarya]]-[[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]---full in six opulences; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---is; [[synonyms:s:sūrya]]-[[synonyms:u:upama]]---compared to the sun.

===== Translation =====

**"A sannyāsī in the renounced order is certainly part and parcel of the complete whole, just as a shining molecular particle of sunshine is part and parcel of the sun itself. Kṛṣṇa is like the sun, full of six opulences, but the living entity is only a fragment of the complete whole.**
