====== CC Madhya 22.50 ======

===== Text =====

> yadṛcchayā mat-kathādau
> jāta-śraddhas tu yaḥ pumān
> na nirviṇṇo nātisakto
> bhakti-yogo 'sya siddhidaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yadṛcchayā]]---by some good fortune; [[synonyms:m:mat]]-[[synonyms:k:kathā]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādau]]---in talk about Me; [[synonyms:j:jāta]]-[[synonyms:ś:śraddhaḥ]]---has awakened his attraction; [[synonyms:t:tu]]---but; [[synonyms:y:yaḥ]] [[synonyms:p:pumān]]---a person who; [[synonyms:n:na]] [[synonyms:n:nirviṇṇaḥ]]---not falsely detached; [[synonyms:n:na]] [[synonyms:a:atisaktaḥ]]---not attached to material existence; [[synonyms:b:bhakti]]-[[synonyms:y:yogaḥ]]---the process of devotional service; [[synonyms:a:asya]]---for such a person; [[synonyms:s:siddhi]]-[[synonyms:d:daḥ]]---bestowing perfection.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Somehow or other, if one is attracted to talks about Me and has faith in the instructions I have set forth in Bhagavad-gītā, and if one is actually detached from material things and material existence, his dormant love for Me will be awakened by devotional service.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (11.20.8) was spoken by Kṛṣṇa at the time of His departure from this material world. It was spoken to Uddhava.
