====== CC Madhya 22.136 ======

===== Text =====

> śrī-viṣṇoḥ śravaṇe parīkṣid abhavad vaiyāsakiḥ kīrtane
> prahlādaḥ smaraṇe tad-aṅghri-bhajane lakṣmīḥ pṛthuḥ pūjane
> akrūras tv abhivandane kapi-patir dāsye 'tha sakhye 'rjunaḥ
> sarvasvātma-nivedane balir abhūt kṛṣṇāptir eṣāṁ parā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:v:viṣṇoḥ]]---of Lord Śrī Viṣṇu; [[synonyms:ś:śravaṇe]]---in hearing; [[synonyms:p:parīkṣit]]---King Parīkṣit, known also as Viṣṇurāta, or one who is protected by Lord Viṣṇu; [[synonyms:a:abhavat]]---was; [[synonyms:v:vaiyāsakiḥ]]---Śukadeva Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:k:kīrtane]]---in reciting //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam//; [[synonyms:p:prahlādaḥ]]---Mahārāja Prahlāda; [[synonyms:s:smaraṇe]]---in remembering; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅghri]]---of Lord Viṣṇu's lotus feet; [[synonyms:b:bhajane]]---in serving; [[synonyms:l:lakṣmīḥ]]---the goddess of fortune; [[synonyms:p:pṛthuḥ]]---Mahārāja Pṛthu; [[synonyms:p:pūjane]]---in worshiping the Deity of the Lord; [[synonyms:a:akrūraḥ]]---Akrūra; [[synonyms:t:tu]]---but; [[synonyms:a:abhivandane]]---in offering prayers; [[synonyms:k:kapi]]-[[synonyms:p:patiḥ]]---Hanumānjī, or Vajrāṅgajī; [[synonyms:d:dāsye]]---in servitude to Lord Rāmacandra; [[synonyms:a:atha]]---moreover; [[synonyms:s:sakhye]]---in friendship; [[synonyms:a:arjunaḥ]]---Arjuna; [[synonyms:s:sarvasva]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātma]]-[[synonyms:n:nivedane]]---in fully dedicating oneself; [[synonyms:b:baliḥ]]---Mahārāja Bali; [[synonyms:a:abhūt]]---was; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:ā:āptiḥ]]---the achievement of the lotus feet of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:e:eṣām]]---of all of them; [[synonyms:p:parā]]---transcendental.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Mahārāja Parīkṣit attained the highest perfection, shelter at Lord Kṛṣṇa's lotus feet, simply by hearing about Lord Viṣṇu. Śukadeva Gosvāmī attained perfection simply by reciting Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam. Prahlāda Mahārāja attained perfection by remembering the Lord. The goddess of fortune attained perfection by massaging the transcendental legs of Mahā-Viṣṇu. Mahārāja Pṛthu attained perfection by worshiping the Deity, and Akrūra attained perfection by offering prayers unto the Lord. Vajrāṅgajī [Hanumān] attained perfection by rendering service to Lord Rāmacandra, and Arjuna attained perfection simply by being Kṛṣṇa's friend. Bali Mahārāja attained perfection by dedicating everything to the lotus feet of Kṛṣṇa.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse appears in the //Padyāvalī// (53) and the //Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu// (1.2.265).
