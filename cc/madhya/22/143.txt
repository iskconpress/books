====== CC Madhya 22.143 ======

===== Text =====

> ajñāne vā haya yadi 'pāpa' upasthita
> kṛṣṇa tāṅre śuddha kare, nā karāya prāyaścitta

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:ajñāne]]---by ignorance; [[synonyms:v:vā]]---or; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---there are; [[synonyms:y:yadi]]---if; [[synonyms:p:pāpa]]---sinful activities; [[synonyms:u:upasthita]]---present; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:t:tāṅre]]---him (the devotee); [[synonyms:ś:śuddha]] [[synonyms:k:kare]]---purifies; [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:k:karāya]]---does not cause; [[synonyms:p:prāyaścitta]]---atonement.

===== Translation =====

**"If, however, a devotee accidentally becomes involved in a sinful activity, Kṛṣṇa purifies him. He does not have to undergo the regulative form of atonement.**

===== Purport =====

Kṛṣṇa purifies from within as //caitya-guru,// the spiritual master within the heart. This is described in the following verse from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (11.5.42).
