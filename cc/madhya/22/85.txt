====== CC Madhya 22.85 ======

===== Text =====

> ata ātyantikaṁ kṣemaṁ
> pṛcchāmo bhavato 'naghāḥ
> saṁsāre 'smin kṣaṇārdho 'pi
> sat-saṅgaḥ sevadhir nṛṇām

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:ataḥ]]---therefore (due to the rareness of seeing pure devotees of the Lord); [[synonyms:ā:ātyantikam]]---supreme; [[synonyms:k:kṣemam]]---auspiciousness; [[synonyms:p:pṛcchāmaḥ]]---we are asking; [[synonyms:ā:ātyantikam]]---supreme; [[synonyms:k:kṣemam]]---auspiciousness; [[synonyms:p:pṛcchāmaḥ]]---we are asking; [[synonyms:b:bhavataḥ]]---you; [[synonyms:a:anaghāḥ]]---O sinless ones; [[synonyms:s:saṁsāre]]---in the material world; [[synonyms:a:asmin]]---this; [[synonyms:k:kṣaṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:ardhaḥ]]---lasting half a moment; [[synonyms:a:api]]---even; [[synonyms:s:sat]]-[[synonyms:s:saṅgaḥ]]---association with devotees; [[synonyms:s:sevadhiḥ]]---a treasure; [[synonyms:n:nṛṇām]]---for human society.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'O devotees! O you who are free from all sins! Let me inquire from you about that which is supremely auspicious for all living entities. Association with a pure devotee for even half a moment in this material world is the greatest treasure for human society.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (11.2.30).
