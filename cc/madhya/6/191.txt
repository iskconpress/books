====== CC Madhya 6.191 ======

===== Text =====

> 'bhaṭṭācārya', jāni---tumi sākṣāt bṛhaspati
> śāstra-vyākhyā karite aiche kāro nāhi śakti

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:bhaṭṭācārya]]---My dear Bhaṭṭācārya; [[synonyms:j:jāni]]---I know; [[synonyms:t:tumi]]---you; [[synonyms:s:sākṣāt]]---directly; [[synonyms:b:bṛhaspati]]---the learned priest of the demigods named Bṛhaspati; [[synonyms:ś:śāstra]]-[[synonyms:v:vyākhyā]]---explanation of the scriptures; [[synonyms:k:karite]]---to do; [[synonyms:a:aiche]]---such; [[synonyms:k:kāro]]---of anyone else; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---there is not; [[synonyms:ś:śakti]]---power.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said, "My dear Bhaṭṭācārya, you are exactly like Bṛhaspati, the priest of the heavenly kingdom. Indeed, no one within this world has the power to explain the scriptures in such a way.**
