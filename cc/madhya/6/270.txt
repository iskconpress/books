====== Cc. Madhya 6.270 ======

===== Text =====

> sālokya-sārṣṭi-sāmīpya-
> sārūpyaikatvam apy uta
> dīyamānaṁ na gṛhṇanti
> vinā mat-sevanaṁ janāḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sālokya]]---the liberation of living in the same planet as the Lord; [[synonyms:s:sārṣṭi]]---to have opulence exactly like that of the Lord; [[synonyms:s:sāmīpya]]---to associate always with the Lord; [[synonyms:s:sārūpya]]---to achieve a body like that of the Lord; [[synonyms:e:ekatvam]]---to merge into the body of the Lord; [[synonyms:a:api]]---although; [[synonyms:u:uta]]---it is said; [[synonyms:d:dīyamānam]]---being offered; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:g:gṛhṇanti]]---do accept; [[synonyms:v:vinā]]---without; [[synonyms:m:mat]]---My; [[synonyms:s:sevanam]]---service; [[synonyms:j:janāḥ]]---the pure devotees.

===== Translation =====

**Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya concluded, " 'Even though he is offered all kinds of liberation, the pure devotee does not accept them. He is fully satisfied engaging in the service of the Lord.' "**
