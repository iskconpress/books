====== CC Madhya 6.159 ======

===== Text =====

> ānandāṁśe 'hlādinī,' sad-aṁśe 'sandhinī'
> cid-aṁśe 'samvit', yāre jñāna kari māni

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:ānanda]]-[[synonyms:a:aṁśe]]---in the part of bliss; [[synonyms:h:hlādinī]]---the pleasure potency; [[synonyms:s:sat]]-[[synonyms:a:aṁśe]]---in the part of eternity; [[synonyms:s:sandhinī]]---the //sandhinī// potency; [[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:a:aṁśe]]---in the part of knowledge; [[synonyms:s:samvit]]---the //samvit// potency; [[synonyms:y:yāre]]---which; [[synonyms:j:jñāna]]---as knowledge; [[synonyms:k:kari]] [[synonyms:m:māni]]---we accept.

===== Translation =====

**"The three portions of the spiritual potency are called hlādinī [the bliss portion], sandhinī [the eternity portion] and samvit [the knowledge portion]. We accept knowledge of these as full knowledge of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.**

===== Purport =====

To acquire knowledge of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, one must take shelter of the //samvit// potency of the Supreme Lord.
