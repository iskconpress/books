====== CC Madhya 6.278 ======

===== Text =====

> yei bhaṭṭācārya pade paḍāya māyāvāde
> tāṅra aiche vākya sphure caitanya-prasāde

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yei]]---that; [[synonyms:b:bhaṭṭācārya]]---Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya; [[synonyms:p:paḍe]]---reads; [[synonyms:p:paḍāya]]---teaches; [[synonyms:m:māyāvāde]]---the philosophy of Māyāvāda impersonalism; [[synonyms:t:tāṅra]]---his; [[synonyms:a:aiche]]---such; [[synonyms:v:vākya]]---explanation; [[synonyms:s:sphure]]---manifested; [[synonyms:c:caitanya]]-[[synonyms:p:prasāde]]---by the mercy of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.

===== Translation =====

**Indeed, that very person who was accustomed to reading and teaching Māyāvāda philosophy was now even hating the word "mukti." This was possible only by the mercy of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.**
