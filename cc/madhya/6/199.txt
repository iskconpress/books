====== CC Madhya 6.199 ======

===== Text =====

> śuni' bhaṭṭācāryera mane haila camatkāra
> prabhuke kṛṣṇa jāni' kare āpanā dhikkāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śuni']]---hearing this; [[synonyms:b:bhaṭṭācāryera]]---of Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya; [[synonyms:m:mane]]---in the mind; [[synonyms:h:haila]]---there was; [[synonyms:c:camatkāra]]---wonder; [[synonyms:p:prabhuke]]---Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:j:jāni']]---accepting as; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:ā:āpanā]]---himself; [[synonyms:d:dhikkāra]]---condemnation.

===== Translation =====

**Upon hearing Caitanya Mahāprabhu's explanation of the ātmārāma verse, Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya was struck with wonder. He then understood Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu to be Kṛṣṇa in person, and he thus condemned himself in the following words.**
