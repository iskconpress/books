====== CC Madhya 6.149 ======

===== Text =====

> aho bhāgyam aho bhāgyaṁ
> nanda-gopa-vrajaukasām
> yan-mitraṁ paramānandaṁ
> pūrṇaṁ brahma sanātanam

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:aho]]---what great; [[synonyms:b:bhāgyam]]---fortune; [[synonyms:a:aho]]---what great; [[synonyms:b:bhāgyam]]---fortune; [[synonyms:n:nanda]]---of Mahārāja Nanda; [[synonyms:g:gopa]]---of other cowherd men; [[synonyms:v:vraja]]-[[synonyms:o:okasām]]---of the inhabitants of Vrajabhūmi; [[synonyms:y:yat]]---of whom; [[synonyms:m:mitram]]---friend; [[synonyms:p:parama]]-[[synonyms:ā:ānandam]]---the supreme bliss; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇam]]---complete; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]---the Absolute Truth; [[synonyms:s:sanātanam]]---eternal.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'How greatly fortunate are Nanda Mahārāja, the cowherd men and all the inhabitants of Vrajabhūmi! There is no limit to their fortune, because the Absolute Truth, the source of transcendental bliss, the eternal Supreme Brahman, has become their friend.'**

===== Purport =====

This quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.14.32) is spoken by Lord Brahmā.
