====== CC Madhya 6.102 ======

===== Text =====

> iti dvāpara urv-īśa
> stuvanti jagad-īśvaram
> nānā-tantra-vidhānena
> kalāv api tathā śṛṇu

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:d:dvāpare]]---in Dvāpara-yuga; [[synonyms:u:uru]]-[[synonyms:ī:īśa]]---O King; [[synonyms:s:stuvanti]]---offer prayers; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:ī:īśvaram]]---unto the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:n:nānā]]---various; [[synonyms:t:tantra]]---of the supplementary Vedic literature; [[synonyms:v:vidhānena]]---by regulative principles; [[synonyms:k:kalau]]---in the Age of Kali; [[synonyms:a:api]]---certainly; [[synonyms:t:tathā]]---so also; [[synonyms:ś:śṛṇu]]---hear.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'In the Age of Kali, as well as in Dvāpara-yuga, the people offer prayers to the Supreme Personality of Godhead by various mantras and observe the regulative principles of the supplementary Vedic literature. Now please hear of this from me.**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (11.5.31).
