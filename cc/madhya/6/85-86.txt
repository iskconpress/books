====== Cc. Madhya 6.85-86 ======

===== Text =====

> yadyapi jagad-guru tumi---śāstra-jñānavān
> pṛthivīte nāhi paṇḍita tomāra samāna
>
> īśvarera kṛpā-leśa nāhika tomāte
> ataeva īśvara-tattva nā pāra jānite

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yadyapi]]---although; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:g:guru]]---a teacher of many disciples; [[synonyms:t:tumi]]---you; [[synonyms:ś:śāstra]]-[[synonyms:j:jñānavān]]---well versed in Vedic knowledge; [[synonyms:p:pṛthivīte]]---on this earth; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---there is not; [[synonyms:p:paṇḍita]]---a learned scholar; [[synonyms:t:tomāra]]---your; [[synonyms:s:samāna]]---equal; [[synonyms:ī:īśvarera]]---of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:k:kṛpā]]---of mercy; [[synonyms:l:leśa]]---a bit; [[synonyms:n:nāhika]]---there is not; [[synonyms:t:tomāte]]---on you; [[synonyms:a:ataeva]]---therefore; [[synonyms:ī:īśvara]]-[[synonyms:t:tattva]]---the Absolute Truth (the Supreme personality of Godhead); [[synonyms:n:nā]] [[synonyms:p:pāra]]---are not able; [[synonyms:j:jānite]]---to know.

===== Translation =====

**Gopīnātha Ācārya then addressed Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya: "You are a great scholar and a teacher of may disciples. Indeed, there is no other scholar like you on earth. Nonetheless, because you are bereft of even a pinch of the Lord's mercy, you cannot understand Him, even though He is present in your home.**
