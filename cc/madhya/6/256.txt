====== Cc. Madhya 6.256 ======

===== Text =====

> ei dui śloka---bhakta-kaṇṭhe ratna-hāra
> sārvabhaumera kīrti ghoṣe ḍhakkā-vādyākāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:d:dui]] [[synonyms:ś:śloka]]---these two verses; [[synonyms:b:bhakta]]-[[synonyms:k:kaṇṭhe]]---on the necks of the devotees; [[synonyms:r:ratna]]-[[synonyms:h:hāra]]---pearl necklaces; [[synonyms:s:sārvabhaumera]]---of Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya; [[synonyms:k:kīrti]]---reputation; [[synonyms:g:ghoṣe]]---declare; [[synonyms:ḍ:ḍhakkā]]---of a drum; [[synonyms:v:vādya]]---of the sound; [[synonyms:ā:ākāra]]---in the form.

===== Translation =====

**These two verses composed by Sārvabhauma Bhaṭṭācārya will always declare his name and fame as loudly as a pounding drum because they have become pearl necklaces around the necks of all devotees.**
