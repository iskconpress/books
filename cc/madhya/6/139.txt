====== CC Madhya 6.139 ======

===== Text =====

> veda-purāṇe kahe brahma-nirūpaṇa
> sei brahma---bṛhad-vastu, īśvara-lakṣaṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:veda]]-[[synonyms:p:purāṇe]]---in the //Vedas// and the //Purāṇas//; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---it is stated; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]-[[synonyms:n:nirūpaṇa]]---explaining the Supreme; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:b:brahma]]---that Supreme; [[synonyms:b:bṛhat]]-[[synonyms:v:vastu]]---the greatest; [[synonyms:ī:īśvara]]-[[synonyms:l:lakṣaṇa]]---feature of the Supreme Personality.

===== Translation =====

**"All the Vedas and literature that strictly follows the Vedic principles explain that the Supreme Brahman is the Absolute Truth, the greatest of all, and a feature of the Supreme Lord.**

===== Purport =====

The greatest of everything is Śrī Kṛṣṇa. Lord Kṛṣṇa states in the //Bhagavad-gītā// [[books:bg:15:15|(Bg. 15.15)]], //vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyaḥ:// "By all the //Vedas,// I am to be known.") In //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// it is said that the Absolute Truth is understood in three phases-namely, Brahman, Paramātmā and Bhagavān, the Supreme Personality of Godhead (//brahmeti paramātmeti bhagavān iti śabdyate//). Thus the Supreme Personality of Godhead is the last word in understanding the Absolute Truth, Brahman.
