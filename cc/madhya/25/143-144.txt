====== Cc. Madhya 25.143-144 ======

===== Text =====

> artho 'yaṁ brahma-sūtrāṇāṁ
> bhāratārtha-vinirṇayaḥ
> gāyatrī-bhāṣya-rūpo 'sau
> vedārtha-paribṛṁhitaḥ
>
> purāṇānāṁ sāma-rūpaḥ
> sākṣād-bhagavatoditaḥ
> dvādaśa-skandha-yukto 'yaṁ
> śata-viccheda-saṁyutaḥ
> grantho 'ṣṭādaśa-sāhasraḥ
> śrīmad-bhāgavatābhidhaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:artaḥ]] [[synonyms:a:ayam]]---this is the meaning; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]-[[synonyms:s:sūtrāṇām]]---of the codes of //Vedānta-sūtra//; [[synonyms:b:bhārata]]-[[synonyms:a:artha]]-[[synonyms:v:vinirṇayaḥ]]---the ascertainment of the //Mahābhārata//; [[synonyms:g:gāyatrī]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāṣya]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpaḥ]]---the purport of Brahma-gāyatrī, the mother of the Vedic literatures; [[synonyms:a:asau]]---that; [[synonyms:v:veda]]-[[synonyms:a:artha]]-[[synonyms:p:paribṛṁhitaḥ]]---expanded by the meanings of all the //Vedas//; [[synonyms:p:purāṇānām]]---of the //Purāṇas//; [[synonyms:s:sāma]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpaḥ]]---the best (like the //Sāma// among the //Vedas//); [[synonyms:s:sākṣāt]]---directly; [[synonyms:b:bhagavatā]] [[synonyms:u:uditaḥ]]---spoken by Vyāsadeva, an incarnation of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:d:dvādaśa]]-[[synonyms:s:skanda]]-[[synonyms:y:yuktaḥ]]---having twelve cantos; [[synonyms:a:ayam]]---this; [[synonyms:ś:śata]]-[[synonyms:v:viccheda]]-[[synonyms:s:saṁyutaḥ]]---having 355 chapters; [[synonyms:g:granthaḥ]]---this great literature; [[synonyms:a:aṣṭādaśa]]-[[synonyms:s:sāhasraḥ]]---having 18,000 verses; [[synonyms:ś:śrīmad]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāgavata]]-[[synonyms:a:abhidhaḥ]]---named //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam.//

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The meaning of the Vedānta-sūtra is present in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam. The full purport of the Mahābhārata is also there. The commentary of the Brahma-gāyatrī is also there and fully expanded with all Vedic knowledge. Śrīmad Bhāgavatam is the supreme Purāṇa, and it was compiled by the Supreme Personality of Godhead in His incarnation as Vyāsadeva. There are twelve cantos, 335 chapters and eighteen thousand verses.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from the //Garuda Purāṇa.//
