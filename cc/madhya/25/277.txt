====== CC Madhya 25.277 ======

===== Text =====

> caitanya-līlā-amṛta-pūra, kṛṣṇa-līlā-sukarpūra,
> duhe mili' haya sumādhurya
> sādhu-guru-prasāde, tāhā yei āsvāde,
> sei jāne mādhurya-prācurya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:c:caitanya]]-[[synonyms:l:līlā]] [[synonyms:a:amṛta]]-[[synonyms:p:pūra]]---the pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu are full of nectar; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:l:līlā]] [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:k:karpūra]]---the pastimes of Lord Kṛṣṇa are exactly like camphor; [[synonyms:d:duhe]] [[synonyms:m:mili']]---the two meeting; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---become; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:m:mādhurya]]---very, very palatable; [[synonyms:s:sādhu]]-[[synonyms:g:guru]]-[[synonyms:p:prasāde]]---by the mercy of saintly persons and devotees in the transcendental position; [[synonyms:t:tāhā]]---that; [[synonyms:y:yei]]---anyone who; [[synonyms:ā:āsvāde]]---relishes this palatable nectar; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:j:jāne]]---he can understand; [[synonyms:m:mādhurya]]-[[synonyms:p:prācurya]]---the extensive quantity of sweetness in devotional service.

===== Translation =====

**The pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu are full of nectar, and the pastimes of Lord Kṛṣṇa are like camphor. When one mixes these, they taste very sweet. By the mercy of the pure devotees, whoever tastes them can understand the depths of that sweetness.**
