====== CC Madhya 25.146 ======

===== Text =====

> sarva-vedānta-sāraṁ hi
> śrīmad-bhāgavatam iṣyate
> tad-rasāmṛta-tṛptasya
> nānyatra syād ratiḥ kvacit

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:v:vedānta]]-[[synonyms:s:sāram]]---the best part of all the Vedānta; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:ś:śrīmad]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāgavatam]]---the great literature about Bhagavān; [[synonyms:i:iṣyate]]---is accepted; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:r:rasa]]-[[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---by the transcendental mellow derived from that great literature; [[synonyms:t:tṛp]]-[[synonyms:t:tasya]]---of one who is satisfied; [[synonyms:n:na]]---never; [[synonyms:a:anyatra]]---anywhere else; [[synonyms:s:syāt]]---is; [[synonyms:r:ratiḥ]]---attraction; [[synonyms:k:kvacit]]---at any time.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam is accepted as the essence of all Vedic literature and Vedānta philosophy. Whoever tastes the transcendental mellow of Śrīmad Bhāgavatam is never attracted to any other literature.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (12.13.15).
