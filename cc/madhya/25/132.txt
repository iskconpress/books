====== Cc. Madhya 25.132 ======

===== Text =====

> vadanti tat tattva-vidas
> tattvaṁ yaj jñānam advayam
> brahmeti paramātmeti
> bhagavān iti śabdyate

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vadanti]]---they say; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---that; [[synonyms:t:tattva]]-[[synonyms:v:vidaḥ]]---those who know the Absolute Truth; [[synonyms:t:tattvam]]---the ultimate goal; [[synonyms:y:yat]]---which; [[synonyms:j:jñānam]] [[synonyms:a:advayam]]---identical knowledge; [[synonyms:b:brahma]] [[synonyms:i:iti]]---as the impersonal Brahman; [[synonyms:p:paramātmā]] [[synonyms:i:iti]]---as the Supersoul; [[synonyms:b:bhagavān]] [[synonyms:i:iti]]---as the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:ś:śabdyate]]---it is described.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The Absolute Truth is known by the self-realized souls as a unified identity known by different names-impersonal Brahman, localized Paramātmā, and Bhagavān, the Supreme Personality of Godhead.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// [[books:sb:1:2:11|(1.2.11)]].
