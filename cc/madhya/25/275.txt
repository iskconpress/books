====== Cc. Madhya 25.275 ======

===== Text =====

> sei sarovare giyā, haṁsa-cakravāka hañā,
> sadā tāhāṅ karaha vilāsa
> khaṇḍibe sakala duḥkha, pāibā parama sukha,
> anāyāse habe premollāsa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:s:sarovare]] [[synonyms:g:giyā]]---going to that lake where the clusters of lotus flowers exist; [[synonyms:h:haṁsa]]-[[synonyms:c:cakravāka]] [[synonyms:h:hañā]]---becoming swans or //cakravāka// birds; [[synonyms:s:sadā]]---always; [[synonyms:t:tāhāṅ]]---there; [[synonyms:k:karaha]] [[synonyms:v:vilāsa]]---enjoy life; [[synonyms:k:khaṇḍibe]]---will be diminished; [[synonyms:s:sakala]] [[synonyms:d:duḥkha]]---all material anxieties and miseries; [[synonyms:p:pāibā]]---you will get; [[synonyms:p:parama]] [[synonyms:s:sukha]]---the highest happiness; [[synonyms:a:anāyāse]]---very easily; [[synonyms:h:habe]]---there will be; [[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:u:ullāsa]]---jubilation in love of God.

===== Translation =====

**All the devotees of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu should go to that lake and, remaining always under the shelter of the lotus feet of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, become swans and cakravāka birds in those celestial waters. They should go on rendering service to Lord Śrī Kṛṣṇa and enjoy life perpetually. In this way all miseries will be diminished, the devotees will attain great happiness, and there will be jubilant love of God.**
