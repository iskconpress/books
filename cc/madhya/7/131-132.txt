~~Title:Caitanya-caritāmṛta, Madhya 7.131-132~~

====== CC Madhya 7.131-132 ======

===== Text =====

> pathe yāite devālaye rahe yei grāme
> yāṅra ghare bhikṣā kare, sei mahā-jane
>
> kūrme yaiche rīti, taiche kaila sarva-ṭhāñi
> nīlācale punaḥ yāvat nā āilā gosāñi

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:pathe]] [[synonyms:y:yāite]]---while passing on the road; [[synonyms:d:devālaye]]---in a temple; [[synonyms:r:rahe]]---He stays; [[synonyms:y:yei]] [[synonyms:g:grāme]]---in any village; [[synonyms:y:yāṅra]] [[synonyms:g:ghare]]---at whose place; [[synonyms:b:bhikṣā]] [[synonyms:k:kare]]---takes alms or eats; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:j:jane]]---to such a great personality; [[synonyms:k:kūrme]]---unto the //brāhmaṇa// Kūrma; [[synonyms:y:yaiche]]---just as; [[synonyms:r:rīti]]---the process; [[synonyms:t:taiche]]---in the same way; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---did; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:ṭ:ṭhāñi]]---in all places; [[synonyms:n:nīlācale]]---to Jagannātha Purī; [[synonyms:p:punaḥ]]---again; [[synonyms:y:yāvat]]---until; [[synonyms:n:nā]]---not; [[synonyms:ā:āilā]]---returned; [[synonyms:g:gosāñi]]---the Lord.

===== Translation =====

**While on His tour, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu would spend the night at a temple or on the roadside. Whenever He accepted food from a person, He would give him the same advice He gave the brāhmaṇa named Kūrma. He adopted this process until He returned from His South Indian tour to Jagannātha Purī.**
