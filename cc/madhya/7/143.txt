====== CC Madhya 7.143 ======

===== Text =====

> kvāhaṁ daridraḥ pāpīyān
> kva kṛṣṇaḥ śrī-niketanaḥ
> brahma-bandhur iti smāhaṁ
> bāhubhyāṁ parirambhitaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kva]]---who; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:d:daridraḥ]]---poor; [[synonyms:p:pāpīyān]]---sinful; [[synonyms:k:kva]]---who; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇaḥ]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:n:niketanaḥ]]---the transcendental form of all opulence; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]-[[synonyms:b:bandhuḥ]]---the friend of a //brāhmaṇa,// not fit even to be called a //brāhmaṇa//; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:s:sma]]---certainly; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:b:bāhubhyām]]---by the arms; [[synonyms:p:parirambhitaḥ]]---embraced.

===== Translation =====

**He said, " 'Who am I? A sinful, poor friend of a brāhmaṇa. And who is Kṛṣṇa? The Supreme Personality of Godhead, full in six opulences. Nonetheless, He has embraced me with His two arms.' "**

===== Purport =====

This verse was spoken by Sudāmā Brāhmaṇa in //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.81.16), in connection with his meeting Lord Kṛṣṇa.
