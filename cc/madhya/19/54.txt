====== CC Madhya 19.54 ======

===== Text =====

> yo 'jñāna-mattaṁ bhuvanaṁ dayālur
> ullāghayann apy akarot pramattam
> sva-prema-sampat-sudhayādbhutehaṁ
> śrī-kṛṣṇa-caitanyam amuṁ prapadye

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yaḥ]]---that Personality of Godhead who; [[synonyms:a:ajñāna]]-[[synonyms:m:mattam]]---maddened by ignorance or foolishly passing time //in karma, jñāna, yoga// and Māyāvāda philosophy; [[synonyms:b:bhuvanam]]---the entire three worlds; [[synonyms:d:dayāluḥ]]---so merciful; [[synonyms:u:ullāghayan]]---subduing such processes as //karma, jñāna// and //yoga//; [[synonyms:a:api]]---despite; [[synonyms:a:akarot]]---made; [[synonyms:p:pra]]-[[synonyms:m:mattam]]---maddened; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:s:sampat]]-[[synonyms:s:sudhayā]]---by the nectar of His personal devotional service, which is an invaluable treasure of bliss; [[synonyms:a:adbhuta]]-[[synonyms:ī:īham]]---whose activities are wonderful; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:c:caitanyam]]---unto Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:a:amum]]---that; [[synonyms:p:prapadye]]---I surrender.

===== Translation =====

**"We offer our respectful obeisances unto that merciful Supreme Personality of Godhead who has converted all three worlds, which were maddened by ignorance, and saved them from their diseased condition by making them mad with the nectar from the treasure-house of love of God. Let us take full shelter of that Personality of Godhead, Śrī Kṛṣṇa Caitanya, whose activities are wonderful."**

===== Purport =====

This verse is found in the //Govinda-līlāmṛta// (1.2).
