====== CC Madhya 19.190 ======

===== Text =====

> sakhya-bhakta---śrīdāmādi, pure bhīmārjuna
> vātsalya-bhakta---mātā pitā, yata guru-jana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sakhya]]-[[synonyms:b:bhakta]]---devotees in fraternity; [[synonyms:ś:śrīdāmā]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---of Śrīdāmā and others; [[synonyms:p:pure]]---in Dvārakā; [[synonyms:b:bhīma]]-[[synonyms:a:arjuna]]---Bhīma and Arjuna; [[synonyms:v:vātsalya]]-[[synonyms:b:bhakta]]---devotees in parental love; [[synonyms:m:mātā]] [[synonyms:p:pitā]]---the mother and father; [[synonyms:y:yata]] [[synonyms:g:guru]]-[[synonyms:j:jana]]---all other similarly superior persons.

===== Translation =====

**"In Vṛndāvana, examples of devotees in fraternity are Śrīdāmā and Sudāmā; in Dvārakā the Lord's friends are Bhīma and Arjuna; in Vṛndāvana the devotees in parental love are mother Yaśodā and father Nanda Mahārāja, and in Dvārakā the Lord's parents are Vasudeva and Devakī. There are also other superior persons who are devotees in parental love.**
