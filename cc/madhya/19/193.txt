====== CC Madhya 19.193 ======

===== Text =====

> gokule 'kevalā' rati---aiśvarya-jñāna-hīna
> purī-dvaye, vaikuṇṭhādye---aiśvarya-pravīṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:gokule]]---in Gokula Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:k:kevalā]] [[synonyms:r:rati]]---flawless attachment; [[synonyms:a:aiśvarya]]-[[synonyms:j:jñāna]]-[[synonyms:h:hīna]]---without reverential considerations; [[synonyms:p:purī]]-[[synonyms:d:dvaye]]---in two //purīs,// namely Mathurā Purī and Dvārakā Purī; [[synonyms:v:vaikuṇṭha]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādye]]---in the Vaikuṇṭha planets; [[synonyms:a:aiśvarya]]-[[synonyms:p:pravīṇa]]---prominence of awe and reverence.

===== Translation =====

**"Pure attachment without reverence is found in Goloka Vṛndāvana. Attachment in which awe and reverence are prominent is found in the two cities Mathurā and Dvārakā and in Vaikuṇṭha.**
