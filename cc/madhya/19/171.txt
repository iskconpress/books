====== CC Madhya 19.171 ======

===== Text =====

> mad-guṇa-śruti-mātreṇa
> mayi sarva-guhāśaye
> manogatir avicchinnā
> yathā gaṅgāmbhaso 'mbudhau

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mat]]---of Me; [[synonyms:g:guṇa]]---of the qualities; [[synonyms:ś:śruti]]-[[synonyms:m:mātreṇa]]---only by hearing; [[synonyms:m:mayi]]---to Me; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:g:guhā]]---in all hearts; [[synonyms:ā:āśaye]]---who am situated; [[synonyms:m:manaḥ]]-[[synonyms:g:gatiḥ]]---the movement of the mind; [[synonyms:a:avicchinnā]]---unobstructed; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]---just as; [[synonyms:g:gaṅgā]]-[[synonyms:a:ambhasaḥ]]---of the celestial waters of the Ganges; [[synonyms:a:ambudhau]]---to the ocean.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Just as the celestial waters of the Ganges flow unobstructed into the ocean, so when My devotees simply hear of Me, their minds come to Me. I reside in the hearts of all.**

===== Purport =====

This verse and the following three verses quoted from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (3.29.11-14), were spoken by Lord Kṛṣṇa in the form of Kapiladeva.
