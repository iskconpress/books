====== CC Madhya 19.195 ======

===== Text =====

> śānta-dāsya-rase aiśvarya kāhāṅ uddīpana
> vātsalya-sakhya-madhure ta' kare saṅkocana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śānta]]-[[synonyms:d:dāsya]]-[[synonyms:r:rase]]---in the transcendental mellows of neutrality and servitude; [[synonyms:a:aiśvarya]]---opulence; [[synonyms:k:kāhāṅ]]---somewhere; [[synonyms:u:uddīpana]]---manifested; [[synonyms:v:vātsalya]]-[[synonyms:s:sakhya]]-[[synonyms:m:madhure]]---in fraternal love, paternity and conjugal love; [[synonyms:t:ta']]---certainly; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:s:saṅkocana]]---minimizing.

===== Translation =====

**"On the transcendental platform of neutrality and service, sometimes the opulence of the Lord is prominent. However, in the transcendental mellows of fraternal, paternal and conjugal love, the opulence is minimized.**
