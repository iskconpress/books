====== CC Madhya 8.143 ======

===== Text =====

> śṛṅgāra-rasarāja-maya-mūrti-dhara
> ataeva ātma-paryanta-sarva-citta-hara

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śṛṅgāra]]-[[synonyms:r:rasa]]-[[synonyms:r:rāja]]-[[synonyms:m:maya]]---consisting of the mellow of conjugal love, which is the king of mellows; [[synonyms:m:mūrti]]-[[synonyms:d:dhara]]---Kṛṣṇa, the personified reservoir of all pleasure; [[synonyms:a:ataeva]]---therefore; [[synonyms:ā:ātma]]-[[synonyms:p:paryanta]]---even up to His own self; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]---all; [[synonyms:c:citta]]---of hearts; [[synonyms:h:hara]]---the attractor.

===== Translation =====

**"Kṛṣṇa is all-attractive for devotees in all devotional mellows because He is the personification of the conjugal mellow. Kṛṣṇa is attractive not only to all the devotees, but to Himself as well.**
