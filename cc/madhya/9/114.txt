====== Cc. Madhya 9.114 ======

===== Text =====

> kasyānubhāvo 'sya na deva vidmahe
> tavāṅghri-reṇu-sparaśādhikāraḥ
> yad-vāñchayā śrīr lalanācarat tapo
> vihāya kāmān su-ciraṁ dhṛta-vratā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kasya]]---of what; [[synonyms:a:anubhāvaḥ]]---a result; [[synonyms:a:asya]]---of the serpent (Kāliya); [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:d:deva]]---O Lord; [[synonyms:v:vidmahe]]---we know; [[synonyms:t:tava]] [[synonyms:a:aṅghri]]---of Your lotus feet; [[synonyms:r:reṇu]]---of the dust; [[synonyms:s:sparaśa]]---for touching; [[synonyms:a:adhikāraḥ]]---qualification; [[synonyms:y:yat]]---which; [[synonyms:v:vāñchayā]]---by desiring; [[synonyms:ś:śrīḥ]]---the goddess of fortune; [[synonyms:l:lalanā]]---the topmost woman; [[synonyms:a:acarat]]---performed; [[synonyms:t:tapaḥ]]---austerity; [[synonyms:v:vihāya]]---giving up; [[synonyms:k:kāmān]]---all desires; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:c:ciram]]---for a long time; [[synonyms:d:dhṛta]]---a law upheld; [[synonyms:v:vratā]]---as a vow.

===== Translation =====

**Caitanya Mahāprabhu then said, " 'O Lord, we do not know how the serpent Kāliya attained such an opportunity to be touched by the dust of Your lotus feet. Even the goddess of fortune, for this end, performed austerities for centuries, giving up all other desires and observing austere vows. Indeed, we do not know how the serpent Kāliya got such an opportunity."'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.16.36); it was spoken by the wives of the Kāliya serpent.
