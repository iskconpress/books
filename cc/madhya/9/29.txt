====== CC Madhya 9.29 ======

===== Text =====

> ramante yogino 'nante
> satyānande cid-ātmani
> iti rāma-padenāsau
> paraṁ brahmābhidhīyate

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:r:ramante]]---take pleasure; [[synonyms:y:yoginaḥ]]---transcendentalists; [[synonyms:a:anante]]---in the unlimited; [[synonyms:s:satya]]-[[synonyms:ā:ānande]]---real pleasure; [[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmani]]---in spiritual existence; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:r:rāma]]---Rāma; [[synonyms:p:padena]]---by the word; [[synonyms:a:asau]]---He; [[synonyms:p:param]]---supreme; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]---truth; [[synonyms:a:abhidhīyate]]---is called.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The Supreme Absolute Truth is called Rāma because the transcendentalists take pleasure in the unlimited true pleasure of spiritual existence.'**

===== Purport =====

This is the eighth verse of the //Śata-nāma-stotra// of Lord Rāmacandra, which is found in the //Padma Purāṇa.//
