====== Cc. Madhya 24.195 ======

===== Text =====

> durūhādbhuta-vīrye 'smin
> śraddhā dūre 'stu pañcake
> yatra svalpo 'pi sambandhaḥ
> saddhiyāṁ bhāva-janmane

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:d:durūha]]---difficult to be reconciled; [[synonyms:a:adbhuta]]---wonderful; [[synonyms:v:vīrye]]---in the power; [[synonyms:a:asmin]]---in this; [[synonyms:ś:śraddhā]]---faith; [[synonyms:d:dūre]]---far away; [[synonyms:a:astu]]---let it be; [[synonyms:p:pañcake]]---in the above-mentioned five principles; [[synonyms:y:yatra]]---in which; [[synonyms:s:svalpaḥ]]---a little; [[synonyms:a:api]]---even; [[synonyms:s:sambandhaḥ]]---connection; [[synonyms:s:sat]]-[[synonyms:d:dhiyām]]---of those who are intelligent and offenseless; [[synonyms:b:bhāva]]-[[synonyms:j:janmane]]---to awaken one's dormant love for Kṛṣṇa.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The power of these five principles is very wonderful and difficult to reconcile. Even without faith in them, a person who is offenseless can experience dormant love of Kṛṣṇa simply by being a little connected with them.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse is also found in the //Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu// (1.2.238).
