====== Cc. Madhya 24.293 ======

===== Text =====

> 'muni', 'nirgrantha', 'ca' 'api',---cāri śabdera artha
> yāhāṅ yei lāge, tāhā kariye samartha

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:muni]]---the sage; [[synonyms:n:nirgrantha]]---either learned or foolish; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:a:api]]---in spite of; [[synonyms:c:cāri]] [[synonyms:ś:śabdera]] [[synonyms:a:artha]]---the meanings of these four words; [[synonyms:y:yāhāṅ]]---wherever; [[synonyms:y:yei]] [[synonyms:l:lāge]]---they apply; [[synonyms:t:tāhā]] [[synonyms:k:kariye]] [[synonyms:s:samartha]]---make them sound.

===== Translation =====

**"When these thirty-two types of devotees are qualified with the words muni nirgrantha ca api, they can be increased in different ways and very soundly elaborated upon.**
