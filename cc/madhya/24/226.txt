====== CC Madhya 24.226 ======

===== Text =====

> 'nirgrantha hañā'---ei duṅhāra 'viśeṣaṇa'
> āra artha śuna, yaiche sādhura saṅgama

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nirgrantha]] [[synonyms:h:hañā]]---becoming //nirgrantha//; [[synonyms:e:ei]]---this; [[synonyms:d:duṅhāra]]---of both; [[synonyms:v:viśeṣaṇa]]---the adjective; [[synonyms:ā:āra]] [[synonyms:a:artha]]---another import; [[synonyms:ś:śuna]]---please hear; [[synonyms:y:yaiche]]---in which; [[synonyms:s:sādhura]] [[synonyms:s:saṅgama]]---there is association with devotees.

===== Translation =====

**"The word nirgrantha is taken as an adjective modifying muni and ātmārāma. There is another meaning, which you may hear from Me, indicating association with a devotee. Now I shall explain how it is that through the association of devotees, even a nirgrantha can become a devotee.**
