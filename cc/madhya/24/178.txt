====== Cc. Madhya 24.178 ======

===== Text =====

> sarasi sārasa-haṁsa-vihaṅgāś
> cāru-gīta-hṛta-cetasa etya
> harim upāsata te yata-cittā
> hanta mīlita-dṛśo dhṛta-maunāḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sarasi]]---in the water; [[synonyms:s:sārasa]]---cranes; [[synonyms:h:haṁsa]]---swans; [[synonyms:v:vihaṅgāḥ]]---birds; [[synonyms:c:cāru]]-[[synonyms:g:gīta]]---by the melodious song of Kṛṣṇa's flute; [[synonyms:h:hṛta]]-[[synonyms:c:cetasaḥ]]---devoid of material consciousness; [[synonyms:e:etya]]---coming near; [[synonyms:h:harim]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:u:upāsata]]---worshiped; [[synonyms:t:te]]---all of them; [[synonyms:y:yata]]-[[synonyms:c:cittāḥ]]---with full attention; [[synonyms:h:hanta]]---alas; [[synonyms:m:mīlita]]-[[synonyms:d:dṛśaḥ]]---closing their eyes; [[synonyms:d:dhṛta]]-[[synonyms:m:maunāḥ]]---completely silent.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'All the cranes and swans in the water are being enchanted by the melodious song of Kṛṣṇa's flute. They have approached and are worshiping the Supreme Personality of Godhead with full attention. Alas, they are closing their eyes and are becoming completely silent.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.35.11). In the day, Kṛṣṇa went to the forest of Vṛndāvana, and at that time, the //gopīs,// being morose due to separation from Him, were lamenting in this way.
