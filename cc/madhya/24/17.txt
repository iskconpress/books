====== CC Madhya 24.17 ======

===== Text =====

> mūrkha, nīca, mleccha ādi śāstra-rikta-gaṇa
> dhana-sañcayī---nirgrantha, āra ye nirdhana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mūrkha]]---foolish, illiterate persons; [[synonyms:n:nīca]]---lowborn; [[synonyms:m:mleccha]]---unclean persons with no principles; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---and others; [[synonyms:ś:śāstra]]-[[synonyms:r:rikta]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---persons devoid of all regulative principles stated in //śāstra//; [[synonyms:d:dhana]]-[[synonyms:s:sañcayī]]---capitalist (one who gathers wealth); [[synonyms:n:nirgrantha]]---called //nirgrantha//; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---also; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---anyone who; [[synonyms:n:nirdhana]]---without riches.

===== Translation =====

**"Nirgrantha also refers to one who is illiterate, lowborn, misbehaved, unregulated and devoid of respect for Vedic literature. The word also refers to one who is a capitalist and to one who has no riches.**
