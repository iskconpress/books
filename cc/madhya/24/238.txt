====== Cc. Madhya 24.238 ======

===== Text =====

> "gosāñi, prayāṇa-patha chāḍi' kene āilā
> tomā dekhi' mora lakṣya mṛga palāilā"

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:g:gosāñi]]---O great saintly person; [[synonyms:p:prayāṇa]]-[[synonyms:p:patha]] [[synonyms:c:chāḍi']]---leaving aside the general path; [[synonyms:k:kene]]---why; [[synonyms:ā:āilā]]---have you come; [[synonyms:t:tomā]] [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing you; [[synonyms:m:mora]] [[synonyms:l:lakṣya]]---my targets; [[synonyms:m:mṛga]]---the animals; [[synonyms:p:palāilā]]---fled.

===== Translation =====

**"The hunter addressed Nārada Muni: 'O gosvāmī! O great saintly person! Why have you left the general path through the forest to come to me? Simply by seeing you, all the animals I was hunting have now fled.'**
