====== CC Madhya 24.71 ======

===== Text =====

> 'brahma' śabdera artha---tattva sarva-bṛhattama
> svarūpa aiśvarya kari' nāhi yāṅra sama

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:brahma]]---brahma; [[synonyms:ś:śabdera]] [[synonyms:a:artha]]---the meaning of the word; [[synonyms:t:tattva]]---the truth; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:b:bṛhat]]-[[synonyms:t:tama]]---summum bonum among the relative truths; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---the original identity; [[synonyms:a:aiśvarya]]---opulence; [[synonyms:k:kari']]---accepting; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---not; [[synonyms:y:yāṅra]]---whose; [[synonyms:s:sama]]---equal.

===== Translation =====

**"The word brahma indicates the summum bonum, the Absolute Truth, which is greater than all other truths. It is the original identity, and there can be no truth equal to that Absolute Truth.**
