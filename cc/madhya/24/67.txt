====== CC Madhya 24.67 ======

===== Text =====

> cānvācaye samāhāre
> 'nyo 'nyārthe ca samuccaye
> yatnāntare tathā pāda-
> pūraṇe 'py avadhāraṇe

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:c:ca]]---this word //ca//; [[synonyms:a:anvācaye]]---in connecting one with another; [[synonyms:s:samāhāre]]---in the sense of aggregation; [[synonyms:a:anyo]] [[synonyms:n:'nya]]-[[synonyms:a:arthe]]---to help one another in the imports; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---the word //ca//; [[synonyms:s:samuccaye]]---in aggregate understanding; [[synonyms:y:yatna]]-[[synonyms:a:antare]]---in another effort; [[synonyms:t:tathā]]---as well as; [[synonyms:p:pāda]]-[[synonyms:p:pūraṇe]]---in completing the verse; [[synonyms:a:api]]---also; [[synonyms:a:avadhāraṇe]]---in the sense of certainty.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The word ca [and] is used to connect a word or sentence with a previous word or sentence, to give the sense of aggregation, to assist the meaning, to give a collective understanding, to suggest another effort or exertion, or to fulfill the meter of a verse. It is also used in the sense of certainty.'**
