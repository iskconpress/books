====== Cc. Madhya 24.233 ======

===== Text =====

> aiche eka śaśaka dekhe āra kata-dūre
> jīvera duḥkha dekhi' nārada vyākula-antare

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:aiche]]---similarly; [[synonyms:e:eka]] [[synonyms:ś:śaśaka]]---one rabbit; [[synonyms:d:dekhe]]---he sees; [[synonyms:ā:āra]] [[synonyms:k:kata]]-[[synonyms:d:dūre]]---still farther ahead; [[synonyms:j:jīvera]]---of the living entity; [[synonyms:d:duḥkha]] [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing such horrible miseries; [[synonyms:n:nārada]]---the great saintly person; [[synonyms:v:vyākula]]-[[synonyms:a:antare]]---very pained within himself.

===== Translation =====

**"When he went farther, he saw a rabbit that was also suffering. Nārada Muni was greatly pained at heart to see living entities suffer so.**
