====== CC Madhya 24.180 ======

===== Text =====

> kiṁvā'dhṛti'-śabde nija-pūrṇatādi-jñāna kaya
> duḥkhābhāve uttama-prāptye mahā-pūrṇa haya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kiṁvā]]---or; [[synonyms:d:dhṛti]]---//dhṛti//; [[synonyms:ś:śabde]]---by this word; [[synonyms:n:nija]]---own; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇatā]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---perfection and so on; [[synonyms:j:jñāna]]---knowledge; [[synonyms:k:kaya]]---says; [[synonyms:d:duḥkha]]-[[synonyms:a:abhāve]]---in the absence of all material miseries; [[synonyms:u:uttama]]---the best; [[synonyms:p:prāptye]]---by obtaining; [[synonyms:m:mahā]]-[[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]] [[synonyms:h:haya]]---becomes perfectly perfect.

===== Translation =====

**"The word dhṛti is also used when one is fully perfect in knowledge. When due to having obtained the lotus feet of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, he has no material miseries, he attains mahā-pūrṇa, the highest level of perfection.**
