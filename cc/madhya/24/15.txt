====== Cc. Madhya 24.15 ======

===== Text =====

> 'muni'-śabde manana-śīla, āra kahe maunī
> tapasvī vratī, yati, āra ṛṣi, muni

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:muni]]-[[synonyms:ś:śabde]]---by the word //muni//; [[synonyms:m:manana]]-[[synonyms:ś:śīla]]---who is thoughtful; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---also; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---it means; [[synonyms:m:maunī]]---one who is silent; [[synonyms:t:tapasvī]]---an ascetic; [[synonyms:v:vratī]]---one who keeps great vows; [[synonyms:y:yati]]---one in the renounced order of life; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:ṛ:ṛṣi]]---a saintly person; [[synonyms:m:muni]]---they are called //muni.//

===== Translation =====

**"The word muni refers to one who is thoughtful, one who is grave or silent, an ascetic, one who keeps great vows, one in the renounced order, a saint. These are the different meanings of the word muni.**
