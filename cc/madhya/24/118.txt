====== CC Madhya 24.118 ======

===== Text =====

> nava-yogīśvara janma haite 'sādhaka' jñānī
> vidhi-śiva-nārada-mukhe kṛṣṇa-guṇa śuni'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nava]]---nine; [[synonyms:y:yogi]]-[[synonyms:ī:īśvara]]---great saintly //yogīs//; [[synonyms:j:janma]] [[synonyms:h:haite]]---from the very birth; [[synonyms:s:sādhaka]]---practicers; [[synonyms:j:jñānī]]---well versed in transcendental knowledge; [[synonyms:v:vidhi]]---Lord Brahmā; [[synonyms:ś:śiva]]---Lord Śiva; [[synonyms:n:nārada]]---the great sage Nārada; [[synonyms:m:mukhe]]---in their mouths; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:g:guṇa]] [[synonyms:ś:śuni']]---hearing the transcendental qualities of Kṛṣṇa.

===== Translation =====

**"From their very births, the nine great mystic yogīs [Yogendras] were impersonal philosophers of the Absolute Truth. However, because they heard about Lord Kṛṣṇa's qualities from Lord Brahmā, Lord Śiva and the great sage Nārada, they also became Kṛṣṇa's devotees.**
