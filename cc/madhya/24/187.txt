====== Cc. Madhya 24.187 ======

===== Text =====

> buddhye rame ātmārāma---dui ta' prakāra
> 'paṇḍita' muni-gaṇa, nirgrantha 'mūrkha' āra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:buddhye]]---in intelligence; [[synonyms:r:rame]]---who enjoys; [[synonyms:ā:ātmārāma]]---is //ātmārāma//; [[synonyms:d:dui]] [[synonyms:t:ta']] [[synonyms:p:prakāra]]---two varieties; [[synonyms:p:paṇḍita]]---learned; [[synonyms:m:muni]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---philosophers; [[synonyms:n:nirgrantha]]---without education; [[synonyms:m:mūrkha]]---foolish; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---also.

===== Translation =====

**"Everyone has some kind of intelligence, and one who utilizes his intelligence is called ātmārāma. There are two types of ātmārāma. One is a learned scholar and a philosopher, and the other is an uneducated, illiterate and foolish person.**
