====== CC Madhya 24.321 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇe svadhāmopagate
> dharma-jñānādibhiḥ saha
> kalau naṣṭa-dṛśām eṣa
> purāṇārko 'dhunoditaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇe]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:d:dhāma]]---to His personal abode; [[synonyms:u:upagate]]---having returned; [[synonyms:d:dharma]]-[[synonyms:j:jñāna]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādibhiḥ]] [[synonyms:s:saha]]---along with religious principles, transcendental knowledge, and so on; [[synonyms:k:kalau]]---in this Age of Kali; [[synonyms:n:naṣṭa]]-[[synonyms:d:dṛśām]]---of persons who have lost their spiritual vision; [[synonyms:e:eṣaḥ]]---this; [[synonyms:p:purāṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:arkaḥ]]---//Purāṇa// or Vedic literature that shines like the sun; [[synonyms:a:adhunā]]---at the present moment; [[synonyms:u:uditaḥ]]---has arisen.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'After Lord Kṛṣṇa departed for His abode along with religious principles and transcendental knowledge, this Purāṇa, Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, has arisen like the sun in this Age of Kali to enlighten those who have no spiritual vision.'**
