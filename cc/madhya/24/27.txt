====== Cc. Madhya 24.27 ======

===== Text =====

> 'hetu'-śabde kahe---bhukti-ādi vāñchāntare
> bhukti, siddhi, mukti---mukhya ei tina prakāre

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:h:hetu]]---cause; [[synonyms:ś:śabde]]---by the word; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---it is said; [[synonyms:b:bhukti]]---enjoying the result by oneself; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---and so on; [[synonyms:v:vāñchā]]-[[synonyms:a:antare]]---because of a different ambition; [[synonyms:b:bhukti]]---enjoying the result of action; [[synonyms:s:siddhi]]---the perfection of doing something; [[synonyms:m:mukti]]---liberation; [[synonyms:m:mukhya]]---chief; [[synonyms:e:ei]]---these; [[synonyms:t:tina]] [[synonyms:p:prakāre]]---in three ways.

===== Translation =====

**"The word hetu [cause] means that a thing is done for some motive. There can be three motives. One may act to enjoy the result personally, to achieve some material perfection, or to act in such a way that one may be liberated.**
