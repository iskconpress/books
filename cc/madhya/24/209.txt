====== Cc. Madhya 24.209 ======

===== Text =====

> kirāta-hūnāndhra-pulinda-pulkaśā
> ābhīra-śumbhā yavanāḥ khaśādayaḥ
> ye 'nye ca pāpā yad-upāśrayāśrayāḥ
> śudhyanti tasmai prabhaviṣṇave namaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kirāta]]---the aborigines named Kirātas; [[synonyms:h:hūna]]---the Hūnas; [[synonyms:ā:āndhra]]---Āndhras; [[synonyms:p:pulinda]]---Pulindas; [[synonyms:p:pulkaśāḥ]]---Pulkaśas; [[synonyms:ā:ābhīra]]---Ābhīras; [[synonyms:ś:śumbhāḥ]]---Śumbhas; [[synonyms:y:yavanāḥ]]---persons who do not follow the Vedic injunctions and who eat cow's flesh; [[synonyms:k:khaśa]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādayaḥ]]---Khaśas and others; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---those who; [[synonyms:a:anye]]---similar others; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:p:pāpāḥ]]---sinful persons; [[synonyms:y:yat]]---of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:u:upāśraya]]---of the devotees; [[synonyms:ā:āśrayāḥ]]---taking shelter; [[synonyms:ś:śudhyanti]]---become purified; [[synonyms:t:tasmai]]---unto Him, Lord Viṣṇu, because of whom they become purified; [[synonyms:p:prabhaviṣṇave]]---to Lord Viṣṇu, the most powerful; [[synonyms:n:namaḥ]]---respectful obeisances.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Kirāta, Hūna, Āndhra, Pulinda, Pulkaśa, Ābhīra, Śumbha, Yavana and the Khaśa races and even others who are addicted to sinful acts can be purified by taking shelter of the devotees of the Lord due to His being the supreme power. I beg to offer my respectful obeisances unto Him.'**
