====== CC Madhya 24.89 ======

===== Text =====

> sei upāsaka haya trividha prakāra
> akāma, mokṣa-kāma, sarva-kāma āra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:u:upāsaka]]---those devotees; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---are; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:v:vidha]] [[synonyms:p:prakāra]]---three varieties; [[synonyms:a:akāma]]---without material desires; [[synonyms:m:mokṣa]]-[[synonyms:k:kāma]]---desiring to become liberated; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:k:kāma]]---filled with all material desires; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and.

===== Translation =====

**"The devotees are divided into three categories-akāma [desireless], mokṣa-kāma [desiring liberation], and sarva-kāma [desiring material perfection].**
