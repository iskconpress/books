====== CC Madhya 24.160 ======

===== Text =====

> yadā hi nendriyārtheṣu
> na karmasv anuṣajjate
> sarva-saṅkalpa-sannyāsī
> yogārūḍhas tadocyate

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yadā]]---when; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:i:indriya]]-[[synonyms:a:artheṣu]]---sense gratification; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:k:karmasu]]---in activities; [[synonyms:a:anuṣajjate]]---one becomes engaged; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]---all kinds of; [[synonyms:s:saṅkalpa]]---desires; [[synonyms:s:sannyāsī]]---renouncing; [[synonyms:y:yoga]]-[[synonyms:ā:ārūḍhaḥ]]---one who has actually attained perfection in the yoga system; [[synonyms:t:tadā]]---at that time; [[synonyms:u:ucyate]]---is said.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'When a person is no longer interested in acting for sense gratification and when he renounces all material desires, he is said to be situated in perfect yoga [yogārūḍha].'**
