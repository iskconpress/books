====== Cc. Madhya 24.36 ======

===== Text =====

> 'ittham-bhūta'-śabdera artha---pūrṇānandamaya
> yāṅra āge brahmānanda tṛṇa-prāya haya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:i:ittham]]-[[synonyms:b:bhūta]]-[[synonyms:ś:śabdera]] [[synonyms:a:artha]]---the meaning or import of the word //ittham-bhūta//; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]-[[synonyms:ā:ānanda]]-[[synonyms:m:maya]]---full of transcendental bliss; [[synonyms:y:yāṅra]] [[synonyms:ā:āge]]---in front of which; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]-[[synonyms:ā:ānanda]]---the transcendental bliss derived from impersonalism; [[synonyms:t:tṛṇa]]-[[synonyms:p:prāya]]---just like straw; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---is.

===== Translation =====

**"The word ittham-bhūta is transcendentally exalted because it means 'full of transcendental bliss.' Before this transcendental bliss, the bliss derived from merging into the existence of the Absolute [brahmānanda] becomes like a piece of straw in comparison.**
