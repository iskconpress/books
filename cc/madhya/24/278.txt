====== CC Madhya 24.278 ======

===== Text =====

> "aho dhanyo 'si devarṣe
> kṛpayā yasya tat-kṣaṇāt
> nīco 'py utpulako lebhe
> lubdhako ratim acyute"

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:aho]]---oh; [[synonyms:d:dhanyaḥ]]---glorified; [[synonyms:a:asi]]---you are; [[synonyms:d:deva]]-[[synonyms:ṛ:ṛṣe]]---O sage among the demigods; [[synonyms:k:kṛpayā]]---by the mercy; [[synonyms:y:yasya]]---of whom; [[synonyms:t:tat]]-[[synonyms:k:kṣaṇāt]]---immediately; [[synonyms:n:nīcaḥ]] [[synonyms:a:api]]---even though one is the lowest of men; [[synonyms:u:utpulakaḥ]]---becoming agitated in ecstatic love; [[synonyms:l:lebhe]]---gets; [[synonyms:l:lubdhakaḥ]]---a hunter; [[synonyms:r:ratim]]---attraction; [[synonyms:a:acyute]]---unto the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

===== Translation =====

**"Parvata Muni continued, 'My dear friend Nārada Muni, you are glorified as the sage among the demigods. By your mercy, even a lowborn person like this hunter can immediately become attached to Lord Kṛṣṇa.'**

===== Purport =====

A pure Vaiṣṇava believes in the statements of the //śāstras.// This verse is quoted from Vedic literature, the //Skanda Purāṇa.//
