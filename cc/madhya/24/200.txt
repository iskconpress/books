====== Cc. Madhya 24.200 ======

===== Text =====

> 'ātmā'-śabde 'svabhāva' kahe, tāte yei rame
> ātmārāma jīva yata sthāvara-jaṅgame

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:ātmā]]-[[synonyms:ś:śabde]]---by the word //ātmā//; [[synonyms:s:svabhāva]]---nature; [[synonyms:k:kahe]]---is sometimes said; [[synonyms:t:tāte]]---in that; [[synonyms:y:yei]] [[synonyms:r:rame]]---one who takes pleasure; [[synonyms:ā:ātmārāma]]---called //ātmārāma//; [[synonyms:j:jīva]]---the living entities; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---all of them; [[synonyms:s:sthāvara]]-[[synonyms:j:jaṅgame]]---the moving and nonmoving.

===== Translation =====

**"Another meaning of the word ātmā is 'one's characteristic nature.' Whoever enjoys his particular type of nature is called ātmārāma. Therefore, all living entities-be they moving or nonmoving-are also called ātmārāma.**
