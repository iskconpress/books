====== CC Madhya 24.47 ======

===== Text =====

> pariniṣṭhito 'pi nairguṇye
> uttamaḥśloka-līlayā
> gṛhīta-cetā rājarṣe
> ākhyānaṁ yad adhītavān

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:pariniṣṭhitaḥ]]---situated; [[synonyms:a:api]]---although; [[synonyms:n:nairguṇye]]---in the transcendental position, freed from the material modes of nature; [[synonyms:u:uttamaḥ]]-[[synonyms:ś:śloka]]-[[synonyms:l:līlayā]]---by the pastimes of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Uttamaḥśloka; [[synonyms:g:gṛhīta]]-[[synonyms:c:ceta]]---the mind became fully taken over; [[synonyms:r:rājarṣe]]---O great King; [[synonyms:ā:ākhyānam]]---the narration; [[synonyms:y:yat]]---which; [[synonyms:a:adhītavān]]---studied.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Śukadeva Gosvāmī addressed Parīkṣit Mahārāja, "My dear King, although I was fully situated in the transcendental position, I was nonetheless attracted to the pastimes of Lord Kṛṣṇa. Therefore I studied Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam from my father.' "**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// [[books:sb:2:1:9|(2.1.9)]].
