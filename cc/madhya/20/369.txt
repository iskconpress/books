====== Cc. Madhya 20.369 ======

===== Text =====

> 'sanakādi', 'nārada', 'pṛthu' 'paraśurāma'
> jīva-rūpa 'brahmāra' āveśāvatāra-nāma

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sanaka]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---the four Kumāras; [[synonyms:n:nārada]]---Nārada; [[synonyms:p:pṛthu]]---Mahārāja Pṛthu; [[synonyms:p:paraśurāma]]---Paraśurāma; [[synonyms:j:jīva]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---as the living entity; [[synonyms:b:brahmāra]]---of Lord Brahmā; [[synonyms:ā:āveśa]]-[[synonyms:a:avatāra]]-[[synonyms:n:nāma]]---all of them are called empowered incarnations.

===== Translation =====

**"Some śaktyāveśa-avatāras are the four Kumāras, Nārada, Mahārāja Pṛthu and Paraśurāma. When a living being is empowered to act as Lord Brahmā, he is also considered a śaktyāveśa-avatāra.**
