{{description>" 'Those who are anxious to awaken their spiritual consciousness, who have unflinching intelligence and who are not deviated, certainly attain the desired goal.'}}

====== Cc. Madhya 20.106 ======

===== Text =====

> acirād eva sarvārthaḥ
> sidhyaty eṣām abhīpsitaḥ
> sad-dharmasyāvabodhāya
> yeṣāṁ nirbandhinī matiḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:acirāt]]---very soon; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---certainly; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:a:arthaḥ]]---the goal of life; [[synonyms:s:sidhyati]]---becomes fulfilled; [[synonyms:e:eṣām]]---of these persons; [[synonyms:a:abhīpsitaḥ]]---desired; [[synonyms:s:sat]]-[[synonyms:d:dharmasya]]---of the path of progressive devotional service; [[synonyms:a:avabodhāya]]---for understanding; [[synonyms:y:yeṣām]]---those whose; [[synonyms:n:nirbandhinī]]---unflinching; [[synonyms:m:matiḥ]]---intelligence.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Those who are anxious to awaken their spiritual consciousness, who have unflinching intelligence and who are not deviated, certainly attain the desired goal.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse, quoted from the //Nāradīya Purāṇa,// is found in the //Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu// (1.2.103).
