====== CC Madhya 20.170 ======

===== Text =====

> citraṁ bataitad ekena
> vapuṣā yugapat pṛthak
> gṛheṣu dvy-aṣṭa-sāhasraṁ
> striya eka udāvahat

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:c:citram]]---wonderful; [[synonyms:b:bata]]---oh; [[synonyms:e:etat]]---this; [[synonyms:e:ekena]]---with one; [[synonyms:v:vapuṣā]]---form; [[synonyms:y:yugapat]]---simultaneously; [[synonyms:p:pṛthak]]---separately; [[synonyms:g:gṛheṣu]]---in the houses; [[synonyms:d:dvi]]-[[synonyms:a:asta]]-[[synonyms:s:sāhasram]]---sixteen thousand; [[synonyms:s:striyaḥ]]---all the queens; [[synonyms:e:ekaḥ]]---the one Śrī Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:u:udāvahat]]---married.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'It is astounding that Lord Śrī Kṛṣṇa, who is one without a second, expanded Himself in sixteen thousand similar forms to marry sixteen thousand queens in their respective homes.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse is spoken by Nārada Muni in //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.69.2).
