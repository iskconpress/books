====== CC Madhya 20.146 ======

===== Text =====

> mukhya-gauṇa-vṛtti, kiṁvā anvaya-vyatireke
> vedera pratijñā kevala kahaye kṛṣṇake

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:mukhya]]---chief; [[synonyms:g:gauṇa]]---secondary; [[synonyms:v:vṛtti]]---meaning; [[synonyms:k:kiṁvā]]---or; [[synonyms:a:anvaya]]-[[synonyms:v:vyatireke]]---directly or indirectly; [[synonyms:v:vedera]] [[synonyms:p:pratijñā]]---ultimate declaration of the Vedas; [[synonyms:k:kevala]]---only; [[synonyms:k:kahaye]]---speaks; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇake]]---about Kṛṣṇa.

===== Translation =====

**"When one accepts Vedic literature by interpretation or even by dictionary meaning, directly or indirectly the ultimate declaration of Vedic knowledge points to Lord Kṛṣṇa.**
