====== Cc. Madhya 20.111 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇera svābhāvika tina-śakti-pariṇati
> cic-chakti, jīva-śakti, āra māyā-śakti

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇera]]---of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:s:svābhāvika]]---natural; [[synonyms:t:tina]]---three; [[synonyms:ś:śakti]]---of energies; [[synonyms:p:pariṇati]]---transformations; [[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:ś:śakti]]---spiritual potency; [[synonyms:j:jīva]]-[[synonyms:ś:śakti]]---spiritual sparks, living entities; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:m:māyā]]-[[synonyms:ś:śakti]]---illusory energy.

===== Translation =====

**"Lord Kṛṣṇa naturally has three energetic transformations, and these are known as the spiritual potency, the living entity potency and the illusory potency.**
