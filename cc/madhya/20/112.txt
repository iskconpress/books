====== Cc. Madhya 20.112 ======

===== Text =====

> viṣṇu-śaktiḥ parā proktā
> kṣetrajñākhyā tathā parā
> avidyā-karma-saṁjñān yā
> tṛtīyā śaktir iṣyate

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:viṣṇu]]-[[synonyms:ś:śaktiḥ]]---the potency of Lord Viṣṇu; [[synonyms:p:parā]]---spiritual; [[synonyms:p:proktā]]---it is said; [[synonyms:k:kṣetra]]-[[synonyms:j:jña]]-[[synonyms:ā:ākhyā]]---the potency known as kṣetrajña; [[synonyms:t:tathā]]---as well as; [[synonyms:p:parā]]---spiritual; [[synonyms:a:avidyā]]---ignorance; [[synonyms:k:karma]]---fruitive activities; [[synonyms:s:saṁjñā]]---known as; [[synonyms:a:anyā]]---other; [[synonyms:t:tṛtīyā]]---third; [[synonyms:ś:śaktiḥ]]---potency; [[synonyms:i:iṣyate]]---known thus.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Originally, Kṛṣṇa's energy is spiritual, and the energy known as the living entity is also spiritual. However, there is another energy, called illusion, which consists of fruitive activity. That is the Lord's third potency.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from the //Viṣṇu Purāṇa// (6.7.61). For a further explanation of this verse, refer to the //Ādi-līlā,// Chapter Seven, verse 119.
