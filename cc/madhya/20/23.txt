====== CC Madhya 20.23 ======

===== Text =====

> 'ei bhūñā kene more sammāna karila?'
> eta cinti' sanātana īśāne puchila

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:b:bhūñā]]---this landlord; [[synonyms:k:kene]]---why; [[synonyms:m:more]]---unto me; [[synonyms:s:sammāna]] [[synonyms:k:karila]]---offered so much respect; [[synonyms:e:eta]] [[synonyms:c:cinti']]---thinking this; [[synonyms:s:sanātana]]---Sanātana; [[synonyms:ī:īśāne]]---from Īśāna, his servant; [[synonyms:p:puchila]]---inquired.

===== Translation =====

**As a former minister for the Nawab, Sanātana could certainly understand diplomacy. He therefore thought, "Why is this landlord offering me such respect?" Thinking in this way, he questioned his servant, whose name was Īśāna.**
