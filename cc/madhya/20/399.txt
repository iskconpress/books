====== CC Madhya 20.399 ======

===== Text =====

> hariḥ pūrṇatamaḥ pūrṇa-
> taraḥ pūrṇa iti tridhā
> śreṣṭha-madhyādibhiḥ śabdair
> nāṭye yaḥ paripaṭhyate

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:h:hariḥ]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]-[[synonyms:t:tamaḥ]]---most complete; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]-[[synonyms:t:taraḥ]]---more complete; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇaḥ]]---complete; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:d:dhā]]---three stages; [[synonyms:ś:śreṣṭha]]---best; [[synonyms:m:madhya]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādibhiḥ]]---middle, etc.; [[synonyms:ś:śabdaiḥ]]---by the words; [[synonyms:n:nāṭye]]---in books on dramatics; [[synonyms:y:yaḥ]]---who; [[synonyms:p:paripaṭhyate]]---is proclaimed.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'This is stated in the dramatic literatures as "perfect," "more perfect," and "most perfect." Thus Lord Kṛṣṇa manifests Himself in three ways-perfect, more perfect and most perfect.**
