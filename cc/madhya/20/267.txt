====== CC Madhya 20.267 ======

===== Text =====

> ādyo 'vatāraḥ puruṣaḥ parasya
> kālaḥ svabhāvaḥ sad-asan-manaś ca
> dravyaṁ vikāro guṇa indriyāṇi
> virāṭ svarāṭ sthāsnu cariṣṇu bhūmnaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:ādyaḥ]] [[synonyms:a:avatāraḥ]]---the original incarnation; [[synonyms:p:puruṣaḥ]]---the Lord; [[synonyms:p:parasya]]---of the Supreme; [[synonyms:k:kālaḥ]]---time; [[synonyms:s:svabhāvaḥ]]---nature; [[synonyms:s:sat]]-[[synonyms:a:asat]]---cause and effect; [[synonyms:m:manaḥ]] [[synonyms:c:ca]]---as well as the mind; [[synonyms:d:dravyam]]---the five elements; [[synonyms:v:vikāraḥ]]---transformation or the false ego; [[synonyms:g:guṇaḥ]]---modes of nature; [[synonyms:i:indriyāṇi]]---senses; [[synonyms:v:virāṭ]]---the universal form; [[synonyms:s:svarāṭ]]---complete independence; [[synonyms:s:sthāsnu]]---immovable; [[synonyms:c:cariṣṇu]]---movable; [[synonyms:b:bhūmnaḥ]]---of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Kāraṇābdhiśāyī Viṣṇu [Mahā-Viṣṇu] is the first incarnation of the Supreme Lord, and He is the master of eternal time, space, cause and effects, mind, elements, material ego, modes of nature, senses, the universal form of the Lord, Garbhodakaśāyī Viṣṇu, and the sum total of all living beings, both moving and nonmoving.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// [[books:sb:2:6:42|(2.6.42)]]. For an explanation, refer to //Ādi-līlā,// Chapter Five, verse 83.
