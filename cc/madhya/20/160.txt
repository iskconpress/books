====== CC Madhya 20.160 ======

===== Text =====

> yasya prabhā prabhavato jagad-aṇḍa-koṭi-
> koṭiṣv aśeṣa-vasudhādi-vibhūti-bhinnam
> tad brahma niṣkalam anantam aśeṣa-bhūtaṁ
> govindam ādi-puruṣaṁ tam ahaṁ bhajāmi

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yasya]]---of whom; [[synonyms:p:prabhā]]---the effulgence; [[synonyms:p:prabhavataḥ]]---of one who excels in power; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:a:aṇḍa]]---of universes; [[synonyms:k:koṭi]]-[[synonyms:k:koṭiṣu]]---in millions and millions; [[synonyms:a:aśeṣa]]---unlimited; [[synonyms:v:vasudhā]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---with planets and other manifestations; [[synonyms:v:vibhūti]]---with opulences; [[synonyms:b:bhinnam]]---becoming variegated; [[synonyms:t:tat]]---that; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]---Brahman; [[synonyms:n:niṣkalam]]---without parts; [[synonyms:a:anantam]]---unlimited; [[synonyms:a:aśeṣa]]-[[synonyms:b:bhūtam]]---being complete; [[synonyms:g:govindam]]---Lord Govinda; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]-[[synonyms:p:puruṣam]]---the original person; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---Him; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:b:bhajāmi]]---worship.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'I worship Govinda, the primeval Lord, who is endowed with great power. The glowing effulgence of His transcendental form is the impersonal Brahman, which is absolute, complete and unlimited and which displays the varieties of countless planets, with their different opulences, in millions and millions of universes.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse is quoted from //Brahma-saṁhitā// (5.40). For an explanation, refer to //Ādi-līlā,// Chapter Two, verse 14.
