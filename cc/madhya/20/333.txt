====== CC Madhya 20.333 ======

===== Text =====

> tretāyāṁ rakta-varṇo 'sau
> catur-bāhus trimekhalaḥ
> hiraṇya-keśas trayy-ātmā
> sruk-sruv-ādy-upalakṣaṇaḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tretāyām]]---in the Tretā-yuga; [[synonyms:r:rakta]]-[[synonyms:v:varṇaḥ]]---of a reddish color; [[synonyms:a:asau]]---He; [[synonyms:c:catuḥ]]-[[synonyms:b:bāhuḥ]]---with four arms; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:m:mekhalaḥ]]---having three circles on the abdomen; [[synonyms:h:hiraṇya]]-[[synonyms:k:keśaḥ]]---hair colored like gold; [[synonyms:t:trayī]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmā]]---whose form manifests the Vedas; [[synonyms:s:sruk]]-[[synonyms:s:sruv]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]-[[synonyms:u:upalakṣaṇaḥ]]---decorated with the sacrificial spoon, ladle and so on.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'In the Tretā-yuga, the Lord appeared in a body that had a reddish hue and four arms. There were three distinctive lines on His abdomen, and His hair was golden. His form manifested the Vedic knowledge, and He bore the symbols of a sacrificial spoon, ladle and so on.'**
