====== Cc. Madhya 20.157 ======

===== Text =====

> jñāna, yoga, bhakti,---tina sādhanera vaśe
> brahma, ātmā, bhagavān---trividha prakāśe

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:j:jñāna]]---knowledge; [[synonyms:y:yoga]]---mystic power; [[synonyms:b:bhakti]]---devotional service; [[synonyms:t:tina]]---three; [[synonyms:s:sādhanera]]---of the processes of spiritual life; [[synonyms:v:vaśe]]---under the control; [[synonyms:b:brahma]]---impersonal Brahman; [[synonyms:ā:ātmā]]---localized Paramātmā; [[synonyms:b:bhagavān]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:v:vidha]] [[synonyms:p:prakāśe]]---three kinds of manifestation.

===== Translation =====

**"There are three kinds of spiritual processes for understanding the Absolute Truth---the processes of speculative knowledge, mystic yoga and bhakti-yoga. According to these three processes, the Absolute Truth is manifested as Brahman, Paramātmā and Bhagavān.**
