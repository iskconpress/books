====== Cc. Madhya 20.113 ======

===== Text =====

> śaktayaḥ sarva-bhāvānām
> acintya-jñāna-gocarāḥ
> yato 'to brahmaṇas tās tu
> sargādyā bhāva-śaktayaḥ
> bhavanti tapatāṁ śreṣṭha
> pāvakasya yathoṣṇatā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śaktayaḥ]]---energies; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāvānām]]---of all types of creation; [[synonyms:a:acintya]]---inconceivable; [[synonyms:j:jñāna]]-[[synonyms:g:gocarāḥ]]---by the range of man's knowledge; [[synonyms:y:yataḥ]]---from whom; [[synonyms:a:ataḥ]]---therefore; [[synonyms:b:brahmaṇaḥ]]---from the Absolute Truth; [[synonyms:t:tāḥ]]---those; [[synonyms:t:tu]]---but; [[synonyms:s:sarga]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādyāḥ]]---bringing about creation, maintenance and annihilation; [[synonyms:b:bhāva]]-[[synonyms:ś:śaktayaḥ]]---the creative energies; [[synonyms:b:bhavanti]]---are; [[synonyms:t:tapatām]]---of all the ascetics; [[synonyms:ś:śreṣṭha]]---O chief; [[synonyms:p:pāvakasya]]---of fire; [[synonyms:y:yathā]]---as; [[synonyms:u:uṣṇatā]]---heat.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'All the creative energies, which are inconceivable to a common man, exist in the Supreme Absolute Truth. These inconceivable energies act in the process of creation, maintenance and annihilation. O chief of the ascetics, just as there are two energies possessed by fire-namely heat and light---these inconceivable creative energies are the natural characteristics of the Absolute Truth.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from the //Viṣṇu Purāṇa// (1.3.2).
