====== CC Madhya 20.326 ======

===== Text =====

> raivate 'vaikuṇṭha' cākṣuṣe 'ajita', vaivasvate 'vāmana'
> sāvarṇye 'sārvabhauma', dakṣa-sāvarṇye 'ṛṣabha' gaṇana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:r:raivate]]---in the Raivata-manvantara; [[synonyms:v:vaikuṇṭha]]---the avatāra named Vaikuṇṭha; [[synonyms:c:cākṣuṣe]]---in the Cākṣuṣa-manvantara; [[synonyms:a:ajita]]---the avatāra named Ajita; [[synonyms:v:vaivasvate]]---in the Vaivasvata-manvantara; [[synonyms:v:vāmana]]---the avatāra named Vāmana; [[synonyms:s:sāvarṇye]]---in the Sāvarṇya-manvantara; [[synonyms:s:sārvabhauma]]---the avatāra named Sārvabhauma; [[synonyms:d:dakṣa]]-[[synonyms:s:sāvarṇye]]---in the Dakṣa-sāvarṇya-manvantara; [[synonyms:ṛ:ṛṣabha]]---the avatāra Ṛṣabha; [[synonyms:g:gaṇana]]---named.

===== Translation =====

**"In the Raivata-manvantara, the avatāra was named Vaikuṇṭha, and in the Cākṣuṣa-manvantara, he was named Ajita. In the Vaivasvata-manvantara, he was named Vāmana, and in the Sāvarṇya-manvantara, he was named Sārvabhauma. In the Dakṣa-sāvarṇya-manvantara, he was named Ṛṣabha.**
