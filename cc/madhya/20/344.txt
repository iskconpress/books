====== CC Madhya 20.344 ======

===== Text =====

> kaler doṣa-nidhe rājann
> asti hy eko mahān guṇaḥ
> kīrtanād eva kṛṣṇasya
> mukta-bandhaḥ paraṁ vrajet

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kaleḥ]]---of the Age of Kali; [[synonyms:d:doṣa]]-[[synonyms:n:nidhe]]---in the ocean of faults; [[synonyms:r:rājan]]---O King; [[synonyms:a:asti]]---there is; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:e:ekaḥ]]---one; [[synonyms:m:mahān]]---very great; [[synonyms:g:guṇaḥ]]---good quality; [[synonyms:k:kīrtanāt]]---by chanting; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---certainly; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇasya]]---of the holy name of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:m:mukta]]-[[synonyms:b:bandhaḥ]]---liberated from material bondage; [[synonyms:p:param]]---to the transcendental spiritual kingdom; [[synonyms:v:vrajet]]---one can go.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'My dear King, although Kali-yuga is full of faults, there is still one good quality about this age. It is that simply by chanting the Hare Kṛṣṇa mahā-mantra, one can become free from material bondage and be promoted to the transcendental kingdom.**

===== Purport =====

This is a verse from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (12.3.51).
