====== CC Madhya 20.312 ======

===== Text =====

> śivaḥ śakti-yuktaḥ śaśvat
> triliṅgo guṇa-saṁvṛtaḥ
> vaikārikas taijasaś ca
> tāmasaś cety ahaṁ tridhā

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śivaḥ]]---Lord Śiva; [[synonyms:ś:śakti]]-[[synonyms:y:yuktaḥ]]---associated with material nature; [[synonyms:ś:śaśvat]]---eternally; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:l:liṅgaḥ]]---in three features; [[synonyms:g:guṇa]]-[[synonyms:s:saṁvṛtaḥ]]---covered by the modes of nature; [[synonyms:v:vaikārikaḥ]]---one is called vaikārika; [[synonyms:t:taijasaḥ]] [[synonyms:c:ca]]---another is called taijasa; [[synonyms:t:tāmasaḥ]] [[synonyms:c:ca]]---as well as tāmasa; [[synonyms:i:iti]]---thus; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---egotism; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:d:dhā]]---three kinds.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The truth about Lord Śiva is that he is always covered with three material coverings-vaikārika, taijasa and tāmasa. Because of these three modes of material nature, he always associates with the external energy and egotism itself.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.88.3).
