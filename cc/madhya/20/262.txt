====== CC Madhya 20.262 ======

===== Text =====

> etau hi viśvasya ca bīja-yonī
> rāmo mukundaḥ puruṣaḥ pradhānam
> anvīya bhūteṣu vilakṣaṇasya
> jñānasya ceśāta imau purāṇau

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:etau]]---these two, namely Rāma and Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:v:viśvasya]]---of the universe; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:b:bīja]]-[[synonyms:y:yonī]]---both the cause and ingredient; [[synonyms:r:rāmaḥ]]---Balarāma; [[synonyms:m:mukundaḥ]]---Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:p:puruṣaḥ]]---the original Mahā-Viṣṇu; [[synonyms:p:pradhānam]]---material energy; [[synonyms:a:anvīya]]---after entering; [[synonyms:b:bhūteṣu]]---into the material elements; [[synonyms:v:vilakṣaṇasya]]---of varieties of manifestation; [[synonyms:j:jñānasya]]---of knowledge; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---also; [[synonyms:ī:īśāte]]---are the controlling power; [[synonyms:i:imau]]---both of Them; [[synonyms:p:purāṇau]]---are the original cause.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Balarāma and Kṛṣṇa are the original efficient and material causes of the material world. As Mahā-Viṣṇu and the material energy, They enter into the material elements and create the diversities by multi-energies. Thus They are the cause of all causes.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse is quoted from //Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam// (10.46.31).
