====== CC Madhya 1.255-256 ======

===== Text =====

> advaita, nityānanda, mukunda, śrīvāsa
> vidyānidhi, vāsudeva, murāri,---yata dāsa

> prativarṣe āise saṅge rahe cāri-māsa
> tāṅ-sabā lañā prabhura vividha vilāsa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:advaita]]---of the name Advaita; [[synonyms:n:nityānanda]]---of the name Nityānanda; [[synonyms:m:mukunda]]---of the name Mukunda; [[synonyms:ś:śrīvāsa]]---of the name Śrīvāsa; [[synonyms:v:vidyānidhi]]---of the name Vidyānidhi; [[synonyms:v:vāsudeva]]---of the name Vāsudeva; [[synonyms:m:murāri]]---of the name Murāri; [[synonyms:y:yata]] [[synonyms:d:dāsa]]---all servitors of the Lord; [[synonyms:p:prativarṣe]]---each year; [[synonyms:ā:āise]]---go there; [[synonyms:s:saṅge]]---in association; [[synonyms:r:rahe]]---remain; [[synonyms:c:cāri]]-[[synonyms:m:māsa]]---four months; [[synonyms:t:tāṅ]]-[[synonyms:s:sabā]]---all of them; [[synonyms:l:lañā]]---taking; [[synonyms:p:prabhura]]---of the Lord; [[synonyms:v:vividha]]---various; [[synonyms:v:vilāsa]]---pastimes.

===== Translation =====

**Other devotees of the Lord-headed by Advaita Ācārya, Nityānanda Prabhu, Mukunda, Śrīvāsa, Vidyānidhi, Vāsudeva and Murāri-used to visit Jagannātha Purī and remain with the Lord for four continuous months. The Lord enjoyed various pastimes in their company.**
