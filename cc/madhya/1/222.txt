====== Cc. Madhya 1.222 ======

===== Text =====

> ihāṅ haite cala, prabhu, ihāṅ nāhi kāya
> yadyapi tomāre bhakti kare gauḍa-rāja

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:i:ihāṅ]] [[synonyms:h:haite]]---from this place; [[synonyms:c:cala]]---please depart; [[synonyms:p:prabhu]]---dear Lord; [[synonyms:i:ihāṅ]]---in this place; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]] [[synonyms:k:kāya]]---there is no other business; [[synonyms:y:yadyapi]]---although; [[synonyms:t:tomāre]]---unto You; [[synonyms:b:bhakti]]---respect; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---shows; [[synonyms:g:gauḍa]]-[[synonyms:r:rāja]]---the King of Bengal.

===== Translation =====

**They said, "Dear Lord, although the King of Bengal, Nawab Hussain Shah, is very respectful toward You, You have no other business here. Kindly depart from this place.**
