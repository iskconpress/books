====== CC Madhya 1.21 ======

===== Text =====

> 'ādi-līlā', 'madhya-līlā', 'antya-līlā' āra
> ebe 'madhya-līlāra' kichu kariye vistāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]-[[synonyms:l:līlā]] [[synonyms:m:madhya]]-[[synonyms:l:līlā]] [[synonyms:a:antya]]-[[synonyms:l:līlā]] [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---therefore there are three periods, namely the //ādi-līlā, madhya-līlā// and //antya-līlā//; [[synonyms:e:ebe]]---now; [[synonyms:m:madhya]]-[[synonyms:l:līlāra]]---of the //madhya-līlā//; [[synonyms:k:kichu]]---something; [[synonyms:k:kariye]]---I shall do; [[synonyms:v:vistāra]]---elaboration.

===== Translation =====

**The pastimes of the Lord are therefore divided into three periods---the ādi-līlā, madhya-līlā and antya-līlā. Now I shall very elaborately describe the madhya-līlā.**
