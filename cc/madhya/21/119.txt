====== CC Madhya 21.119 ======

===== Text =====

> karma, tapa, yoga, jñāna, vidhi-bhakti, japa, dhyāna,
> ihā haite mādhurya durlabha
> kevala ye rāga-mārge, bhaje kṛṣṇe anurāge,
> tāre kṛṣṇa-mādhurya sulabha

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:karma]]---fruitive activities; [[synonyms:t:tapa]]---austerities; [[synonyms:y:yoga]]---the practice of mystic //yoga//; [[synonyms:j:jñāna]]---speculative cultivation of knowledge; [[synonyms:v:vidhi]]-[[synonyms:b:bhakti]]---regulative principles in devotional service; [[synonyms:j:japa]]---chanting; [[synonyms:d:dhyāna]]---meditation; [[synonyms:i:ihā]] [[synonyms:h:haite]]---from these things; [[synonyms:m:mādhurya]]---the sweetness of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:d:durlabha]]---very difficult to perceive; [[synonyms:k:kevala]]---only; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---one; [[synonyms:r:rāga]]-[[synonyms:m:mārge]]---by the path of spontaneous ecstatic love; [[synonyms:b:bhaje]]---worships; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇe]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:a:anurāge]]---with transcendental feeling; [[synonyms:t:tāre]]---unto him; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:m:mādhurya]]---the sweetness of Kṛṣṇa's; [[synonyms:s:sulabha]]---very easily appreciated.

===== Translation =====

**"The transcendental mellows generated from the dealings between the gopīs and Kṛṣṇa cannot be tasted by fruitive activity, yogic austerities, speculative knowledge, regulative devotional service, mantra-yoga or meditation. This sweetness can be tasted only through the spontaneous love of liberated persons who chant the holy names with great ecstatic love.**
