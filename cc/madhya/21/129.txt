====== Cc. Madhya 21.129 ======

===== Text =====

> nāce makara-kuṇḍala, netra---līlā-kamala,
> vilāsī rājā satata nācāya
> bhrū---dhanu, netra---bāṇa, dhanur-guṇa---dui kāṇa,
> nārī-mana-lakṣya vindhe tāya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nāce]]---dance; [[synonyms:m:makara]]-[[synonyms:k:kuṇḍala]]---the earrings shaped like sharks; [[synonyms:n:netra]]---the eyes; [[synonyms:l:līlā]]---for play; [[synonyms:k:kamala]]---like lotus flowers; [[synonyms:v:vilāsī]]---the enjoyer; [[synonyms:r:rājā]]---the king; [[synonyms:s:satata]] [[synonyms:n:nācāya]]---always causes to dance; [[synonyms:b:bhrū]]---two eyebrows; [[synonyms:d:dhanu]]---just like bows; [[synonyms:n:netra]]---the eyes; [[synonyms:b:bāṇa]]---just like arrows; [[synonyms:d:dhanuḥ]]-[[synonyms:g:guṇa]]---the string of the bow; [[synonyms:d:dui]] [[synonyms:k:kāṇa]]---the two ears; [[synonyms:n:nārī]]-[[synonyms:m:mana]]---the minds of the //gopīs//; [[synonyms:l:lakṣya]]---the target; [[synonyms:v:vindhe]]---pierces; [[synonyms:t:tāya]]---there.

===== Translation =====

**"Kṛṣṇa's face is the enjoyer king. That full-moon face makes His shark-shaped earrings and lotus eyes dance. His eyebrows are like bows, and His eyes are like arrows. His ears are fixed on the string of that bow, and when His eyes spread to His ears, He pierces the hearts of the gopīs.**
