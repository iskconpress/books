====== CC Madhya 21.41 ======

===== Text =====

> yasyaika-niśvasita-kālam athāvalambya
> jīvanti loma-vilajā jagad-aṇḍa-nāthāḥ
> viṣṇur mahān sa iha yasya kalā-viśeṣo
> govindam ādi-puruṣaṁ tam ahaṁ bhajāmi

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yasya]]---whose; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:n:niśvasita]]---of breath; [[synonyms:k:kālam]]---time; [[synonyms:a:atha]]---thus; [[synonyms:a:avalambya]]---taking shelter of; [[synonyms:j:jīvanti]]---live; [[synonyms:l:loma]]-[[synonyms:v:vilajāḥ]]---grown from the hair holes; [[synonyms:j:jagat]]-[[synonyms:a:aṇḍa]]-[[synonyms:n:nāthāḥ]]---the masters of the universes (the Brahmās); [[synonyms:v:viṣṇuḥ]] [[synonyms:m:mahān]]---the Supreme Lord Mahā-Viṣṇu; [[synonyms:s:saḥ]]---that; [[synonyms:i:iha]]---here; [[synonyms:y:yasya]]---whose; [[synonyms:k:kalā]]-[[synonyms:v:viśeṣaḥ]]---particular plenary portion or expansion; [[synonyms:g:govindam]]---Lord Govinda; [[synonyms:ā:ādi]]-[[synonyms:p:puruṣam]]---the original person; [[synonyms:t:tam]]---Him; [[synonyms:a:aham]]---I; [[synonyms:b:bhajāmi]]---worship.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The Brahmās and other lords of the mundane worlds appear from the pores of the Mahā-Viṣṇu and remain alive for the duration of His one exhalation. I adore the primeval Lord, Govinda, for Mahā-Viṣṇu is a portion of His plenary portion.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from //Brahma-saṁhitā// (5.48). See also //Ādi-līlā// (5.71).
