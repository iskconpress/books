====== CC Madhya 21.95 ======

===== Text =====

> maṇi-pīṭhe ṭhekāṭheki, uṭhe jhanjhani
> pīṭhera stuti kare mukuṭa---hena anumāni

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:m:maṇi]]-[[synonyms:p:pīṭhe]]---between the gems and the lotus feet or the throne; [[synonyms:ṭ:ṭhekāṭheki]]---collision; [[synonyms:u:uṭhe]]---arises; [[synonyms:j:jhanjhani]]---a jingling sound; [[synonyms:p:pīṭhera]]---to the lotus feet or the throne; [[synonyms:s:stuti]]---prayers; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---offer; [[synonyms:m:mukuṭa]]---all the helmets; [[synonyms:h:hena]]---thus; [[synonyms:a:anumāni]]---we can imagine.

===== Translation =====

**"When the gems on the helmets of all the predominating deities collide before the throne and the Lord's lotus feet, there is a jingling sound, which seems like prayers offered by the helmets at Kṛṣṇa's lotus feet.**
