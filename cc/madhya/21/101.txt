====== CC Madhya 21.101 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇera yateka khelā, sarvottama nara-līlā,
> nara-vapu tāhāra svarūpa
> gopa-veśa, veṇu-kara, nava-kiśora, naṭa-vara,
> nara-līlāra haya anurūpa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇera]]---of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:y:yateka]]---all; [[synonyms:k:khelā]]---pastimes; [[synonyms:s:sarva]]-[[synonyms:u:uttama]]---the most attractive of all; [[synonyms:n:nara]]-[[synonyms:l:līlā]]---the pastimes as a human being; [[synonyms:n:nara]]-[[synonyms:v:vapu]]---a body just like that of a human being; [[synonyms:t:tāhāra]]---of that; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---the real form; [[synonyms:g:gopa]]-[[synonyms:v:veśa]]---the dress of a cowherd boy; [[synonyms:v:veṇu]]-[[synonyms:k:kara]]---with a flute in the hands; [[synonyms:n:nava]]-[[synonyms:k:kiśora]]---newly youthful; [[synonyms:n:naṭa]]-[[synonyms:v:vara]]---an expert dancer; [[synonyms:n:nara]]-[[synonyms:l:līlāra]]---for exhibiting the pastimes as a human being; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---is; [[synonyms:a:anurūpa]]---suitable.

===== Translation =====

**"Lord Kṛṣṇa has many pastimes, of which His pastimes as a human being are the best. His form as a human being is the supreme transcendental form. In this form He is a cowherd boy. He carries a flute in His hand, and His youth is new. He is also an expert dancer. All this is just suitable for His pastimes as a human being.**
