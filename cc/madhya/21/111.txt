====== CC Madhya 21.111 ======

===== Text =====

> kahite kṛṣṇera rase, śloka paḍe premāveśe,
> preme sanātana-hāta dhari'
> gopī-bhāgya, kṛṣṇa guṇa, ye karila varṇana,
> bhāvāveśe mathurā-nāgarī

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kahite]]---to describe; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇera]]---of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:r:rase]]---the different types of mellows; [[synonyms:ś:śloka]]---a verse; [[synonyms:p:paḍe]]---recites; [[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśe]]---absorbed in ecstatic love; [[synonyms:p:preme]]---in such love; [[synonyms:s:sanātana]]-[[synonyms:h:hāta]] [[synonyms:d:dhari']]---catching the hand of Sanātana Gosvāmī; [[synonyms:g:gopī]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāgya]]---the fortune of the //gopīs//; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]] [[synonyms:g:guṇa]]---the transcendental qualities of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---which; [[synonyms:k:karila]] [[synonyms:v:varṇana]]---described; [[synonyms:b:bhāva]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśe]]---in ecstatic love; [[synonyms:m:mathurā]]-[[synonyms:n:nāgarī]]---the women of the city of Mathurā.

===== Translation =====

**Just as the women of Mathurā ecstatically described the fortune of the gopīs of Vṛndāvana and the transcendental qualities of Kṛṣṇa, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu described the different mellows of Kṛṣṇa and became overwhelmed with ecstatic love. Grasping the hand of Sanātana Gosvāmī, He recited the following verse.**
