====== Cc. Madhya 21.56 ======

===== Text =====

> tripād-vibhūter dhāmatvāt
> tripād-bhūtaṁ hi tat padam
> vibhūtir māyikī sarvā
> proktā pādātmikā yataḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:p:pād]]-[[synonyms:v:vibhūteḥ]]---of the three fourths of the energy; [[synonyms:d:dhāmatvāt]]---because of being the abode; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:p:pād]]-[[synonyms:b:bhūtam]]---consisting of three fourths of the energy; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:t:tat]] [[synonyms:p:padam]]---that abode; [[synonyms:v:vibhūtiḥ]]---the energy or potency; [[synonyms:m:māyikī]]---material; [[synonyms:s:sarvā]]---all; [[synonyms:p:proktā]]---said; [[synonyms:p:pāda]]-[[synonyms:ā:ātmikā]]---only one fourth; [[synonyms:y:yataḥ]]---therefore.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Because it consists of three fourths of the Lord's energy, the spiritual world is called tripād-bhūta. Being a manifestation of one fourth of the Lord's energy, the material world is called eka-pāda.'**

===== Purport =====

This verse is found in //Laghu-bhāgavatāmṛta// (1.5.286).
