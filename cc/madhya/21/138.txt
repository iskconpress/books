====== CC Madhya 21.138 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇāṅga---lāvaṇya-pūra, madhura haite sumadhura,
> tāte yei mukha sudhākara
> madhura haite sumadhura, tāhā ha-ite sumadhura,
> tāra yei smita jyotsnā-bhara

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅga]]---the bodily features of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:l:lāvaṇya]]-[[synonyms:p:pūra]]---the city of attractive beauty; [[synonyms:m:madhura]]---sweetness; [[synonyms:h:haite]]---than; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:m:madhura]]---still more sweet; [[synonyms:t:tāte]]---in that body; [[synonyms:y:yei]]---that; [[synonyms:m:mukha]]---face; [[synonyms:s:sudhākara]]---like the moon; [[synonyms:m:madhura]] [[synonyms:h:haite]] [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:m:madhura]]---sweeter than sweetness; [[synonyms:t:tāhā]] [[synonyms:h:ha]]-[[synonyms:i:ite]]---than that; [[synonyms:s:su]]-[[synonyms:m:madhura]]---still more sweet; [[synonyms:t:tāra]]---of which; [[synonyms:y:yei]]---that; [[synonyms:s:smita]]---smiling; [[synonyms:j:jyotsnā]]-[[synonyms:b:bhara]]---like the moonshine.

===== Translation =====

**"Kṛṣṇa's body is a city of attractive features, and it is sweeter than sweet. His face, which is like the moon, is sweeter still, and the gentle smile on that moonlike face is like rays of moonshine.**

===== Purport =====

The smile on Kṛṣṇa's face is just like the smiling of the moon, which generates greater and greater happiness for the //gopīs.//
