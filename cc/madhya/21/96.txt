====== CC Madhya 21.96 ======

===== Text =====

> nija-cic-chakte kṛṣṇa nitya virājamāna
> cic-chakti-sampattira 'ṣaḍ-aiśvarya' nāma

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nija]]---His own; [[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:ś:śakte]]---in spiritual potency; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:n:nitya]]---eternally; [[synonyms:v:virājamāna]]---existing; [[synonyms:c:cit]]-[[synonyms:ś:śakti]]---of the spiritual potency; [[synonyms:s:sampattira]]---of the opulence; [[synonyms:ṣ:ṣaṭ]]-[[synonyms:a:aiśvarya]]---the six opulences; [[synonyms:n:nāma]]---named.

===== Translation =====

**"Kṛṣṇa is thus situated eternally in His spiritual potency, and the opulence of that spiritual potency is called ṣaḍ-aiśvarya, indicating six kinds of opulence.**
