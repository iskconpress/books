====== Cc. Madhya 21.122 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇa dekhi' nānā jana, kaila nimiṣe nindana,
> vraje vidhi ninde gopī-gaṇa
> sei saba śloka paḍi', mahāprabhu artha kari',
> sukhe mādhurya kare āsvādana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:d:dekhi']]---seeing; [[synonyms:n:nānā]] [[synonyms:j:jana]]---various persons; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---did; [[synonyms:n:nimiṣe]]---due to the blinking of the eyes; [[synonyms:n:nindana]]---blaming; [[synonyms:v:vraje]]---in Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:v:vidhi]]---Lord Brahmā; [[synonyms:n:ninde]]---blame; [[synonyms:g:gopī]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---all the //gopīs//; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:s:saba]]---all those; [[synonyms:ś:śloka]]---verses; [[synonyms:p:paḍi']]---reciting; [[synonyms:m:mahāprabhu]]---Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:a:artha]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---explaining the meaning; [[synonyms:s:sukhe]]---in happiness; [[synonyms:m:mādhurya]]---transcendental sweetness; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:ā:āsvādana]]---tasting.

===== Translation =====

**"After seeing Kṛṣṇa, various people criticize the blinking of their eyes. In Vṛndāvana, especially, all the gopīs criticize Lord Brahmā because of this defect in the eyes." Then Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu recited some verses from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam and explained them vividly, thus enjoying the taste of transcendental sweetness with great happiness.**
