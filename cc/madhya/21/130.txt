====== Cc. Madhya 21.130 ======

===== Text =====

> ei cāndera baḍa nāṭa, pasāri' cāndera hāṭa,
> vinimūle vilāya nijāmṛta
> kāhoṅ smita-jyotsnāmṛte, kāṅhāre adharāmṛte,
> saba loka kare āpyāyita

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:c:cāndera]]---of this moon of the face; [[synonyms:b:baḍa]]---big; [[synonyms:n:nāṭa]]---dancing; [[synonyms:p:pasāri']]---expanding; [[synonyms:c:cāndera]] [[synonyms:h:hāṭa]]---the marketplace of full moons; [[synonyms:v:vinimūle]]---without a price; [[synonyms:v:vilāya]]---distributes; [[synonyms:n:nija]]-[[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---its personal nectar; [[synonyms:k:kāhoṅ]]---to some; [[synonyms:s:smita]]-[[synonyms:j:jyotsnā]]-[[synonyms:a:amṛte]]---by the nectar of the sweetly smiling moonrays; [[synonyms:k:kāṅhāre]]---to someone; [[synonyms:a:adhara]]-[[synonyms:a:amṛte]]---by the nectar of the lips; [[synonyms:s:saba]] [[synonyms:l:loka]]---all people; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:ā:āpyāyita]]---pleases.

===== Translation =====

**"The dancing features of His face surpass all other full moons and expand the marketplace of full moons. Although priceless, the nectar of Kṛṣṇa's face is distributed to everyone. Some purchase the moonrays of His sweet smiles, and others purchase the nectar of His lips. Thus He pleases everyone.**
