====== CC Madhya 14.117-118 ======

===== Text =====

> yadyapi jagannātha karena dvārakāya vihāra
> sahaja prakaṭa kare parama udāra
>
> tathāpi vatsara-madhye haya eka-bāra
> vṛndāvana dekhite tāṅra utkaṇṭhā apāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yadyapi]]---although; [[synonyms:j:jagannātha]]---Lord Jagannātha; [[synonyms:k:karena]]---does; [[synonyms:d:dvārakāya]]---in Dvārakā-dhāma; [[synonyms:v:vihāra]]---enjoyment; [[synonyms:s:sahaja]]---natural; [[synonyms:p:prakaṭa]]---manifestation; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---does; [[synonyms:p:parama]]---sublime; [[synonyms:u:udāra]]---liberal; [[synonyms:t:tathāpi]]---still; [[synonyms:v:vatsara]]-[[synonyms:m:madhye]]---within a year; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---becomes; [[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:b:bāra]]---once; [[synonyms:v:vṛndāvana]] [[synonyms:d:dekhite]]---to visit Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:t:tāṅra]]---His; [[synonyms:u:utkaṇṭhā]]---eagerness; [[synonyms:a:apāra]]---unlimited.

===== Translation =====

**"Although Lord Jagannātha enjoys His pastimes at Dvārakā-dhāma and naturally manifests sublime liberality there, still, once a year, He becomes unlimitedly eager to see Vṛndāvana."**
