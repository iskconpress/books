====== Cc. Madhya 14.168 ======

===== Text =====

> 'kila-kiñcita', 'kuṭṭamita', 'vilāsa', 'lalita'
> 'vivvoka', 'moṭṭāyita', āra 'maugdhya', 'cakita'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kila]]-[[synonyms:k:kiñcita]]---a particular type of ornament at the time of seeing Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:k:kuṭṭamita]]---the symptom explained in verse 197; [[synonyms:v:vilāsa]]---the symptom explained in verse 187; [[synonyms:l:lalita]]---the symptom explained in verse 192; [[synonyms:v:vivvoka]]---neglecting the presentation given by the hero; [[synonyms:m:moṭṭāyita]]---awakening of lusty desires by the remembrance and words of the hero; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:m:maugdhya]]---assuming the position of not knowing things although everything is known; [[synonyms:c:cakita]]---a position in which the heroine appears very afraid although she is not at all afraid.

===== Translation =====

**"Some of the symptoms critically explained in the following verses are kilakiñcita, kuṭṭamita, vilāsa, lalita, vivvoka, moṭṭāyita, maugdhya and cakita.**
