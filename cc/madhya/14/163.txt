====== CC Madhya 14.163 ======

===== Text =====

> aher iva gatiḥ premṇaḥ
>   svabhāva-kuṭilā bhavet
> ato hetor ahetoś ca
> yūnor māna udañcati

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:aheḥ]]---of the snake; [[synonyms:i:iva]]---like; [[synonyms:g:gatiḥ]]---the movement; [[synonyms:p:premṇaḥ]]---of the loving affairs; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāva]]---by nature; [[synonyms:k:kuṭilā]]---crooked; [[synonyms:b:bhavet]]---is; [[synonyms:a:ataḥ]]---therefore; [[synonyms:h:hetoḥ]]---from some cause; [[synonyms:a:ahetoḥ]]---from the absence of a cause; [[synonyms:c:ca]]---and; [[synonyms:y:yūnoḥ]]---of the young couple; [[synonyms:m:mānaḥ]]---anger; [[synonyms:u:udañcati]]---appears.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'The progress of loving affairs between young couples is by nature crooked, like the movement of a snake. Because of this, two types of anger arise between young couples-anger with a cause and anger without a cause.' "**

===== Purport =====

This is a quotation from Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī's //Ujjvala-nīlamaṇi// (//Śṛṅgāra-bheda-prakaraṇa// 102).
