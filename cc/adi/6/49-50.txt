====== CC Ādi 6.49-50 ======

===== Text =====

> śrīvāsa, haridāsa, rāmadāsa, gadādhara
> murāri, mukunda, candraśekhara, vakreśvara
>
> e-saba paṇḍita-loka parama-mahattva
> caitanyera dāsye sabāya karaye unmatta

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrīvāsa]]---Śrīvāsa Ṭhākura; [[synonyms:h:haridāsa]]---Haridāsa Ṭhākura; [[synonyms:r:rāmadāsa]]---Rāmadāsa; [[synonyms:g:gadādhara]]---Gadādhara; [[synonyms:m:murāri]]---Murāri; [[synonyms:m:mukunda]]---Mukunda; [[synonyms:c:candraśekhara]]---Candraśekhara; [[synonyms:v:vakreśvara]]---Vakreśvara; [[synonyms:e:e]]-[[synonyms:s:saba]]---all of them; [[synonyms:p:paṇḍita]]-[[synonyms:l:loka]]---very learned scholars; [[synonyms:p:parama]]-[[synonyms:m:mahattva]]---very much glorified; [[synonyms:c:caitanyera]]---of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:d:dāsye]]---the servitude; [[synonyms:s:sabāya]]---all of them; [[synonyms:k:karayeunmatta]]---makes mad.

===== Translation =====

**Śrīvāsa, Haridāsa, Rāmadāsa, Gadādhara, Murāri, Mukunda, Candraśekhara and Vakreśvara are all glorious and are all learned scholars, but the sentiment of servitude to Lord Caitanya makes them mad in ecstasy.**
