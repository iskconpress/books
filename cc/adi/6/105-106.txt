====== CC Ādi 6.105-106 ======

===== Text =====

> bhakta-bhāva aṅgīkari' balarāma, lakṣmaṇa
> advaita, nityānanda, śeṣa, saṅkarṣaṇa

> kṛṣṇera mādhurya-rasāmṛta kare pāna
> sei sukhe matta, kichu nāhi jāne āna

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:b:bhakta]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāva]]---the conception of being a devotee; [[synonyms:a:aṅgīkari']]---accepting; [[synonyms:b:balarāma]]---Lord Balarāma; [[synonyms:l:lakṣmaṇa]]---Lord Lakṣmaṇa; [[synonyms:a:advaita]]---Advaita Ācārya; [[synonyms:n:nityānanda]]---Lord Nityānanda; [[synonyms:ś:śeṣa]]---Lord Śeṣa; [[synonyms:s:saṅkarṣaṇa]]---Lord Saṅkarṣaṇa; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇera]]---of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:m:mādhurya]]---transcendental bliss; [[synonyms:r:rasa]]-[[synonyms:a:amṛta]]---the nectar of such a taste; [[synonyms:k:kare]] [[synonyms:p:pāna]]---they drink; [[synonyms:s:sei]] [[synonyms:s:sukhe]]---in such happiness; [[synonyms:m:matta]]---mad; [[synonyms:k:kichu]]---anything; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---do not; [[synonyms:j:jāne]]---know; [[synonyms:ā:āna]]---else.

===== Translation =====

**Baladeva, Lakṣmaṇa, Advaita Ācārya, Lord Nityānanda, Lord Śeṣa and Lord Saṅkarṣaṇa taste the nectarean mellows of the transcendental bliss of Lord Kṛṣṇa by recognizing Themselves as being His devotees and servants. They are all mad with that happiness, and they know nothing else.**
