====== CC Ādi 1.69-70 ======

===== Text =====

> eka-i vigraha yadi haya bahu-rūpa
> ākāre ta' bheda nāhi, eka-i svarūpa

> mahiṣī-vivāhe, yaiche yaiche kaila rāsa
> ihāke kahiye kṛṣṇera mukhya 'prakāśa'

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:i:i]]---the same one; [[synonyms:v:vigraha]]---person; [[synonyms:y:yadi]]---if; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---becomes; [[synonyms:b:bahu]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---many forms; [[synonyms:ā:ākāre]]---in appearance; [[synonyms:t:ta']]---certainly; [[synonyms:b:bheda]]---difference; [[synonyms:n:nāhi]]---there is not; [[synonyms:e:eka]]-[[synonyms:i:i]]---one; [[synonyms:s:sva]]-[[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---identity; [[synonyms:m:mahiṣī]]---with the queens of Dvārakā; [[synonyms:v:vivāhe]]---in the marriage; [[synonyms:y:yaiche]] [[synonyms:y:yaiche]]---in a similar way; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---He did; [[synonyms:r:rāsa]]---//rāsa// dance; [[synonyms:i:ihāke]]---this; [[synonyms:k:kahiye]]---I say; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇera]]---of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:m:mukhya]]---principal; [[synonyms:p:prakāśa]]---manifested forms.

===== Translation =====

**When the Personality of Godhead expands Himself in many forms, all nondifferent in Their features, as Lord Kṛṣṇa did when He married sixteen thousand queens and when He performed His rāsa dance, such forms of the Lord are called manifested forms [prakāśa-vigrahas].**
