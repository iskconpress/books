====== CC Ādi 1.65-66 ======

===== Text =====

> īśvarera avatāra e-tina prakāra
> aṁśa-avatāra, āra guṇa-avatāra
>
> śaktyāveśa-avatāra---tṛtīya e-mata
> aṁśa-avatāra---puruṣa-matsyādika yata

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ī:īśvarera]]---of the Supreme Lord; [[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---incarnations; [[synonyms:e:e]]-[[synonyms:t:tina]]---these three; [[synonyms:p:prakāra]]---kinds; [[synonyms:a:aṁśa]]-[[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---partial incarnations; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:g:guṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---qualitative incarnations; [[synonyms:ś:śakti]]-[[synonyms:ā:āveśa]]-[[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---empowered incarnations; [[synonyms:t:tṛtīya]]---the third; [[synonyms:e:e]]-[[synonyms:m:mata]]---thus; [[synonyms:a:aṁśa]]-[[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---partial incarnations; [[synonyms:p:puruṣa]]---the three //puruṣa// incarnations; [[synonyms:m:matsya]]---the fish incarnation; [[synonyms:ā:ādika]]---and so on; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---all.

===== Translation =====

**There are three categories of incarnations of Godhead: partial incarnations, qualitative incarnations and empowered incarnations. The puruṣas and Matsya are examples of partial incarnations.**
