====== Cc. Ādi 1.88-89 ======

===== Text =====

> sūrya-candra hare yaiche saba andhakāra
> vastu prakāśiyā kare dharmera pracāra

> ei mata dui bhāi jīvera ajñāna-
> tamo-nāśa kari' kaila tattva-vastu-dāna

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sūrya]]-[[synonyms:c:candra]]---the sun and the moon; [[synonyms:h:hare]]---drive away; [[synonyms:y:yaiche]]---just as; [[synonyms:s:saba]]---all; [[synonyms:a:andhakāra]]---darkness; [[synonyms:v:vastu]]---truth; [[synonyms:p:prakāśiyā]]---manifesting; [[synonyms:k:kare]]---do; [[synonyms:d:dharmera]]---of inborn nature; [[synonyms:p:pracāra]]---preaching; [[synonyms:e:ei]] [[synonyms:m:mata]]---like this; [[synonyms:d:dui]]---two; [[synonyms:b:bhāi]]---brothers; [[synonyms:j:jīvera]]---of the living being; [[synonyms:a:ajñāna]]---of ignorance; [[synonyms:t:tamaḥ]]---of the darkness; [[synonyms:n:nāśa]]---destruction; [[synonyms:k:kari']]---doing; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---made; [[synonyms:t:tattva]]-[[synonyms:v:vastu]]---of the Absolute Truth; [[synonyms:d:dāna]]---gift.

===== Translation =====

**As the sun and moon drive away darkness by their appearance and reveal the nature of everything, these two brothers dissipate the darkness of ignorance covering the living beings' and enlighten them with knowledge of the Absolute Truth.**
