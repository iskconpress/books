====== CC Ādi 1.17 ======

===== Text =====

> śrīmān rāsa-rasārambhī
> vaṁśīvaṭa-taṭa-sthitaḥ
> karṣan veṇu-svanair gopīr
> gopī-nāthaḥ śriye 'stu naḥ

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:m:mān]]---most beautiful; [[synonyms:r:rāsa]]---of the //rāsa// dance; [[synonyms:r:rasa]]---of the mellow; [[synonyms:ā:ārambhī]]---the initiator; [[synonyms:v:vaṁśī]]-[[synonyms:v:vaṭa]]---of the name Vaṁśīvaṭa; [[synonyms:t:taṭa]]---on the shore; [[synonyms:s:sthitaḥ]]---standing; [[synonyms:k:karṣan]]---attracting; [[synonyms:v:veṇu]]---of the flute; [[synonyms:s:svanaiḥ]]---by the sounds; [[synonyms:g:gopīḥ]]---the cowherd girls; [[synonyms:g:gopī]]-[[synonyms:n:nāthaḥ]]---Śrī Gopīnātha; [[synonyms:ś:śriye]]---benediction; [[synonyms:a:astu]]---let there be; [[synonyms:n:naḥ]]---our.

===== Translation =====

**Śrī Śrīla Gopīnātha, who originated the transcendental mellow of the rāsa dance, stands on the shore in Vaṁśīvaṭa and attracts the attention of the cowherd damsels with the sound of His celebrated flute. May they all confer upon us their benediction.**
