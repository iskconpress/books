====== CC Ādi 1.37 ======

===== Text =====

> ei chaya guru---śikṣā-guru ye āmāra
> tāṅ'-sabāra pāda-padme koṭi namaskāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:e:ei]]---these; [[synonyms:c:chaya]]---six; [[synonyms:g:guru]]---spiritual masters; [[synonyms:ś:śikṣā]]-[[synonyms:g:guru]]---instructing spiritual masters; [[synonyms:y:ye]]---who are; [[synonyms:ā:āmāra]]---my; [[synonyms:t:tāṅ']]-[[synonyms:s:sabāra]]---of all of them; [[synonyms:p:pāda]]-[[synonyms:p:padme]]---unto the lotus feet; [[synonyms:k:koṭi]]---ten million; [[synonyms:n:namaskāra]]---respectful obeisances.

===== Translation =====

**These six are my instructing spiritual masters, and therefore I offer millions of respectful obeisances unto their lotus feet.**

===== Purport =====

By accepting the six Gosvāmīs as his instructing spiritual masters, the author specifically makes it clear that one should not be recognized as a Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇava if he is not obedient to them.
