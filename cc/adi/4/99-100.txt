====== Cc. Ādi 4.99-100 ======

===== Text =====

> prema-bhakti śikhāite āpane avatari
> rādhā-bhāva-kānti dui aṅgīkāra kari'
>
> śrī-kṛṣṇa-caitanya-rūpe kaila avatāra
> ei ta' pañcama ślokera artha paracāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:p:prema]]-[[synonyms:b:bhakti]]---devotional service in love of Godhead; [[synonyms:ś:śikhāite]]---to teach; [[synonyms:ā:āpane]]---Himself; [[synonyms:a:avatari]]---descending; [[synonyms:r:rādhā]]-[[synonyms:b:bhāva]]---the mood of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:k:kānti]]---and luster; [[synonyms:d:dui]]---two; [[synonyms:a:aṅgīkāra]] [[synonyms:k:kari']]---accepting; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:c:caitanya]]---of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:r:rūpe]]---in the form; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---made; [[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---incarnation; [[synonyms:e:ei]]---this; [[synonyms:t:ta']]---certainly; [[synonyms:p:pañcama]]---fifth; [[synonyms:ś:ślokera]]---of the verse; [[synonyms:a:artha]]---meaning; [[synonyms:p:paracāra]]---proclamation.

===== Translation =====

**To promulgate prema-bhakti [devotional service in love of Godhead], Kṛṣṇa appeared as Śrī Kṛṣṇa Caitanya with the mood and complexion of Śrī Rādhā. Thus I have explained the meaning of the fifth verse.**
