====== CC Ādi 4.74-75 ======

===== Text =====

> kṛṣṇa-kāntā-gaṇa dekhi tri-vidha prakāra
> eka lakṣmī-gaṇa, pure mahiṣī-gaṇa āra
>
> vrajāṅganā-rūpa, āra kāntā-gaṇa-sāra
> śrī-rādhikā haite kāntā-gaṇera vistāra

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:k:kāntā]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---the lovers of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:d:dekhi]]---I see; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:v:vidha]]---three; [[synonyms:p:prakāra]]---kinds; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:l:lakṣmī]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---the goddesses of fortune; [[synonyms:p:pure]]---in the city; [[synonyms:m:mahiṣī]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---the queens; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---and; [[synonyms:v:vraja]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅganā]]---of the beautiful women of Vraja; [[synonyms:r:rūpa]]---having the form; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---another type; [[synonyms:k:kāntā]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇa]]---of the lovers; [[synonyms:s:sāra]]---the essence; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:r:rādhikā]] [[synonyms:h:haite]]---from Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī; [[synonyms:k:kāntā]]-[[synonyms:g:gaṇera]]---of the lovers of Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:v:vistāra]]---the expansion.

===== Translation =====

**The beloved consorts of Lord Kṛṣṇa are of three kinds: the goddesses of fortune, the queens, and the milkmaids of Vraja, who are the foremost of all. These consorts all proceed from Rādhikā.**
