====== CC Ādi 4.11-12 ======

===== Text =====

> nārāyaṇa, catur-vyūha, matsyādy-avatāra
> yuga-manvantarāvatāra, yata āche āra
>
> sabe āsi' kṛṣṇa-aṅge haya avatīrṇa
> aiche avatare kṛṣṇa bhagavān pūrṇa

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nārāyaṇa]]---Lord Nārāyaṇa; [[synonyms:c:catuḥ]]-[[synonyms:v:vyūha]]---the four expansions; [[synonyms:m:matsya]]-[[synonyms:ā:ādi]]---beginning with Matsya; [[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---the incarnations; [[synonyms:y:yuga]]-[[synonyms:m:manv]]-[[synonyms:a:antara]]-[[synonyms:a:avatāra]]---the //yuga// and //manv-antara// incarnations; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---as many as; [[synonyms:ā:āche]]---there are; [[synonyms:ā:āra]]---other; [[synonyms:s:sabe]]---all; [[synonyms:ā:āsi']]---coming; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]-[[synonyms:a:aṅge]]---in the body of Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---are; [[synonyms:a:avatīrṇa]]---incarnated; [[synonyms:a:aiche]]---in this way; [[synonyms:a:avatare]]---incarnates; [[synonyms:k:kṛṣṇa]]---Lord Kṛṣṇa; [[synonyms:b:bhagavān]]---the Supreme Personality of Godhead; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]---full.

===== Translation =====

**Lord Nārāyaṇa, the four primary expansions [Vāsudeva, Saṅkarṣaṇa, Pradyumna and Aniruddha], Matsya and the other līlā incarnations, the yuga-avatāras, the manv-antara incarnations and as many other incarnations as there are-all descend in the body of Lord Kṛṣṇa. In this way the complete Supreme Godhead, Lord Kṛṣṇa Himself, appears.**
