====== CC Ādi 5.218-219 ======

===== Text =====

> vṛndāvane yoga-pīṭhe kalpa-taru-vane
> ratna-maṇḍapa, tāhe ratna-siṁhāsane
>
> śrī-govinda vasiyāchena vrajendra-nandana
> mādhurya prakāśi' karena jagat mohana

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:v:vṛndāvane]]---at Vṛndāvana; [[synonyms:y:yoga]]-[[synonyms:p:pīṭhe]]---at the principal temple; [[synonyms:k:kalpa]]-[[synonyms:t:taru]]-[[synonyms:v:vane]]---in the forest of desire trees; [[synonyms:r:ratna]]-[[synonyms:m:maṇḍapa]]---an altar made of gems; [[synonyms:t:tāhe]]---upon it; [[synonyms:r:ratna]]-[[synonyms:s:siṁha]]-[[synonyms:ā:āsane]]---on the throne of gems; [[synonyms:ś:śrī]]-[[synonyms:g:govinda]]---Lord Govinda; [[synonyms:v:vasiyāchena]]---was sitting; [[synonyms:v:vrajendra]]-[[synonyms:n:nandana]]---the son of Nanda Mahārāja; [[synonyms:m:mādhurya]] [[synonyms:p:prakāśi']]---manifesting His sweetness; [[synonyms:k:karena]]---does; [[synonyms:j:jagat]] [[synonyms:m:mohana]]---enchantment of the whole world.

===== Translation =====

**On an altar made of gems in the principal temple of Vṛndāvana, amidst a forest of desire trees, Lord Govinda, the son of the King of Vraja, sits upon a throne of gems and manifests His full glory and sweetness, thus enchanting the entire world.**
