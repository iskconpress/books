====== CC Ādi 16.70 ======

===== Text =====

> sundara śarīra yaiche bhūṣaṇe bhūṣita
> eka śveta-kuṣṭhe yaiche karaye vigīta

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:s:sundara]]---beautiful; [[synonyms:ś:śarīra]]---body; [[synonyms:y:yaiche]]---as; [[synonyms:b:bhūṣaṇe]]---with ornaments; [[synonyms:b:bhūṣita]]---decorated; [[synonyms:e:eka]]---one; [[synonyms:ś:śveta]]-[[synonyms:k:kuṣṭhe]]---with a white spot of leprosy; [[synonyms:y:yaiche]]---as; [[synonyms:k:karaye]]---is made; [[synonyms:v:vigīta]]---abominable.

===== Translation =====

**"One's beautiful body may be decorated with jewels, but one spot of white leprosy makes the entire body abominable.**

===== Purport =====

The great sage Bharata Muni, an authority on poetic metaphor, has given his opinion in this connection as follows.
