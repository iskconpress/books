{{description>" 'Without first mentioning what is known, one should not introduce the unknown, for that which has no solid basis can never be established anywhere.'}}

====== Cc. Ādi 16.58 ======

===== Text =====

> anuvādam anuktvaiva
> na vidheyam udīrayet
> na hy alabdhāspadaṁ kiñcit
> kutracit pratitiṣṭhati

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:a:anuvādam]]---things already known; [[synonyms:a:anuktvā]]---without mentioning; [[synonyms:e:eva]]---certainly; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:v:vidheyam]]---unknown subject matters; [[synonyms:u:udīrayet]]---one should mention; [[synonyms:n:na]]---not; [[synonyms:h:hi]]---certainly; [[synonyms:a:alabdha]]-[[synonyms:ā:āspadam]]---without having achieved a proper place; [[synonyms:k:kiñcit]]---something; [[synonyms:k:kutracit]]---anywhere; [[synonyms:p:pratitiṣṭhati]]---has a position.

===== Translation =====

**“ 'Without first mentioning what is known, one should not introduce the unknown, for that which has no solid basis can never be established anywhere.'**

===== Purport =====

This is a verse from the //Ekādaśī-tattva.//
