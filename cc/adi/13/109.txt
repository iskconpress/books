====== CC Ādi 13.109 ======

===== Text =====

> yautuka pāila yata, ghare vā āchila kata,
> saba dhana vipre dila dāna
> yata nartaka, gāyana, bhāṭa, akiñcana jana,
> dhana diyā kaila sabāra māna

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:y:yautuka]]---presentation; [[synonyms:p:pāila]]---received; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---as much as; [[synonyms:g:ghare]]---in the house; [[synonyms:v:vā]]---or; [[synonyms:ā:āchila]]---there was; [[synonyms:k:kata]]---whatever; [[synonyms:s:sabadhana]]---all riches; [[synonyms:v:vipre]]---unto the //brāhmaṇas//; [[synonyms:d:dila]]---gave; [[synonyms:d:dāna]]---in charity; [[synonyms:y:yata]]---all; [[synonyms:n:nartaka]]---dancers; [[synonyms:g:gāyana]]---singers; [[synonyms:b:bhāṭa]]---blessers; [[synonyms:a:akiñcana]] [[synonyms:j:jana]]---poor men; [[synonyms:d:dhana]] [[synonyms:d:diyā]]---giving them riches; [[synonyms:k:kaila]]---did; [[synonyms:s:sabāra]]---everyone's; [[synonyms:m:māna]]---honor.

===== Translation =====

**Whatever riches Jagannātha Miśra collected in the form of gifts and presentations, and whatever he had in his house, he distributed among the brāhmaṇas, professional singers, dancers, bhāṭas and the poor. He honored them all by giving them riches in charity.**
