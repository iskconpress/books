====== CC Ādi 13.98 ======

===== Text =====

> nadīyā-udayagiri, pūrṇacandra gaurahari,
> kṛpā kari' ha-ila udaya
> pāpa-tamaḥ haila nāśa, tri-jagatera ullāsa,
> jagabhari' hari-dhvani haya

===== Synonyms =====

[[synonyms:n:nadīyā]]---the place known as Nadīyā; [[synonyms:u:udayagiri]]---is the appearing place; [[synonyms:p:pūrṇa]]-[[synonyms:c:candra]]---the full moon; [[synonyms:g:gaurahari]]---Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; [[synonyms:k:kṛpā]]---by mercy; [[synonyms:k:kari']]---doing so; [[synonyms:h:ha]]-[[synonyms:i:ila]]---became; [[synonyms:u:udaya]]---risen; [[synonyms:p:pāpa]]---sinful; [[synonyms:t:tamaḥ]]---darkness; [[synonyms:h:haila]]---became; [[synonyms:n:nāśa]]---dissipated; [[synonyms:t:tri]]-[[synonyms:j:jagatera]]---of the three worlds; [[synonyms:u:ullāsa]]---happiness; [[synonyms:j:jaga]]-[[synonyms:b:bhari']]---filling the whole world; [[synonyms:h:hari]]-[[synonyms:d:dhvani]]---the transcendental vibration of Hari; [[synonyms:h:haya]]---resounded.

===== Translation =====

**Thus by His causeless mercy the full moon, Gaurahari, rose in the district of Nadia, which is compared to Udayagiri, where the sun first becomes visible. His rising in the sky dissipated the darkness of sinful life, and thus the three worlds became joyful and chanted the holy name of the Lord.**
